Guiyang Hostels with the Best Communal Kitchens

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Nestled in the heart of China’s southwestern Guizhou Province, Guiyang is a city that effortlessly blends urban charm with natural beauty. For backpackers and budget travelers, hostels are the go-to accommodation, offering affordability and a chance to connect with fellow wanderers. But what truly sets some hostels apart are their communal kitchens—spaces where travelers can whip up meals, share recipes, and bond over food. If you’re planning a trip to Guiyang and love the idea of cooking while traveling, here are the hostels with the best communal kitchens.

Why Communal Kitchens Matter for Travelers

For many travelers, especially long-term backpackers, eating out every meal can quickly drain the budget. A well-equipped communal kitchen is a game-changer, allowing you to:
- Save money by preparing your own meals.
- Experience local markets by shopping for fresh ingredients.
- Share cultural exchanges through cooking with other travelers.
- Accommodate dietary restrictions without hassle.

Guiyang’s hostels understand this, and several have invested in top-notch kitchen facilities that make cooking a joy rather than a chore.

Top Guiyang Hostels with Outstanding Communal Kitchens

1. Wanderlust Inn Guiyang

Kitchen Features:

  • Spacious, well-lit cooking area with multiple stovetops.
  • High-quality cookware and utensils (no more struggling with dull knives!).
  • A communal fridge stocked with free condiments and spices.
  • A cozy dining area where guests often share meals.

Why Backpackers Love It:

Wanderlust Inn’s kitchen is the heart of the hostel. The staff frequently organizes potluck dinners, encouraging guests to showcase dishes from their home countries. Located near a bustling local market, it’s easy to grab fresh veggies, Guizhou’s famous chili peppers, or even some la rou (cured pork) to experiment with.

2. Green Forest Hostel

Kitchen Features:

  • Eco-friendly setup with compost bins and recycling stations.
  • Herb garden where guests can pick fresh basil, mint, or cilantro.
  • A large wooden table perfect for group cooking sessions.

Why Backpackers Love It:

Green Forest takes sustainability seriously, and their kitchen reflects that. The herb garden is a unique touch, and the hostel often hosts “zero-waste cooking” workshops. Plus, it’s just a short walk from Guiyang’s Qingyan Ancient Town, where you can find incredible local snacks to inspire your own creations.

3. Mountain View Hostel

Kitchen Features:

  • Panoramic windows with stunning views of the surrounding hills.
  • A breakfast bar with complimentary rice and noodles.
  • A “recipe exchange board” where travelers leave their favorite dishes.

Why Backpackers Love It:

The view alone makes cooking here a delight. The hostel’s location near Qianling Mountain means you can hike in the morning and return to cook a hearty meal. The recipe board is a goldmine for discovering new flavors—don’t be surprised if you leave with a newfound love for sour fish soup, a Guizhou specialty.

4. Nomad’s Nest Guiyang

Kitchen Features:

  • Industrial-style kitchen with stainless steel countertops.
  • A dedicated baking station (rare in hostels!).
  • Free tea and coffee available 24/7.

Why Backpackers Love It:

Nomad’s Nest attracts a creative crowd, and the baking station is a huge hit. Whether you’re making cookies or attempting sticky rice cakes, this kitchen has you covered. The hostel also runs occasional “bake-offs,” where guests compete for the title of best dessert.

Tips for Cooking in Guiyang Hostels

Explore Local Markets

Guiyang’s markets are a feast for the senses. Head to Hongfu Temple Market for fresh produce, or Nanjing Road Night Market for street food inspiration. Don’t shy away from trying unfamiliar ingredients—ask vendors for cooking tips!

Master a Guizhou Dish

Why not learn to cook a local favorite? Siwawa (a fresh spring roll with spicy dipping sauce) or Changwang Noodles are great starters. Many hostels have staff or fellow travelers who can guide you.

Join a Cooking Class

Some hostels partner with local chefs to offer classes. It’s a fantastic way to dive deeper into Guizhou cuisine and meet other food-loving travelers.

Respect Kitchen Etiquette

  • Clean up after yourself.
  • Label your food in the fridge.
  • Offer to share if you’ve made extra.

Final Thoughts

Guiyang’s hostel scene is thriving, and the best communal kitchens turn a simple stay into a memorable culinary adventure. Whether you’re frying up la zi ji (spicy chicken) or baking cookies for new friends, these kitchens are more than just a place to cook—they’re where travel stories are made. So pack your apron and get ready to chop, stir, and savor your way through Guiyang!
