Guiyang’s Autumn Foliage: Where to Take Stunning Photos

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Forget New England for a moment. There’s a lesser-known, utterly magical autumn destination where the foliage burns with a unique, subtropical brilliance: Guiyang. Nestled in the heart of China’s Guizhou province, this city transforms from a summer retreat into a canvas of fire-kissed reds, radiant yellows, and deep amber from mid-October through late November. The cool, crisp air, often laced with a gentle mist, creates a photographer’s dream—soft, diffused light that makes every color pop. This isn't just about leaves changing; it's about capturing the soul of a karst landscape dressed in its finest, most fleeting gown. Here is your definitive guide to finding and photographing Guiyang’s stunning autumn foliage.

Beyond the City: The Crown Jewel of Qianling Park

No autumn photo tour of Guiyang is complete without a pilgrimage to Qianling Park. This urban oasis becomes the epicenter of fall’s spectacle.

The Maple Path and Hongfu Temple

The main path winding up towards Hongfu Temple is lined with ancient maple trees. Their canopy creates a tunnel of blazing red. The key here is timing. Arrive at opening time to capture the first rays of sun piercing through the leaves, with the mist rising from the forest floor. Use a wide aperture (like f/2.8 or f/4) to isolate details—a single dew-covered scarlet leaf, the mossy steps carpeted in gold, or the elegant temple eaves framed by fiery branches. The contrast between the vermilion leaves and the temple’s dark green tiles and wooden structures is a classic composition.

The Macaque Element

Don’t overlook the park’s famous residents—the troops of Tibetan macaques. A portrait of a monkey sitting contemplatively on a branch surrounded by golden ginkgo leaves is a unique and storytelling shot. Use a longer lens (70-200mm) for safety and compression. Remember, they are wild; observe from a distance and never provoke them for a photo.

The Urban Tapestry: Ginkgo Biloba Brilliance

While maples provide the fire, Ginkgo biloba trees provide the gold. Guiyang’s streets and squares are adorned with these living fossils, creating stunning urban autumn scenes.

Huaxi Park’s Golden Avenue

Head to Huaxi Park, where rows of towering ginkgo trees create a breathtaking golden corridor. On a sunny day, the entire ground shimmers like a carpet of gold coins. For a stunning shot, lie down for a low-angle perspective looking up the avenue, making the trees appear to converge in a vortex of gold. Alternatively, capture the joy of locals strolling, flying kites, or children playing in the leaves. This adds life and scale to your images. The best light is during the golden hour, just after sunrise or before sunset, when the low sun backlights the leaves, making them glow.

City Contrasts

Explore the older parts of the city, like near the Nanzhong River. Look for a solitary bright yellow ginkgo tree standing next to a traditional grey-tiled house or a modern glass building. This juxtaposition of ancient nature and human habitation is powerfully evocative. Use the rule of thirds, placing the tree off-center for a balanced composition.

Day Trip Wonders: Capturing the Karst Landscape in Fall

The true magic of Guiyang’s autumn unfolds when you venture into the surrounding karst countryside. The rolling hills, often dotted with Miao and Buyi villages, take on a patchwork quilt of color.

Qingyan Ancient Town

This 600-year-old town framed by fortress walls offers a historical backdrop for fall photos. Climb the ancient town walls and use a telephoto lens to compress the view of russet and orange trees against the slate-grey rooftops and distant limestone hills. Capture details: a basket of harvested persimmons by a stone doorway, or a local artisan’s shop window framed by a spray of red leaves. The mix of cultural heritage and natural beauty is irresistible.

The Highways and Byways

Renting a car or hiring a driver for a day is perhaps the best photographic investment. The drive towards Huangguoshu Waterfall or into the villages of Kaihua offers unplanned, breathtaking vistas. Pull over safely when you see a terraced field, now with harvested golden rice stalks, set against a backdrop of forested karst peaks tinged with autumn hues. The ever-present mist creates layers of depth—foreground detail, mid-tone hills, and faint background peaks—perfect for creating moody, atmospheric landscape shots. A polarizing filter will help cut through the haze and deepen the colors of the foliage.

Photographer’s Toolkit: Gear and Techniques for Perfect Foliage Shots

Essential Gear

While a smartphone can capture the beauty, a DSLR or mirrorless camera offers control. Bring a versatile zoom lens (24-70mm) for landscapes and a fast prime lens (50mm f/1.8) for details and portraits. A sturdy travel tripod is crucial for low-light scenes in misty forests and for achieving sharp focus in waterfall areas. Don’t forget extra batteries and memory cards—you’ll need them.

Mastering Light and Composition

Guiyang’s autumn light is often soft, but seek out backlighting to make leaves translucent and radiant. After a rain is a prime time; water droplets on leaves act as natural jewels. Look for reflections in the city’s rivers and lakes—a double dose of color. For composition, use natural frames like archways or tree branches. Include a human element, like a friend in a solid-colored coat (cream, navy, or maroon work well), to provide a focal point amidst the chaos of color.

Drone Perspectives (Where Permitted)

In permitted areas outside the city center, a drone can reveal the breathtaking scale of the autumn transformation. An aerial shot of a winding road cutting through a sea of red and gold forest, or a bird’s-eye view of a village nestled in a colorful valley, are perspectives that define a portfolio. Always check local regulations and fly responsibly.

Embracing the Season: More Than Just Photos

To truly capture the essence of Guiyang’s autumn, engage all your senses. Visit a local tea house and order a cup of Guizhou green tea while looking out at a ginkgo tree. The warmth of the cup in your hands, the bitter-steamed siwei (a local herb) in the air, and the visual feast outside complete the experience. Sample seasonal foods like roasted sweet potatoes and persimmons from street vendors—their warm, earthy flavors are the taste of autumn. These moments of immersion will inform and enrich your photography, moving it beyond postcard shots into the realm of storytelling.

The autumn foliage in Guiyang is a transient masterpiece. It’s a dance of light, color, and culture unique to this corner of the world. With its accessible parks, dramatic karst landscapes, and charming urban scenes, it offers a diverse and profoundly beautiful stage for any photographer. So pack your camera, your sense of adventure, and prepare to capture the golden, fiery heart of Southwest China. The mountains are calling, and they are dressed in red and gold.
