Guiyang Temple Hopping Itinerary: Sacred Sites & Serenity

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Guiyang, the capital of Guizhou Province, is a city where modernity meets tradition. Nestled among lush mountains and vibrant urban life, it’s home to some of China’s most serene and spiritually significant temples. This temple-hopping itinerary will guide you through sacred sites that offer tranquility, cultural insights, and breathtaking architecture—perfect for travelers seeking both adventure and inner peace.

Day 1: Exploring the Heart of Guiyang’s Spirituality

Hongfu Temple (Hongfusi)

Start your journey at Hongfu Temple, one of Guiyang’s most revered Buddhist sites. Founded during the Qing Dynasty, this temple is a masterpiece of traditional Chinese architecture, with intricate carvings and sweeping rooftops. The temple complex includes multiple halls, each dedicated to different Buddhist deities. Don’t miss the Mahavira Hall, where the golden statue of Sakyamuni Buddha radiates a sense of calm.

Pro Tip: Visit early in the morning to witness monks chanting during their daily rituals. The temple’s hilltop location also offers panoramic views of Guiyang’s skyline.

Jiaxiu Pavilion

Though not a temple, Jiaxiu Pavilion is a must-visit for its historical and cultural significance. This iconic Ming Dynasty structure sits elegantly atop a stone bridge over the Nanming River. Inside, you’ll find calligraphy and paintings that reflect Guizhou’s artistic heritage. The pavilion is especially magical at sunset, when the golden light reflects off the river.

Day 2: Venturing Beyond the City Center

Qianling Mountain Park & Hongfu Temple’s Hidden Gems

Spend your second day at Qianling Mountain Park, a sprawling green oasis just west of downtown Guiyang. The park is home to a smaller but equally enchanting branch of Hongfu Temple, as well as the Kaiyuan Temple. Hike up the mountain trails, where you’ll encounter wild monkeys—friendly but mischievous!

Kaiyuan Temple is quieter than its counterpart, making it ideal for meditation. The temple’s Sutra Library houses ancient Buddhist texts, and the surrounding gardens are perfect for a peaceful stroll.

Wenchang Pavilion

End your day at Wenchang Pavilion, a lesser-known gem dedicated to the Taoist god of literature, Wenchang Dijun. Students and scholars often visit to pray for success in exams. The pavilion’s design is a blend of Taoist and local Miao architectural styles, offering a unique cultural fusion.

Day 3: A Day Trip to Qingyan Ancient Town

Qingyan’s Temples and Time Capsule Charm

Just an hour from Guiyang, Qingyan Ancient Town is a well-preserved Ming Dynasty village with several temples worth exploring. Start at Ciyun Temple, a serene spot with a stunning courtyard and ancient stone carvings. Next, visit Zhenshan Temple, which blends Buddhist and Taoist elements.

Foodie Alert: Qingyan is famous for its local snacks. Try the "Qingyan Tofu" (a spicy, fermented delicacy) and "Rose Sugar Ice Powder" for a refreshing treat.

Tianhe Pool

Before heading back to Guiyang, stop by Tianhe Pool, a sacred spring believed to have healing properties. The nearby Tianhe Temple is small but deeply spiritual, with locals often leaving offerings for good health.

Travel Tips for Temple Hopping in Guiyang

Dress Respectfully

Temples are active places of worship. Wear modest clothing (avoid shorts and sleeveless tops) and remove your hat when entering halls.

Best Times to Visit

Spring (March-May) and autumn (September-November) offer pleasant weather. Avoid national holidays when temples can get crowded.

Getting Around

Guiyang’s metro and buses are efficient, but taxis or ride-hailing apps are more convenient for temple hopping.

Local Etiquette

  • Always walk clockwise around stupas or statues.
  • Ask before taking photos of monks or worshippers.
  • Donate a small amount if you light incense (usually ¥5-10).

Hidden Temples & Off-the-Beaten-Path Gems

Dongshan Temple

Located in the eastern suburbs, Dongshan Temple is a quiet retreat with a stunning view of the city. The temple’s Thousand Buddha Wall is a mesmerizing sight.

Longquan Temple

For a truly offbeat experience, visit Longquan Temple, nestled in a forested area. The temple’s natural springs and secluded vibe make it a favorite among locals seeking solitude.

Guiyang’s temples are more than just tourist stops—they’re gateways to understanding the region’s spiritual and cultural soul. Whether you’re a history buff, a photography enthusiast, or simply in need of serenity, this itinerary promises a journey of discovery and peace.
