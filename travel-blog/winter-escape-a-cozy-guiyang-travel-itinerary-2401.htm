Winter Escape: A Cozy Guiyang Travel Itinerary

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

When winter arrives, many travelers seek destinations that offer warmth, charm, and unique cultural experiences. Guiyang, the capital of Guizhou Province, is an underrated gem that perfectly fits the bill. With its mild winter climate, vibrant food scene, and stunning natural landscapes, Guiyang is an ideal destination for a cozy winter getaway. Here’s a carefully crafted itinerary to help you make the most of your trip.

Day 1: Arrival and Exploring the City

Morning: Settle into Your Cozy Accommodation

Start your winter escape by checking into a boutique hotel or a traditional guesthouse in Guiyang’s city center. Options like the Guiyang Kempinski Hotel or a charming Miao-style inn offer warmth and comfort, perfect for the season.

Afternoon: Stroll Through Qianling Park

Head to Qianling Park, a serene oasis in the heart of the city. Winter brings a quiet beauty to the park, with misty lakes and lush greenery. Don’t miss the Hongfu Temple, a tranquil Buddhist temple nestled on the hillside.

Evening: Indulge in Guiyang’s Street Food

Guiyang is famous for its spicy and flavorful street food. Visit Jiaxiu Lou Night Market to try local specialties like:
- Siwawa (a fresh spring roll with spicy dipping sauce)
- Changwang Noodles (a hearty, spicy noodle soup)
- Grilled Fish with Fermented Chili (a must-try winter warmer)

Day 2: Cultural Immersion and Hot Springs

Morning: Visit Qingyan Ancient Town

Take a short trip to Qingyan Ancient Town, a well-preserved Ming Dynasty town. Wander through its cobblestone streets, admire the ancient architecture, and sample local snacks like rice tofu and rose sugar candy.

Afternoon: Relax at Xifeng Hot Springs

After exploring, unwind at Xifeng Hot Springs, just an hour’s drive from Guiyang. Soak in the mineral-rich waters surrounded by misty mountains—a perfect winter activity.

Evening: Dinner at a Miao Village

Experience the hospitality of the Miao ethnic group with a traditional dinner in a nearby village. Enjoy sour fish soup, sticky rice cakes, and lively folk performances.

Day 3: Nature and Adventure

Morning: Hike in Huaxi National Wetland Park

For nature lovers, Huaxi National Wetland Park offers scenic trails and tranquil lakes. Winter mornings here are crisp and refreshing, with fewer crowds.

Afternoon: Explore Tianhetan Scenic Area

Venture to Tianhetan, a breathtaking karst landscape with waterfalls and caves. The winter light creates a magical glow over the limestone formations.

Evening: Cozy Tea Time in a Hidden Café

End your day at a tucked-away teahouse like Yangming’s Tea House, where you can sip locally grown Guiyang Maojian tea by a warm fireplace.

Day 4: Day Trip to Huangguoshu Waterfall

Full-Day Excursion to Huangguoshu

No trip to Guiyang is complete without visiting Huangguoshu Waterfall, one of Asia’s largest waterfalls. Winter brings thinner crowds and a mystical atmosphere as the mist rises from the cascading water.

Optional: Visit Dragon Palace Cave

If time allows, stop by the Dragon Palace Cave, a stunning underground karst cave system with illuminated rock formations.

Day 5: Souvenir Hunting and Departure

Morning: Shop at Guiyang’s Local Markets

Pick up unique souvenirs at Nanjiao Market or Zhenghong Shopping Street. Look for Miao silver jewelry, batik textiles, or local chili sauces to bring a taste of Guiyang home.

Afternoon: Farewell Feast

Before heading to the airport, enjoy a final meal at Lao Kaili Sour Soup Fish, a beloved local restaurant known for its tangy and spicy hot pot.

With its blend of culture, nature, and culinary delights, Guiyang is the perfect winter escape for those seeking warmth and adventure off the beaten path.
