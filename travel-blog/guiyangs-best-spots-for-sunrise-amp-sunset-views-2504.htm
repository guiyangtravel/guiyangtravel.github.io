Guiyang’s Best Spots for Sunrise & Sunset Views

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Guiyang, the capital of Guizhou Province, is a city where modernity meets nature in the most breathtaking ways. Known for its lush greenery, karst landscapes, and vibrant culture, Guiyang also offers some of the most stunning sunrise and sunset views in China. Whether you’re an early riser chasing the first light of day or a romantic seeking golden-hour magic, here are the top spots to witness Guiyang’s celestial spectacles.

Mount Qianling: The Classic Sunrise Haven

Why It’s a Must-Visit

Mount Qianling (Qianling Shan) is Guiyang’s most famous urban mountain park, and for good reason. Its elevated vantage points provide unobstructed views of the city waking up under a soft, golden glow. The mix of misty valleys and distant cityscapes creates a surreal backdrop for sunrise chasers.

Best Viewing Spots

  • Hongfu Temple Area: Climb the steps to this ancient Buddhist temple, where the first rays of sunlight pierce through the surrounding forest.
  • Qianling Lake: The reflection of the sunrise on the lake’s surface doubles the beauty.
  • Observation Deck at the Summit: A 360-degree panorama of Guiyang bathed in morning light.

Pro Tips

  • Arrive at least 30 minutes before sunrise to secure a good spot.
  • Wear comfortable shoes—the hike up can be steep in parts.
  • Combine your visit with a post-sunrise stroll through the park’s monkey-filled trails.

Huaxi National Urban Wetland Park: Sunset by the Water

Why It’s a Must-Visit

Huaxi Park is a serene escape from the city’s hustle, with sprawling wetlands, meandering rivers, and traditional stone bridges. As the sun dips below the horizon, the water mirrors the sky’s fiery hues, creating a painterly scene.

Best Viewing Spots

  • Ten-Mile Riverbank: A long, scenic walkway perfect for sunset gazing.
  • Moon Island: A small islet that offers uninterrupted views of the sun setting over the water.
  • Bamboo Raft Rides: For a unique perspective, float along the river as the sky changes colors.

Pro Tips

  • Visit in autumn for cooler weather and clearer skies.
  • Bring a picnic—sunset views taste better with local snacks like Siwawa (a Guizhou-style spring roll).
  • Stay for the park’s evening light displays if visiting on weekends.

Jiaxiu Pavilion: Sunset Over Historic Elegance

Why It’s a Must-Visit

Jiaxiu Pavilion, a Ming Dynasty landmark, stands proudly on the Nanming River. At sunset, the pavilion’s traditional architecture is silhouetted against a gradient sky, making it a photographer’s dream.

Best Viewing Spots

  • Fuyu Bridge: Capture the pavilion with the sun setting behind it.
  • Riverside Promenade: Walk along the water’s edge for dynamic angles.
  • Pavilion’s Upper Floors: Climb up for a bird’s-eye view of the river aflame with sunset colors.

Pro Tips

  • Visit on weekdays to avoid crowds.
  • Pair your sunset visit with a night tour—the pavilion is beautifully lit after dark.
  • Try nearby Changwang Noodles for a post-sunset meal.

Qingyan Ancient Town: A Twilight Fairytale

Why It’s a Must-Visit

Qingyan, a well-preserved 600-year-old town, transforms at sunset. The ancient stone streets and moss-covered walls take on a warm, nostalgic glow, blending history with natural beauty.

Best Viewing Spots

  • Town Walls: Walk along the ramparts for elevated sunset views over the rooftops.
  • Back Alleys: Less crowded, these narrow pathways offer intimate golden-hour moments.
  • Dingguang Gate: One of the town’s iconic gates, perfect for dramatic sunset shots.

Pro Tips

  • Arrive early to explore the town before sunset.
  • Sample Qingyan Tofu from street vendors as you wander.
  • Stay overnight—the town’s charm lingers after the tourists leave.

Baihua Lake: Sunrise in Solitude

Why It’s a Must-Visit

For those who prefer quieter sunrise spots, Baihua Lake delivers. Surrounded by rolling hills and forests, the lake’s tranquil waters reflect the dawn sky in near-perfect stillness.

Best Viewing Spots

  • Lakeside Pier: Minimal obstructions for a clear sunrise view.
  • Nearby Hilltops: Short hikes lead to higher vantage points.
  • Floating Walkways: Get closer to the water for mirror-like reflections.

Pro Tips

  • Visit in spring or summer for lush greenery.
  • Bring a thermos of tea—mornings can be chilly.
  • Keep an eye out for migratory birds at dawn.

Wenchang Pavilion: Sunset with a City View

Why It’s a Must-Visit

Perched on a hill in downtown Guiyang, Wenchang Pavilion offers a unique blend of urban and natural sunset views. Watch as the city transitions from day to night, with skyscrapers catching the last light.

Best Viewing Spots

  • Pavilion’s Terrace: The highest point for sweeping city vistas.
  • Adjacent Parks: Less crowded alternatives with equally good views.
  • Nearby Cafés: Some rooftop spots let you enjoy the sunset with a drink in hand.

Pro Tips

  • Time your visit with clear weather for the best visibility.
  • Explore the surrounding Zhonghua Road for dinner options afterward.
  • Visit during weekdays—the pavilion gets busy on weekends.

Final Thoughts on Timing & Photography

Best Seasons for Sunrise/Sunset

  • Spring (March-May): Mild weather, occasional mist adding drama.
  • Autumn (September-November): Crisp air, vibrant skies.

Photography Gear Recommendations

  • Wide-angle lens for landscapes.
  • Tripod for long exposures.
  • Polarizing filter to enhance colors.

Local Insights

  • Ask taxi drivers for hidden spots—they often know lesser-known viewpoints.
  • Check weather apps for cloud coverage predictions.
  • Sunrise times vary significantly by season—plan accordingly.

Guiyang’s sunrises and sunsets are more than just daily occurrences—they’re moments where the city’s natural and cultural beauty shines brightest. Whether you’re atop a mountain, by a lake, or in an ancient town, each spot offers a unique way to experience the magic of golden hour.
