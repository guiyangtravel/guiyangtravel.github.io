Guiyang Travel Itinerary: Best Photo Spots

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Guiyang, the capital of Guizhou Province, is a hidden gem in Southwest China. Known for its lush landscapes, vibrant ethnic culture, and unique urban charm, this city offers countless opportunities for breathtaking photography. Whether you're an amateur shutterbug or a professional photographer, Guiyang’s mix of natural beauty and cultural richness will leave you inspired. Here’s a curated list of the best photo spots in Guiyang to capture unforgettable moments.

1. Qianling Mountain Park (黔灵山公园)

Why It’s a Must-Visit for Photos

Qianling Mountain Park is one of Guiyang’s most iconic destinations. The park combines serene lakes, dense forests, and playful macaque monkeys, making it a paradise for nature photography.

Best Spots Within the Park

  • Hongfu Temple (弘福寺): This ancient Buddhist temple nestled in the mountains offers a mystical atmosphere, especially in the early morning mist.
  • Qianling Lake: The reflections of the surrounding greenery on the lake create a mirror-like effect, perfect for symmetrical shots.
  • Monkey Zone: Capture candid moments of the park’s resident macaques—just keep your snacks hidden!

Best Time to Visit

Early morning (6–8 AM) for soft lighting and fewer crowds.

2. Jiaxiu Pavilion (甲秀楼)

A Symbol of Guiyang’s Heritage

Jiaxiu Pavilion, a Ming Dynasty-era landmark, sits elegantly on the Nanming River. Its traditional Chinese architecture and illuminated night views make it a favorite among photographers.

Photography Tips

  • Golden Hour Shots: Visit during sunset to capture the pavilion glowing against the sky.
  • Night Photography: The pavilion lights up beautifully after dark, creating a magical scene reflected on the river.
  • Long Exposure: Use a tripod for smooth water effects in long-exposure shots.

3. Qingyan Ancient Town (青岩古镇)

Step Back in Time

This 600-year-old Ming Dynasty town is a maze of stone streets, ancient walls, and traditional shops. Its rustic charm makes it ideal for cultural and street photography.

Top Photo Opportunities

  • City Walls: Climb the ancient walls for panoramic views of the town’s tiled rooftops.
  • Stone Alleyways: The narrow, winding streets lined with old wooden houses are perfect for moody, atmospheric shots.
  • Local Cuisine Stalls: Capture vibrant food scenes, especially the famous Qingyan braised pork (卤猪脚).

Best Time to Visit

Weekday mornings to avoid tourist crowds.

4. Huaxi Wetland Park (花溪湿地公园)

A Natural Escape

This expansive park features rivers, wetlands, and flower fields, offering endless possibilities for landscape photography.

Key Photography Spots

  • Tulip Fields (Spring): If visiting in spring, the blooming tulips create a colorful, Instagram-worthy backdrop.
  • Bamboo Forests: The tall bamboo groves provide a tranquil setting for minimalist compositions.
  • Lotus Ponds (Summer): The ponds burst with pink and white lotus flowers in summer.

Best Time for Photos

Sunrise or late afternoon for soft, golden light.

5. Guiyang’s Urban Skyline at Night

Modern Meets Traditional

Guiyang’s skyline has transformed with futuristic skyscrapers, yet retains pockets of traditional charm. The contrast makes for striking urban photography.

Best Viewpoints

  • Guanshan Lake (观山湖): The lake’s reflections of the city lights create a dazzling effect.
  • CCB Tower Observation Deck: For a bird’s-eye view of the city.
  • Nanming River Bridges: The illuminated bridges add dynamic lines to night shots.

Gear Tip

A wide-angle lens is ideal for capturing the full skyline.

6. Ethnic Minority Villages (苗寨 & 侗寨)

Cultural Photography at Its Best

Guiyang is surrounded by Miao and Dong ethnic villages, where traditional clothing, architecture, and festivals offer rich visual storytelling opportunities.

Top Villages to Visit

  • Xijiang Miao Village (西江千户苗寨): The largest Miao settlement, famous for its terraced houses and silver jewelry.
  • Zhaoxing Dong Village (肇兴侗寨): Known for its drum towers and wind-and-rain bridges.

Photography Tips

  • Festivals: Plan around events like the Miao New Year for vibrant costumes and dances.
  • Portrait Permissions: Always ask before photographing locals—some may expect a small tip.

7. Baihua Lake (百花湖)

A Scenic Lakeside Retreat

Located just outside Guiyang, Baihua Lake is dotted with small islands and fishing villages, offering a peaceful setting for landscape photography.

Best Shots to Take

  • Fishing Boats at Dawn: The misty lake with silhouetted boats is pure magic.
  • Island Panoramas: Use a drone (if permitted) for aerial views of the scattered islets.

When to Go

Early morning for the best lighting and calm waters.

8. Guiyang’s Street Food Markets

A Feast for the Lens

Guiyang’s night markets are a sensory overload of sizzling skewers, steaming bowls of sour soup fish (酸汤鱼), and colorful local snacks.

Best Markets for Food Photography

  • Erqi Road Night Market (二七路小吃街): Packed with stalls offering Guizhou delicacies.
  • Zhenning Road Food Street: A mix of traditional and modern street eats.

Pro Tip

Use a fast lens to capture the action in low-light conditions.

Final Photography Gear Recommendations

  • Wide-Angle Lens (16–35mm): For landscapes and architecture.
  • Prime Lens (50mm f/1.8): Great for portraits and street scenes.
  • Tripod: Essential for night shots and long exposures.
  • Polarizing Filter: Helps reduce glare on water and enhance skies.

Guiyang is a photographer’s dream, blending natural beauty, cultural depth, and urban energy. Whether you’re chasing golden-hour landscapes or vibrant street scenes, this city has it all. Pack your camera, explore these spots, and let Guiyang’s charm unfold through your lens.
