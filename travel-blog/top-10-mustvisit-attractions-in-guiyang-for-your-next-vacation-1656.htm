Top 10 Must-Visit Attractions in Guiyang for Your Next Vacation

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Guiyang, the capital of Guizhou Province, is a hidden gem in Southwest China. Known for its lush landscapes, rich ethnic culture, and mouthwatering cuisine, this city offers a unique blend of modernity and tradition. Whether you're an adventure seeker, a foodie, or a culture enthusiast, Guiyang has something for everyone. Here are the top 10 must-visit attractions to add to your itinerary.

1. Qianling Mountain Park

A Green Oasis in the City

Qianling Mountain Park is a sprawling urban park that offers a refreshing escape from the hustle and bustle of the city. The park is home to hundreds of wild macaques, making it a fun spot for animal lovers.

Highlights:

  • Hongfu Temple: A serene Buddhist temple nestled in the mountains.
  • Cable Car Ride: Enjoy panoramic views of Guiyang from above.
  • Hiking Trails: Perfect for nature lovers and fitness enthusiasts.

2. Jiaxiu Pavilion

The Iconic Landmark of Guiyang

Jiaxiu Pavilion, or "First Scholar’s Pavilion," is a historic tower built during the Ming Dynasty. It stands elegantly on the Nanming River and is especially stunning when lit up at night.

Why Visit?

  • Night Views: The illuminated pavilion reflects beautifully on the river.
  • Cultural Significance: Learn about its role in ancient imperial examinations.
  • Nearby Food Streets: Sample local snacks like Siwawa (a spicy spring roll) and Changwang Noodles.

3. Qingyan Ancient Town

Step Back in Time

Located just 30 km from Guiyang, Qingyan Ancient Town is a well-preserved Ming-era town with cobblestone streets and ancient architecture.

Must-Do Activities:

  • Taste Qingyan Tofu: A local specialty with a unique texture.
  • Explore Temples and Shrines: Discover Taoist and Buddhist influences.
  • Shop for Handicrafts: Pick up souvenirs like wax-dyed fabrics.

4. Huaxi National Urban Wetland Park

A Scenic Retreat

This expansive wetland park is ideal for boating, cycling, and picnicking. The crystal-clear lakes and rolling hills create a picturesque setting.

Top Experiences:

  • Boat Ride on Huaxi Lake: Glide past willow trees and karst formations.
  • Cherry Blossom Season: Visit in spring for breathtaking blooms.
  • Local Minority Performances: Catch Miao and Dong cultural shows.

5. Tianhetan Scenic Area

Nature’s Masterpiece

Tianhetan is famous for its stunning waterfall and karst caves. The area is a paradise for photographers and hikers.

Don’t Miss:

  • Tianhetan Waterfall: A majestic 20-meter cascade.
  • Cave Explorations: Marvel at stalactites and underground rivers.
  • Zip-lining Adventures: For thrill-seekers.

6. Guiyang Polar Ocean World

Fun for the Whole Family

This aquarium features polar animals like penguins and beluga whales, along with interactive exhibits.

Highlights:

  • Underwater Tunnel: Walk surrounded by sharks and rays.
  • Animal Shows: Dolphin and sea lion performances.
  • Educational Programs: Great for kids.

7. Guiyang Confucius Academy

A Cultural Haven

This academy is dedicated to Confucian studies and traditional Chinese culture. The architecture is a blend of ancient and modern styles.

What to Do:

  • Attend a Lecture: On Confucianism or calligraphy.
  • Stroll the Gardens: Peaceful and photogenic.
  • Tea Ceremony Experience: Learn about Chinese tea culture.

8. Nanjiang Grand Canyon

Adventure Awaits

For adrenaline junkies, this canyon offers rafting, cliff jumping, and breathtaking scenery.

Thrilling Activities:

  • White-Water Rafting: Navigate through rapids.
  • Suspension Bridges: Test your courage high above the river.
  • Camping Under the Stars: Perfect for outdoor lovers.

9. Guiyang Ethnic Village

Immerse in Minority Cultures

This open-air museum showcases the traditions of Guizhou’s ethnic groups, including the Miao, Dong, and Buyi people.

Experiences:

  • Traditional Dance Performances: Colorful and energetic.
  • Handicraft Workshops: Try your hand at embroidery or silver jewelry-making.
  • Ethnic Cuisine: Savor dishes like Sour Fish Soup and Sticky Rice Cake.

10. Guiyang Night Markets

A Food Lover’s Paradise

No trip to Guiyang is complete without exploring its vibrant night markets. From spicy street food to exotic snacks, the flavors are unforgettable.

Must-Try Foods:

  • Grilled Fish with Fermented Chili: A local favorite.
  • Zhusun Gao (Bamboo Rice): Sticky rice cooked in bamboo tubes.
  • Mala Tang: DIY spicy hot pot skewers.

Each of these attractions offers a unique glimpse into Guiyang’s charm. Whether you're soaking in natural beauty, diving into history, or indulging in culinary delights, your vacation here will be nothing short of extraordinary.
