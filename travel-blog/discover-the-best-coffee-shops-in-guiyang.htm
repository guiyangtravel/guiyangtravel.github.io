Discover the Best Coffee Shops in Guiyang

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Nestled among the lush, rolling hills of Guizhou Province, Guiyang is a city that often surprises travelers with its vibrant culture, stunning natural scenery, and rapidly evolving urban charm. While it’s famous for its spicy cuisine, minority cultures, and cool climate, there’s another scene brewing quietly beneath the surface—a thriving coffee culture that blends traditional Chinese elements with modern craft. For travelers seeking a cozy retreat after exploring Huangguoshu Waterfall or wandering through Qingyan Ancient Town, Guiyang’s coffee shops offer not just a caffeine fix, but a window into the city’s soul.

Why Coffee Culture is Blooming in Guiyang

Guiyang might not be the first city that comes to mind when you think of specialty coffee in China, but that’s exactly what makes it so special. Over the past decade, a wave of young entrepreneurs and coffee enthusiasts has transformed the city into a hidden gem for coffee lovers. The rise of coffee culture here is intertwined with Guiyang’s identity as a growing tech hub and a popular destination for domestic tourism. With an influx of visitors seeking unique experiences, coffee shops have become spaces where tradition meets innovation. Many cafes source locally from Yunnan, China’s premier coffee-growing region, creating a narrative that connects the drink to the land.

Top Coffee Shops to Explore in Guiyang

1. QianCheng Coffee Lab

Tucked away in the bustling Nanming District, QianCheng Coffee Lab is a pioneer in Guiyang’s specialty coffee scene. The minimalist interior, with its concrete walls and wooden accents, appeals to urban explorers and digital nomads. They roast their own beans, focusing on single-origin Yunnan coffees that tell a story of terroir. Try their signature pour-over, which often features notes of tropical fruit and subtle spice—a perfect reflection of Guizhou’s flavor profile. The baristas are passionate educators, happy to explain the brewing process to curious travelers.

2. Old Town Brew

Located near Qingyan Ancient Town, this cafe is a beautiful fusion of history and modernity. Housed in a restored traditional building with stone courtyards and sloping roofs, Old Town Brew offers a tranquil escape. Their menu includes classic espresso drinks alongside innovative options like jiangshui coffee, which incorporates local fermented rice water for a uniquely Guizhou twist. It’s an ideal spot to relax after a morning of exploring ancient streets and sampling local snacks like siwawa and changwangmian.

3. Mountain Cloud Coffee

Perched on the outskirts of the city with views of the surrounding karst landscapes, Mountain Cloud Coffee is all about the experience. The journey to this cafe feels like an adventure—part of why it’s become a hotspot for travel influencers and nature lovers. Their outdoor seating area overlooks terraced fields, making it especially popular during sunrise and sunset. The coffee here is robust and earthy, often paired with homemade pastries infused with local ingredients like honey from nearby villages.

4. Urban Leaf Coffee

In the heart of Guiyang’s shopping district, Urban Leaf offers a green oasis amid the chaos. Known for its plant-filled interior and sustainable practices, this cafe appeals to eco-conscious travelers. They serve organic coffee brewed with precision, along with vegan snacks. The cold brew infused with maotai—a local baijiu—is a daring must-try for those looking to taste Guizhou’s iconic spirit in a new form.

5. Minority Blend

This culturally rich cafe celebrates the diversity of Guizhou’s ethnic minorities. Decorated with handicrafts from the Miao and Dong communities, Minority Blend offers more than just coffee—it’s a cultural experience. Their signature drink, the “Colorful Guizhou Latte,” is artfully crafted with natural dyes from butterfly pea flowers and turmeric, mirroring the vibrant textiles of the region. It’s a great place to learn about local traditions while enjoying a meticulously prepared flat white.

How Coffee Shops Enhance the Guiyang Travel Experience

Coffee shops in Guiyang aren’t just places to drink coffee; they’re social hubs, creative spaces, and gateways to deeper travel experiences. Many cafes host events like live music, poetry readings, and photography exhibitions that highlight local talent. For travelers, these venues provide opportunities to connect with residents and other explorers, sharing stories over a cup of expertly brewed coffee. Additionally, cafes often double as information centers where baristas recommend off-the-beaten-path attractions, such as hidden waterfalls or lesser-known villages.

Beyond Coffee: Local Delights and Pairings

No visit to a Guiyang coffee shop is complete without trying some local snacks. Pair your coffee with traditional treats like liangfen (chilled rice jelly) or nuomici (glutinous rice balls). Some cafes even collaborate with nearby bakeries to offer la rou bao (spicy pork buns)—a perfect blend of local flavor and global coffee culture. For those with a sweet tooth, don’t miss rose petal cakes or honey-glazed nuts, which complement the acidity of light roast coffees.

Seasonal Travel Tips for Coffee Lovers

Guiyang’s climate is mild year-round, but each season offers a unique coffee experience. Spring is ideal for visiting outdoor cafes like Mountain Cloud, when the hills are blanketed in blossoms. Summer, though warm, is perfect for iced coffees enjoyed in air-conditioned spaces like Urban Leaf. Autumn brings cool breezes, making it the best time to explore historic cafes near Qingyan Ancient Town. Winter calls for cozying up with a hot pour-over in QianCheng Coffee Lab, especially after a day trip to the hot springs of Guizhou.

Navigating Guiyang’s Coffee Scene Like a Local

To truly immerse yourself, learn a few phrases like “yī bēi kāfēi” (a cup of coffee) or “zhè shì nǎlǐ de dòu?” (Where are these beans from?). Locals often start their day with a simple americano but enjoy lingering over hand-dripped coffee on weekends. Payment is typically via WeChat or Alipay, though international cards are accepted in some larger cafes. For the best experience, visit during weekdays when shops are less crowded, and strike up a conversation with the staff—they’re usually eager to share their knowledge and recommendations.

The Future of Coffee in Guiyang

As Guiyang continues to grow as a tourist destination, its coffee culture is evolving rapidly. New shops are opening with a focus on sustainability, innovation, and cultural preservation. The upcoming Coffee Festival in 2024 aims to put Guiyang on the global coffee map, featuring barista competitions and local roaster collaborations. For travelers, this means even more reasons to return and rediscover the city’s vibrant caffeine landscape.
