Guiyang to Chishui: A 5-Day Nature Lover’s Itinerary

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

If you’re craving an escape into China’s lesser-known natural wonders, the journey from Guiyang to Chishui is a dream come true. This 5-day itinerary is packed with lush forests, dramatic waterfalls, and serene rivers—perfect for hikers, photographers, and anyone who loves the great outdoors.

Day 1: Arrival in Guiyang & Exploring Qianling Park

Morning: Touchdown in Guiyang

Start your adventure by flying into Guiyang Longdongbao International Airport. The city, known as the "Forest City," is your gateway to Guizhou’s wild beauty. Drop your bags at a centrally located hotel (try the Guiyang Kempinski Hotel for luxury or Loft Youth Hostel for budget stays).

Afternoon: Qianling Park Hike

Head to Qianling Park, a green oasis in the heart of the city. The park is famous for its wild macaques—keep your snacks hidden! Hike up to Hongfu Temple, a serene Buddhist monastery nestled in the hills. The panoramic view of Guiyang from here is worth the climb.

Evening: Dinner at Siwawa Snack Street

For dinner, dive into local flavors at Siwawa Snack Street. Try Changwang Noodles (spicy beef noodles) or Siwawa (a cold rice-paper wrap with crunchy veggies). Don’t miss the Mijiu (sweet fermented rice drink) for a light buzz.

Day 2: The Spectacular Huangguoshu Waterfall

Morning: Road Trip to Huangguoshu

Rent a car or join a tour for the 2-hour drive to Huangguoshu Waterfall, Asia’s tallest waterfall. Stop at Tianxing Bridge Scenic Area en route to marvel at karst caves and stone forests.

Midday: Waterfall Wonders

At Huangguoshu, walk behind the curtain of the Water-Curtain Cave (bring a raincoat!). For the best photo ops, hike to Guanpu Pavilion—the misty rainbow at noon is magical.

Evening: Stay in Anshun

Check into a nearby guesthouse like Huangguoshu Hotel. Wind down with Anshun Tofu and Sour Soup Fish at a local eatery.

Day 3: Into the Wild – Chishui’s Danxia Landscapes

Morning: Scenic Drive to Chishui

Hit the road early for the 4-hour drive to Chishui. The route winds through terraced fields and bamboo forests—stop at Bing’an Ancient Town for a quick tea break.

Afternoon: Chishui Danxia National Park

Explore the Chishui Danxia Landforms, a UNESCO site with red sandstone cliffs and emerald rivers. Hike the Bamboo Sea Trail or paddle a kayak along the Chishui River.

Evening: Homestay in a Miao Village

Book a night at a Miao ethnic homestay. Learn to cook Lusheng Chicken (bamboo-steamed poultry) and join a bonfire dance under the stars.

Day 4: Waterfalls & Vineyards

Morning: Shizhang Cave Waterfall

Visit Shizhang Cave, a tiered waterfall hidden in a jungle. The 2-hour hike rewards you with swimming holes—pack a picnic!

Afternoon: Chishui Wine Tasting

Guizhou is China’s baijiu capital. Tour the Chishui Distillery to sample Maotai-style liquor, or sip local berry wines at a riverside vineyard.

Evening: Night Market Feasting

Stroll Chishui Night Market for grilled river fish, Zhusun Gao (bamboo rice cakes), and fiery La Rou (cured pork).

Day 5: Farewell to Nature

Sunrise at Foguangyan

Hike Foguangyan at dawn for a misty sunrise over the Chishui Valley. It’s a quiet finale to your trip.

Late Morning: Souvenir Hunting

Grab last-minute gifts: handmade bamboo crafts, chili-infused baijiu, or Miao embroidery.

Afternoon: Return to Guiyang

Drive back to Guiyang (or take a direct bus). If time allows, unwind at Nanming River Tea Houses with a cup of Maojian green tea before your flight.

This itinerary blends adrenaline and serenity—proof that Guizhou’s wild side is every nature lover’s paradise. Pack sturdy shoes, a waterproof camera, and an appetite for adventure!
