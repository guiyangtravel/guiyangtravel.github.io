Discover Guiyang: Best Landmarks and Scenic Areas

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Nestled in the rolling karst mountains of southwest China, Guiyang, the capital of Guizhou province, has long been a well-kept secret. For years, travelers blazed past it on their way to the iconic rice terraces of the east or the Himalayan vistas of the west. But today, the secret is out. Guiyang is emerging as a destination in its own right—a city where ancient minority cultures, a stunning natural landscape, a surprisingly vibrant food scene, and a forward-thinking, eco-conscious ethos converge. Forget the humid, oppressive summers of other Chinese metropolises; Guiyang boasts a perpetually pleasant, spring-like climate, earning it the moniker "The Forest City" and "China's Summer Capital." This is your guide to discovering the very best landmarks and scenic areas that make Guiyang an unforgettable stop.

Where Nature Reigns Supreme: The Iconic Landscapes

Guiyang’s skyline isn’t dominated by endless skyscrapers, but by lush, green peaks. The city is woven into the landscape, creating a unique urban-natural harmony.

Qianling Mountain Park: The City's Green Lung

No visit to Guiyang is complete without a morning at Qianling Mountain Park. This isn't just a park; it's a vast, wild ecosystem at the city's heart. The main attraction is the troop of hundreds of wild macaques that call the mountain home. Watching them interact, play, and curiously observe visitors is a delight. Hike up the winding paths through dense forest to Hongfu Temple, one of Guizhou's most significant Buddhist monasteries. The serenity here, with the scent of incense mingling with pine, is a world away from the city buzz. For a panoramic reward, climb to the top of the Fuyun Pavilion and witness Guiyang sprawled out between the karst towers—a breathtaking vista.

Huaxi National Urban Wetland Park: A Poetic Waterscape

South of the city center, Huaxi Park presents a different, more poetic side of Guiyang's nature. Centered around the meandering Huaxi River, this park is a masterpiece of classic Chinese garden design, blended with the region's natural karst formations. Stroll along willow-lined paths, cross elegant stone bridges like the Tianhe Tan, and take a boat ride on the tranquil waters. The park is particularly magical in the early morning when mist clings to the river, or in the evening when the bridges and pagodas are softly lit. It’s a favorite spot for local photographers and couples, offering countless picture-perfect moments.

A Dive into Culture and History

Beyond the mountains and rivers, Guiyang is a cultural crossroads, home to the Han Chinese and over 17 ethnic minority groups, most notably the Miao and the Buyi.

Qingyan Ancient Town: A Step Back in Time

Located in Huaxi District, Qingyan is a beautifully preserved Ming and Qing dynasty town. Its maze-like streets are paved with bluestone, flanked by ancient stone walls, wooden shopfronts, and crumbling temples. Unlike some overly commercialized ancient towns, Qingyan retains an authentic, lived-in feel. This is the place to dive into local snacks: try the legendary Qingyan Tofu (beancurd), spicy and savory; sample the Rose Sugar; and for the adventurous, there's the peculiar Changwang Noodles. Explore the Zhang Family Courtyard, the Black Temple, and climb the old town walls for a view of tiled roofs against a mountain backdrop.

Provincial Museum: Unlocking Guizhou's Soul

To truly understand the region you're exploring, the Guizhou Provincial Museum is essential. Its striking, modern architecture is a landmark itself. Inside, world-class exhibits detail the incredible geology of the karst landscape, the dazzling silver jewelry and intricate embroidery of the Miao people, and the unique customs of the Dong and Buyi communities. It provides crucial context before you venture out to more remote villages and helps you appreciate the depth of history and tradition that shapes life here.

The Modern Pulse and Culinary Heat

Guiyang is not stuck in the past. It's a dynamic, growing city with a youthful energy and a famously spicy palate.

Jiaxiu Tower: The Symbol Illuminated

Standing proudly on Fuyu Bridge over the Nanming River, Jiaxiu Tower is the undisputed architectural symbol of Guiyang. This three-story, Ming-dynasty pavilion has watched over the city for centuries. By day, its elegant wooden structure is impressive. By night, it becomes the city's glittering centerpiece, beautifully illuminated and reflecting in the river below. The surrounding area, Nanming Riverwalk, has been revitalized into a trendy zone with cafes, bookstores, and bars. It’s the perfect place for an evening stroll, feeling the old and new Guiyang seamlessly blend.

The Culinary Adventure: Sour, Spicy, and Addictive

Guiyang’s food is a major travel hotspot in itself. The local mantra is "suan tang yu" (sour and spicy), and it defines the cuisine. You cannot leave without trying: * Si Wa Wa (Cute Baby): A Guiyang specialty—thin potato starch wrappers filled with crunchy vegetables and drenched in a sour-spicy sauce. * Changwang Mian: A noodle dish with a rich, complex broth, often featuring pork intestines or blood cake. * Sour Soup Fish: The ultimate local comfort food. Fish is poached in a fermented tomato and chili-based broth, creating a uniquely tangy and fiery hot pot. For the full experience, head to the Erqi Road Snack Street or the Qingyun Road Night Market. Follow your nose, join the longest queues (a sure sign of quality), and prepare for a flavor explosion.

Venturing Further: Day Trip Gems

Guiang’s true magic often lies just beyond its borders, making it a perfect base for exploration.

Huangguoshu Waterfall: China's Mightiest Cascade

While technically not in Guiyang, a day trip to Huangguoshu Waterfall is non-negotiable. Just two hours by high-speed train or car, this is the largest waterfall in Asia. The thunderous roar and the mist that soaks you from hundreds of meters away is a humbling, awe-inspiring experience. Walk behind the curtain of water through the Water-Curtain Cave for a truly surreal perspective. It’s a full-day adventure that showcases the raw, powerful beauty of Guizhou's geology.

Thousand-Year-Old Dong Villages: A Cultural Pilgrimage

Venture about three hours from Guiyang to the southeastern corners of Guizhou to find the cultural heart of the Dong people. Villages like Zhaoxing are home to some of the most stunning vernacular architecture in the world: the colossal Dong Wind-Rain Bridges and the towering Drum Towers, all built without a single nail. Visiting here is like stepping into another century. If your timing aligns with a local festival, you might witness hauntingly beautiful polyphonic Dong Grand Choirs, a UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage.

The Intangible Essence: What Makes Guiyang Special

The landmarks are the destinations, but the experience is in the details. It's in the cool, clean air that makes every hike refreshing. It's in the genuine curiosity and warmth of the local people, less jaded by mass tourism. It's in the sight of elderly Miao women in traditional dress shopping in a modern supermarket. It's the vibrant, almost chaotic energy of the night markets contrasted with the profound peace of a mountain temple.

Guiang is a city that invites you to slow down. Sip a locally grown Guiyang green tea on a terrace overlooking the hills. Get lost in a morning market. Let the rhythm of the city, dictated more by nature than by neon, reset your own. In a world of hyper-connected, frantic travel, Guiyang offers a different path—one of natural wonder, deep cultural resonance, and a refreshing, cool breeze that seems to whisper for you to stay just a little bit longer.
