Guiyang’s Most Scenic Picnic Spots for a Weekend Outing

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Nestled in the heart of Guizhou Province, Guiyang is a city where lush greenery, karst landscapes, and a mild climate create the perfect setting for outdoor adventures. If you’re looking to escape the urban hustle and enjoy a leisurely picnic with family or friends, Guiyang offers plenty of idyllic spots. Here’s a curated list of the most scenic picnic destinations for your next weekend outing.

Qianling Park: A Classic Urban Retreat

Why It’s Perfect for Picnics

Qianling Park is a beloved green oasis in the heart of Guiyang. With its serene lake, shaded pathways, and playful macaque monkeys, it’s an ideal spot for a laid-back picnic. The park’s open lawns provide ample space to spread out a blanket, while the surrounding hills offer a picturesque backdrop.

What to Bring

Pack light snacks like fresh fruit, sandwiches, and local Guizhou specialties such as la rou (cured pork) or siwawa (a refreshing spring roll). Don’t forget a thermos of tea—Guiyang’s famous Maojian green tea pairs perfectly with the tranquil setting.

Pro Tip

Visit early in the morning to avoid crowds and catch the mist rising over the lake—a magical sight for photography enthusiasts.

Huaxi National Urban Wetland Park: Lakeside Serenity

The Scenic Appeal

Huaxi Park is one of Guiyang’s most photogenic locations, featuring crystal-clear lakes, arched bridges, and blooming lotus flowers in summer. The park’s vast open spaces make it a prime picnic spot, especially near the water’s edge.

Activities Beyond Picnicking

After your meal, rent a paddleboat or take a leisurely stroll along the wooden boardwalks. The park is also a hotspot for birdwatching, so bring binoculars if you’re into wildlife spotting.

Best Picnic Setup

Opt for a spot near the iconic Tianhe Tan (Heavenly Pool) for the best views. A charcuterie board with local cheeses and dried fruits would complement the serene atmosphere.

Baihua Lake: A Hidden Gem

Why It’s Worth the Trip

Located about 22 km from downtown Guiyang, Baihua Lake is a lesser-known retreat with emerald waters and rolling hills. The lack of large crowds makes it perfect for a peaceful picnic with uninterrupted nature views.

Picnic Essentials

Since facilities are limited, bring a portable cooler with drinks and easy-to-eat finger foods. A foldable camping table can enhance your setup if you prefer not to sit on the ground.

Bonus Adventure

If you’re feeling active, hike one of the nearby trails for panoramic views of the lake. Sunset picnics here are particularly breathtaking.

Qingyan Ancient Town: A Historic Picnic Setting

Combining Culture and Leisure

For those who love history with their outdoor dining, Qingyan Ancient Town offers a unique picnic experience. Spread your blanket in the grassy areas just outside the ancient walls, where you can enjoy a meal before exploring the town’s Ming- and Qing-era architecture.

Must-Try Local Bites

Grab some Qingyan tofu or rose sugar from the vendors inside the town to add a local flavor to your picnic.

Ideal Timing

Weekday visits are quieter, but weekends bring lively street performances that can add entertainment to your outing.

Nanjiang Grand Canyon: For the Adventurous Picnicker

Rugged Beauty

If you’re up for a short road trip, Nanjiang Grand Canyon’s dramatic cliffs and waterfalls create an unforgettable picnic backdrop. The sound of rushing water and the fresh mountain air elevate the experience.

Packing Smart

Sturdy picnic gear is a must—think waterproof blankets and secure food containers. Energy-boosting snacks like nuts and dark chocolate are great for refueling after a hike.

Safety Note

Stick to designated picnic areas, as some trails can be slippery near the waterfalls.

Guiyang Forest Park: A Family-Friendly Option

Why Families Love It

With playgrounds, barbecue pits, and plenty of shaded areas, Guiyang Forest Park is a hit among families. The tall pine trees provide natural shade, making it comfortable even on warmer days.

Picnic Menu Ideas

Go for a barbecue picnic—many locals bring portable grills for an interactive meal. Marinated meats, corn, and mushrooms are crowd-pleasers.

Extra Fun

Rent bikes or try the park’s mini zip line for added excitement after eating.

Tips for the Perfect Guiyang Picnic

  • Check the Weather: Guiyang’s climate is mild, but sudden rain showers are common. A lightweight tarp or umbrella can save the day.
  • Eco-Friendly Practices: Always clean up afterward to preserve these beautiful spots.
  • Local Flavors: Incorporate Guizhou snacks like liangfen (cold mung bean jelly) or zhusun chao rou (bamboo shoots with pork) for an authentic touch.

Whether you prefer lakeside tranquility, historic charm, or rugged adventure, Guiyang’s picnic spots cater to every taste. So pack your basket, gather your favorite people, and savor a weekend surrounded by nature’s beauty.
