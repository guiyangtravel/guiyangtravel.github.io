Adventure Seekers’ Guide to Guiyang’s Must-Visit Spots

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Guiyang, the capital of Guizhou Province, is a hidden gem for adventure seekers. Nestled in the heart of China’s karst landscape, this city offers a mix of thrilling outdoor activities, rich cultural experiences, and breathtaking natural wonders. Whether you’re into hiking, exploring caves, or immersing yourself in local traditions, Guiyang has something for every adrenaline junkie. Here’s your ultimate guide to the must-visit spots in Guiyang.

1. Embrace the Wild: Outdoor Adventures in Guiyang

Qianling Mountain Park – Hike with Monkeys

For a quick escape into nature, head to Qianling Mountain Park. This urban oasis is home to wild macaques that roam freely, adding an element of surprise to your hike. The trails range from easy strolls to challenging climbs, with the Hongfu Temple perched at the top as a rewarding endpoint. The panoramic view of Guiyang from the summit is worth every step.

Nanjiang Grand Canyon – Rafting and Cliff Jumping

If water adventures are your thing, Nanjiang Grand Canyon is a must. The canyon offers exhilarating white-water rafting experiences, with rapids ranging from Class II to IV. For the bold, there are spots for cliff jumping into crystal-clear pools. The surrounding cliffs and lush greenery make this a visually stunning adventure.

Tianhetan Scenic Area – Caving and Zip-lining

Tianhetan is a paradise for cave explorers. The area features massive karst caves with underground rivers, stalactites, and chambers that feel like another world. Some tours even include zip-lining across caverns for an extra thrill. Don’t miss the "Sky River" waterfall inside the cave—it’s a surreal sight.

2. Cultural Thrills: Unique Local Experiences

Qingyan Ancient Town – Time Travel on Cobblestone Streets

Step back in time at Qingyan Ancient Town, a well-preserved Ming and Qing dynasty village. The maze-like streets are perfect for exploration, with hidden temples, old fortifications, and traditional snack stalls. Try the local delicacy, Qingyan Tofu, or challenge yourself with the spicy Siwawa (a rice-flour wrap filled with fiery sauces).

Miao and Dong Villages – Ethnic Adventures

Just outside Guiyang, you’ll find villages of the Miao and Dong ethnic minorities. These communities offer immersive experiences like traditional drum dancing, handicraft workshops, and even homestays. The Lusheng Festival (a reed-pipe music celebration) is a high-energy event where visitors can join in the dancing and singing.

Guiyang’s Night Markets – A Foodie’s Dare

For a different kind of adventure, dive into Guiyang’s night markets. Stalls serve everything from grilled insects to Changwang Noodles (a spicy, sour local favorite). Dare to try "Sour Soup Fish", a fermented broth that’s an acquired taste but a true Guizhou specialty.

3. Off-the-Beaten-Path Thrills

Getu River – Rock Climbing Mecca

A few hours from Guiyang, Getu River is a world-class rock-climbing destination. The towering karst cliffs attract climbers from around the globe. Even if you’re a beginner, guided climbs are available. The nearby Chuandong Cave is another highlight—a massive cavern where local climbers practice deep-water soloing.

Huangguoshu Waterfall – Behind the Curtain

While Huangguoshu is famous, few know about the Water-Curtain Cave behind the falls. Walking through this tunnel lets you experience the thunderous waterfall from behind—prepare to get soaked! The misty rainbow views are unforgettable.

Bijiashan Park – Urban Cliffside Walks

Right in the city, Bijiashan Park offers cliffside walkways with dramatic drops. The park’s trails wind along steep rock faces, giving urban explorers a rush without leaving Guiyang. Sunrise hikes here are particularly magical.

4. Seasonal Adventures

Spring: Cherry Blossom Trekking in Pingba

In early spring, the Pingba Cherry Blossom Festival turns the hills pink. Hiking through these blooming valleys feels like walking through a fairy tale.

Summer: Cool Off in Dragon Palace Cave

The Dragon Palace Cave system stays cool year-round. Summer visitors can boat through its underground lakes, surrounded by glittering stalactites.

Autumn: Golden Rice Terraces in Zhaoxing

A bit farther out, Zhaoxing’s terraced fields turn gold in autumn. Trekking through these landscapes is a photographer’s dream.

Winter: Hot Springs in Xifeng

After a year of adventures, unwind in Xifeng’s geothermal hot springs. The contrast of cold air and warm water is pure bliss.

Guiyang is more than just a stopover—it’s a playground for adventurers. From heart-pounding outdoor activities to deep cultural dives, this city promises unforgettable experiences. Pack your gear, bring your curiosity, and get ready to explore Guiyang like never before.
