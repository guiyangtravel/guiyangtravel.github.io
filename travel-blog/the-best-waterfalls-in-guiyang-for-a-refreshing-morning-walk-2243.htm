The Best Waterfalls in Guiyang for a Refreshing Morning Walk

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Guiyang, the capital of Guizhou Province, is a city blessed with lush greenery, karst landscapes, and some of the most stunning waterfalls in China. If you're an early riser looking for a serene escape, a morning walk to one of Guiyang’s breathtaking waterfalls is the perfect way to start your day. The cool mist, the sound of rushing water, and the fresh mountain air create an invigorating experience. Here are the best waterfalls in Guiyang for a refreshing morning adventure.

1. Huangguoshu Waterfall: The Crown Jewel

Why It’s a Must-Visit

Huangguoshu Waterfall is not just the most famous waterfall in Guiyang—it’s one of the largest in Asia. Standing at 77.8 meters tall and 101 meters wide, this colossal cascade is a sight to behold, especially in the early morning when the sunlight creates rainbows in the mist.

Best Time to Visit

Arrive just after sunrise to avoid crowds. The morning light enhances the golden hues of the waterfall, and the cool air makes the walk even more pleasant.

Walking Trails

The area around Huangguoshu offers several well-maintained trails:
- Water-Viewing Pavilion Trail: A short, easy path leading to the best vantage points.
- Rhinoceros Pool Trail: A slightly longer route that takes you behind the waterfall for a thrilling perspective.

2. Tianxingqiao Scenic Area: A Hidden Gem

The Three-Step Waterfall

Tianxingqiao is lesser-known but equally mesmerizing. The waterfall here cascades in three distinct steps, creating a rhythmic, soothing sound. The surrounding karst formations add to the mystical atmosphere.

Morning Walk Highlights

  • Stone Forest Path: Wander through towering limestone pillars before reaching the falls.
  • Misty Bridge: A small wooden bridge where you can feel the waterfall’s spray—perfect for cooling off on a warm morning.

Pro Tip

Pack a light breakfast and enjoy it by the waterfall. The tranquility of the early hours makes it an ideal spot for meditation or quiet reflection.

3. Dragon Palace Waterfall: A Surreal Experience

The Cave Waterfall

What makes Dragon Palace Waterfall unique is its location—inside a karst cave. The waterfall plunges into an emerald-green pool, illuminated by natural light filtering through the cave entrance.

Best Morning Route

  • Boat Ride: Take a short boat trip through the cave system to reach the waterfall. The morning mist adds an ethereal touch.
  • Cliffside Walkway: For those who prefer walking, a suspended path offers stunning views of the waterfall from above.

Why Go Early?

The cave is less crowded in the morning, and the soft light creates a magical glow on the water.

4. Zhijin Cave Waterfall: For the Adventurous

A Waterfall Inside a Cave?

Yes! Zhijin Cave is one of the most spectacular cave systems in China, and its underground waterfall is a hidden wonder. The sound of water echoing through the caverns is unforgettable.

Morning Exploration Tips

  • Guided Tours: Since the cave is vast, a guided morning tour ensures you don’t miss the waterfall.
  • Sturdy Footwear: The paths can be slippery, so wear shoes with good grip.

Unique Feature

The waterfall’s flow changes with the seasons, so visiting in the rainy season (May–September) offers the most dramatic views.

5. Qingshui River Waterfall: A Peaceful Retreat

Off-the-Beaten-Path Beauty

Located along the Qingshui River, this waterfall is quieter than Huangguoshu but just as stunning. The surrounding bamboo forests and clear pools make it a perfect morning escape.

Walking Routes

  • Riverside Trail: A gentle path following the river, leading to the waterfall.
  • Bamboo Grove Detour: A short side trail takes you through dense bamboo, adding to the serenity.

Why Visit in the Morning?

The water is crystal clear in the early hours, and you might spot local wildlife like birds and butterflies.

6. Detian Waterfall: A Borderline Wonder

Where Guizhou Meets Guangxi

Though technically on the border with Guangxi, Detian Waterfall is close enough to Guiyang for a day trip. It’s the largest transnational waterfall in Asia, with water cascading down multiple tiers.

Morning Magic

  • Bamboo Raft Ride: Glide across the pool at the base of the falls for an up-close experience.
  • Viewing Platforms: Several platforms offer panoramic views, especially stunning in the soft morning light.

Best Season

Visit between July and October when the water flow is at its peak.

Final Tips for Your Waterfall Walk

  • Wear Comfortable Shoes: Many trails involve uneven terrain.
  • Bring Water and Snacks: Staying hydrated is key, especially if you’re hiking.
  • Respect Nature: Stick to marked paths and avoid littering.

Guiyang’s waterfalls are more than just natural wonders—they’re gateways to tranquility and adventure. Whether you’re a solo traveler, a couple, or a family, a morning walk to these cascades will leave you refreshed and inspired.
