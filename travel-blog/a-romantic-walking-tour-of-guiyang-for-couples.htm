A Romantic Walking Tour of Guiyang for Couples

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Forget the crowded, checklist-driven tours. The true soul of Guiyang, the capital of China’s lush Guizhou province, is best discovered on foot, hand-in-hand. This is a city where cool mountain air mingles with the spicy aroma of street food, where ancient stone pathways hide behind modern facades, and where the pace of life invites you to slow down and connect. This walking tour is designed for couples seeking a blend of serene nature, whispered history, and authentic local flavor—a perfect itinerary to fall in love with Guiyang, and perhaps, with each other a little more.

Morning: Whispers of History & Aromatic Alleys

Begin your day not with an alarm, but with the gentle promise of exploration. Start in the heart of the old city at Qianling Park. While many climb to the Hongfu Temple on the mountain, we suggest a more intimate path. Stroll around Qianling Lake, where the morning mist clings to the water and locals practice tai chi. Find a quiet bench overlooking the lake; it’s a moment of tranquility before the city fully awakens.

From here, meander towards the Jiaxiu Tower, Guiyang’s iconic landmark. Standing proudly on Fuyu Bridge over the Nanming River, this three-story Ming Dynasty pavilion is a symbol of scholarly aspiration. Cross the bridge slowly, feeling the history underfoot. The view of the tower reflected in the river is a classic photo opportunity for two.

The Soul of the City: Zhongshan Road & Culinary Adventures

Now, dive into the sensory tapestry of Zhongshan Road. This pedestrian street is a living museum of architecture, where Art Deco buildings stand beside traditional wooden houses. But the real romance here is culinary. This is a shared adventure for your taste buds.

Stop at a local stall for “Si Wa Wa” – literally “little baby” – a adorable local snack where a thin rice wrapper cradles a mix of crispy vegetables, herbs, and a savory sauce. Feed each other the first bite. Follow the irresistible smell of tofu to find “Chang Wang Mian” stalls, serving Guizhou’s famous fermented tofu, its strong aroma a hilarious and bonding experience. For a sweet pause, share a bowl of “Ice Jelly” (Liangfen), a refreshing, crystal-clear jelly doused in rose syrup and honey—a perfect metaphor for Guiyang itself: cool, sweet, and utterly unique.

Afternoon: Artistic Retreats & Forest Canopies

After lunch, escape the urban buzz with a short taxi ride to the Guiyang Art Museum or a local pottery studio in the surrounding hills. The region’s minority cultures, like the Miao and Dong, inspire incredible contemporary art and crafts. Participating in a short, hands-on workshop—painting a simple ceramic piece or learning a basic embroidery stitch—creates a tangible, personal souvenir of your journey together.

Next, immerse yourselves in Guiyang’s title as the “City of Forests.” Nanming River Urban Wetland Park offers a gorgeous, easy walk along manicured paths by the water. However, for the truly magical experience, venture to Qianling Mountain’s back trails. Walk under a dense canopy of ancient trees, where sunlight filters through in dappled patterns. The air is noticeably fresher, filled with the scent of earth and pine. Find a secluded spot and just listen: to the birds, the wind in the leaves, and the quiet between you. It’s a natural couples’ therapy session.

The Hidden Gem: A Coffee Break in a Bookstore

As the afternoon wanes, seek out one of Guiyang’s many boutique bookstores or hidden cafes. Places like Also Bookstore or small cafes tucked away in the Nanjing Road area offer a cozy respite. Curl up with a book on Guizhou’s folklore, share a pot of local Meitan tea, or simply enjoy the quiet hum of a space dedicated to calm. It’s a modern romantic interlude in the midst of your historical and natural exploration.

Evening: Twilight Lights & The Symphony of Night Markets

As dusk paints the sky, make your way to Jiaxiu Tower again. Seeing it illuminated against the darkening sky, its golden lights shimmering on the river, is a completely different, and profoundly romantic, spectacle. The surrounding plaza comes alive with families and couples, adding a joyful energy to the majestic scene.

No romantic tour of Guiyang is complete without surrendering to the glorious chaos of a night market. Erqi Road Night Market is an institution. Here, romance is found in shared curiosity and bold culinary leaps. Hold hands tightly as you navigate the vibrant crowds.

A Feast for Two: The Ultimate Foodie Bonding

This is your dinner, course by course, from different stalls. Start with grilled skewers, the smoky aroma irresistible. Be adventurous and try the “Zhu’ergen” (a local root vegetable). Then, move to the main event: Guizhou Sour Soup Fish. Share a steaming pot of this iconic dish—the tangy, slightly spicy broth and tender fish is a warming, communal experience. For the brave, a taste of “Yang Rou Fen” (mutton rice noodles) is a must. Finally, end your feast with sweet, sticky “Ciba” (glutinous rice cake) roasted over coals. Sharing these bold, unforgettable flavors creates stories you’ll tell for years.

Beyond the Walk: Romantic Hot-Spot Extensions

If your love story in Guiyang has more than one day, consider these deeper dive experiences, currently trending among savvy couples.

Day Trip to Qingyan Ancient Town

A short trip from the city, this 600-year-old Ming Dynasty town is a labyrinth of stone-slab streets, crumbling city walls, and ancient temples. It’s wonderfully touristy yet authentically charming. Get lost in the narrow alleyways, discover a quiet courtyard, and sample local treats like “Qingyan Braised Pork” and rose sugar. The journey there, through Guizhou’s iconic karst landscape, is part of the romance.

The Ultimate Natural Wonder: Huangguoshu Waterfall

While requiring a day trip, no couple’s visit to the region is complete without witnessing the thunderous power and misty beauty of Huangguoshu, Asia’s largest waterfall. Walk hand-in-hand behind the curtain of water through the Water-Curtain Cave, where the roar of the falls vibrates through you—a powerful, awe-inspiring moment to share. The surrounding national park offers plenty of serene spots for quiet reflection amidst breathtaking scenery.

Staying in Style: Boutique Hotels & Unique Lodging

The accommodation scene in Guiyang has blossomed. Skip the international chains and opt for a boutique hotel in the Nanming River area, offering stunning city views from your private balcony. For a truly unique experience, look for a designer guesthouse (“Minsu”) in the hills surrounding the city, where you can wake up to misty mountain vistas and the sound of nature. This focus on aesthetic and intimate lodging is a major travel trend, turning your hotel into a key part of the romantic experience.

Guiyang’s romance isn’t loud or ostentatious. It’s in the shared bowl of sour soup, the quiet hold of a hand on a forest path, the mutual smile after a surprising taste, and the golden glow of an ancient tower against a night sky. It’s a city that invites you to wander, to wonder, and to weave your own love story into its cool, green, and wonderfully flavorful tapestry.
