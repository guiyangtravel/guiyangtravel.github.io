Best Places to Enjoy a Night of Board Games and Drinks in Guiyang

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

The city of Guiyang, cradled by verdant mountains, is famous for its cool climate, fiery cuisine, and the mesmerizing nightscape of the Nanming River. But as the sun dips behind the karst peaks, a different kind of energy pulses through the city's veins. Beyond the bustling night markets and vibrant bars, a thriving social scene has taken root, one that combines cerebral strategy with clinking glasses and shared laughter. For travelers and locals alike, an evening spent navigating board games with a well-crafted drink in hand has become a quintessential Guiyang experience. It’s a perfect fusion of the city's warm, communal spirit and its modern, cosmopolitan ambitions. This is your guide to the best places to dive into this world of dice, cards, and cocktails.

Why Guiyang is a Perfect Backdrop for Game Nights

Guiyang might not be the first city that comes to mind for a global board game renaissance, but it possesses an innate charm that makes it ideal for this pastime. The weather, often cool and misty, creates a cozy atmosphere that makes you want to settle indoors with good company. The local culture is deeply social; meals are shared from communal hot pots, and conversations flow as freely as the local Moutai. This inherent desire for connection translates seamlessly into the world of board games.

Furthermore, Guiyang is a city of contrasts. Ancient temples stand in the shadow of gleaming skyscrapers. This blend of old and new is reflected in its entertainment options. You can spend your afternoon exploring Qingyan Ancient Town and your evening conquering empires in a modern board game cafe. The city's youthful energy, driven by its growing tech industry and universities, has fostered a demand for innovative and interactive social spaces. Board game cafes and bars are the perfect answer.

The Social Fabric: More Than Just Games

At its heart, a board game night in Guiyang is about renao—the Chinese concept of lively, bustling fun. It’s the sound of triumph when someone wins a tight game of Catan, the groans when a hidden traitor is revealed in The Resistance, and the collaborative cheers during a tense game of Pandemic. It’s a way to break down barriers. For solo travelers, it's an incredible opportunity to meet people. For groups of friends, it's a new way to bond. The games become a universal language, transcending any potential language gaps and creating memories far more lasting than any sightseeing tour.

Top Venues for Your Game Night Adventure

From dedicated cafes with walls of games to trendy bars with a curated selection, Guiyang offers a variety of atmospheres for every type of gamer and drinker.

The Strategist's Sanctuary: "Meeple Mountain Board Game Cafe"

Tucked away on the second floor of a building near the city center, Meeple Mountain is a pilgrimage site for serious gamers. The walls are lined, floor to ceiling, with over a thousand games, meticulously organized from light party games to complex Eurogames that take hours to complete. The staff, true board game aficionados, are more than happy to teach you the rules of any game that catches your eye.

The Vibe: The atmosphere is focused and intellectual, punctuated by bursts of excitement. Large, well-lit tables and comfortable chairs are designed for long campaigns. This is the place to bring your fellow gamers for a deep dive into the hobby.

The Game Selection: Everything from classic gateway games like Ticket to Ride and Carcassonne to heavyweight champions like Terraforming Mars and Gloomhaven. They have an entire section dedicated to Chinese-themed games, which can be a fascinating cultural experience.

The Drinks: While they serve excellent pour-over coffee and artisanal teas, their evening menu shines with local craft beers from Nanbrewery and a selection of creative cocktails named after famous games. Try the "Settler's G&T" or the "Carcassonne Spritz."

The Trendy Social Hub: "Dice & Drafts"

Located in the hip and artistic district of Guiyang, Dice & Drafts is where style meets strategy. It feels more like a chic lounge than a traditional game cafe, with low lighting, industrial decor, and a constant, cool soundtrack. It’s a popular spot for a younger, fashion-conscious crowd looking to socialize.

The Vibe: Energetic, stylish, and unapologetically social. It's less about intense strategy and more about the experience—the perfect backdrop for a first date or a fun night out with colleagues after work.

The Game Selection: Curated for fun and interaction. You'll find a lot of party games like Codenames, Dixit, and Just One, as well as accessible strategy games like Splendor and 7 Wonders. It's the ideal place to be introduced to the hobby without feeling overwhelmed.

The Drinks: This is their standout feature. A long, sleek bar serves as the centerpiece, offering a massive menu of signature cocktails, imported beers, and a wide range of baijiu for the brave. Their "D20 Old Fashioned" is a local legend. The bartenders are artists, and the drinks are as much a part of the evening as the games.

The Cozy Nook: "The Lucky Draw"

For those seeking a more intimate and relaxed setting, The Lucky Draw is a hidden gem. With soft armchairs, warm wood tones, and bookshelves filled with both games and novels, it feels like the living room you wish you had. It’s a favorite among small groups of friends and couples.

The Vibe: Intimate, quiet, and incredibly cozy. The background music is a soft jazz or acoustic playlist, allowing for easy conversation. It’s a sanctuary from the city's buzz.

The Game Selection: Focused on two-player and small-group games. You'll find beautifully crafted abstract games like Azul, clever card games like Arboretum, and narrative-driven games like Sherlock Holmes: Consulting Detective. It's perfect for a more thoughtful and connected gaming session.

The Drinks: They specialize in tea-based cocktails and high-quality Chinese teas, a nod to Guiyang's proximity to famous tea regions. Their "Jasmine Pearl Martini" is sublime. They also offer a great selection of warm sake and craft cider.

Beyond the Game: Pairing Your Evening with Local Flavors

No night in Guiyang is complete without indulging in its culinary scene. You can easily combine your board game adventure with a taste of the city's famous street food.

The Pre-Game Fuel: A Trip to a Night Market

Before you settle in for an evening of gaming, head to one of Guiyang's legendary night markets. The aroma of si wa wa (a local-style spring roll) and sizzling zhua jian (a Guizhou street-food sandwich) will guide you. Grab a few sticks of chuan'er (barbecue skewers) and a bowl of sour and spicy Guizhou-style noodles. This bold, flavorful start will energize you for the battles to come on the game board.

The In-Game Snack: A Shared Platter

Many of the board game venues offer food menus designed for sharing. Order a platter of dried fruits and nuts, some spicy potato chips, or a plate of local la rou (cured meat) to nibble on between turns. It keeps the energy up and adds a communal, almost familial feel to the table.

The Post-Game Wind-Down: A Nightcap by the River

After your final game has been won and the dice have been put away, take a short stroll to the banks of the Nanming River. The city lights reflecting on the water create a magical atmosphere. Find a quiet bar with a terrace, order a nightcap—perhaps a smooth local craft beer—and debrief the night's triumphs and betrayals with your friends. It's the perfect, serene conclusion to a night of lively interaction.

Pro Tips for Your Guiyang Board Game Night

  • Go with an Open Mind: Be willing to try games you've never heard of. The staff's recommendations are often the highlight of the night.
  • Embrace the Local Crowd: Don't be shy! Guiyang people are famously friendly. You might just end up in a spontaneous multi-table game with a group of locals.
  • Manage Your Time: Light games can take 30 minutes, while heavy strategy games can take 3-4 hours. Plan your evening accordingly.
  • Respect the Space: These are social businesses. Be mindful of your volume if the venue is quiet, and always clean up your area.
  • Learn a Few Key Phrases: While many young people in Guiyang speak some English, knowing how to say "your turn" (lun dao ni le) or "cheers" (gan bei) can add a fun, local touch to the evening.
