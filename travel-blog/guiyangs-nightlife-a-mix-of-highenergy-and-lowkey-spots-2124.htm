Guiyang’s Nightlife: A Mix of High-Energy and Low-Key Spots

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Guiyang, the capital of Guizhou Province, is often overshadowed by China’s megacities like Shanghai or Beijing. But for those in the know, this mountainous city offers a nightlife scene that’s both vibrant and diverse. From pulsating nightclubs to serene riverside bars, Guiyang has something for every nocturnal adventurer. Whether you’re a party animal or someone who prefers a quiet drink under the stars, here’s your guide to the best after-dark experiences in Guiyang.

The High-Energy Side of Guiyang

If you’re looking for a night of dancing, loud music, and endless energy, Guiyang won’t disappoint. The city’s club scene has grown rapidly, with venues catering to different tastes—from EDM lovers to hip-hop enthusiasts.

1. Playhouse Club: The Ultimate Party Destination

Playhouse is one of Guiyang’s most famous nightclubs, known for its high-end sound systems, dazzling light shows, and top-tier DJs. The venue often hosts international and domestic DJs, making it a hotspot for those who want to dance until dawn. The crowd here is a mix of locals, expats, and tourists, all united by a love for electronic beats.

2. V Plus Lounge: A Trendy VIP Experience

For a more upscale night out, V Plus Lounge offers bottle service, plush seating, and a sleek, modern design. It’s a favorite among Guiyang’s young professionals and influencers. The music leans toward hip-hop and R&B, with occasional live performances. If you’re celebrating a special occasion, this is the place to be.

3. Mao Livehouse: Where Music and Culture Collide

If you prefer live music over DJ sets, Mao Livehouse is a must-visit. This venue hosts indie bands, rock acts, and even underground hip-hop artists. The atmosphere is raw and energetic, with a crowd that’s passionate about music. Check their schedule in advance—some nights feature local Guizhou bands blending traditional Miao music with modern genres.

The Low-Key Side of Guiyang

Not everyone wants a wild night out. For those who prefer a relaxed vibe, Guiyang offers plenty of cozy bars, teahouses, and late-night eateries where you can unwind.

1. Nanming River Bar Street: A Riverside Retreat

One of the most picturesque spots for a laid-back evening is Nanming River Bar Street. Lined with small bars and cafés, this area is perfect for sipping a cocktail while watching the river shimmer under the city lights. Many bars here offer outdoor seating, making it ideal for warm summer nights.

2. 28 Bar: A Hidden Gem for Craft Beer Lovers

Tucked away in a quiet alley, 28 Bar is a haven for craft beer enthusiasts. The owner, a passionate brewer, offers a rotating selection of local and imported beers. The vibe is intimate, with wooden décor and soft lighting—perfect for deep conversations or solo relaxation.

3. Traditional Teahouses: A Taste of Guizhou’s Culture

Guiyang’s teahouses are a unique way to experience local culture after dark. Places like Qingyan Old Town Teahouse serve fragrant teas alongside traditional snacks. The ambiance is serene, often accompanied by live Guqin (a classical Chinese instrument) performances. It’s a far cry from the city’s clubs but just as memorable.

Late-Night Eats: Fueling the Night

No nightlife guide would be complete without mentioning Guiyang’s legendary street food. After a night of partying or bar-hopping, these spots are where locals go to refuel.

1. Siwawa (丝娃娃): Guiyang’s Must-Try Snack

This DIY-style dish consists of thin rice pancakes wrapped around fresh vegetables, tofu, and spicy sauces. You’ll find vendors selling Siwawa late into the night, especially near Jiaxiu Lou, the city’s iconic pavilion.

2. Changwang Noodles (肠旺面): A Spicy Midnight Feast

A local favorite, Changwang Noodles features chewy noodles in a rich, spicy broth topped with pork intestines and blood curd. It might sound intense, but it’s a beloved comfort food for night owls.

3. Barbecue Stalls on Zhongshan Road

For grilled skewers (chuan’r), head to Zhongshan Road. Vendors serve everything from lamb to mushrooms, all seasoned with Guizhou’s signature spicy and sour flavors. Pair it with a cold beer for the ultimate late-night feast.

Unique Nighttime Experiences

Beyond bars and food, Guiyang offers some one-of-a-kind nighttime activities.

1. Night Views from Qianling Mountain

For a peaceful escape, hike up Qianling Mountain (or take a taxi) to see the city lights spread out below. The view is breathtaking, especially on clear nights.

2. Night Markets in Qingyan Ancient Town

A short drive from downtown, Qingyan Ancient Town transforms at night with lantern-lit streets and vendors selling handmade crafts and snacks. It’s like stepping back in time.

3. KTV: Sing the Night Away

No Chinese nightlife experience is complete without KTV. Guiyang has plenty of high-quality KTV lounges where you can belt out tunes with friends in private rooms.

Whether you’re chasing adrenaline or tranquility, Guiyang’s nightlife delivers. The city’s blend of modern energy and traditional charm makes it a hidden gem for night owls. So next time you’re in Guizhou, don’t turn in early—Guiyang after dark is an adventure waiting to happen.
