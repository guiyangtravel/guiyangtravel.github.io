A Day Trip to Guiyang’s Most Famous Nature Parks

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Guiyang, the capital of Guizhou Province, is a hidden gem in Southwest China. Known for its lush landscapes, karst formations, and refreshing climate, it’s a paradise for nature lovers. If you’re planning a day trip to explore Guiyang’s most famous nature parks, this guide will take you through the must-visit spots, local delicacies, and insider tips to make the most of your adventure.

Qianling Park: The Green Heart of Guiyang

Why Visit Qianling Park?

Nestled in the heart of the city, Qianling Park is a serene escape from urban hustle. It’s famous for its dense forests, tranquil lakes, and playful macaque monkeys. The park is a favorite among locals for morning tai chi sessions and leisurely strolls.

Highlights of the Park

  • Hongfu Temple: A historic Buddhist temple perched on a hill, offering panoramic views of Guiyang.
  • Macau Lake: Rent a paddleboat or simply enjoy the peaceful surroundings.
  • Monkey Zone: Watch (but don’t feed!) the mischievous macaques roaming freely.

Pro Tip

Visit early in the morning to avoid crowds and catch the misty sunrise over the lake.

Huaxi National Urban Wetland Park: A Waterfront Wonderland

What Makes Huaxi Special?

Huaxi is a sprawling wetland park with crystal-clear rivers, bamboo groves, and traditional Miao minority villages. It’s perfect for cycling, picnicking, or just soaking in the scenery.

Must-Do Activities

  • Bamboo Rafting: Glide along the Huaxi River for a relaxing ride.
  • Ten-Mile Gallery: A scenic pathway lined with cherry blossoms in spring.
  • Local Snacks: Try siwawa (a spicy Guizhou wrap) from vendors near the entrance.

Best Time to Visit

Spring and autumn are ideal, with comfortable temperatures and vibrant foliage.

Baihua Lake: The "Thousand-Island Lake" of Guizhou

Why Baihua Lake Stands Out

This artificial lake is dotted with hundreds of small islands, creating a dreamy landscape. It’s less touristy than other spots, making it perfect for a quiet retreat.

Top Experiences

  • Island Hopping: Take a ferry to explore hidden coves and tiny islets.
  • Photography: Capture the mirror-like water at sunrise or sunset.
  • Lakeside Cafés: Sip local maojian tea while enjoying the view.

Insider Advice

Pack a picnic—there are plenty of secluded spots to enjoy a meal by the water.

Local Flavors to Try Along the Way

No trip to Guiyang is complete without indulging in its fiery, flavorful cuisine. Here are three must-try dishes:
1. Sour Fish Soup (suantangyu): A tangy, spicy broth with fresh river fish.
2. Changwang Noodles: Chewy noodles topped with braised beef and chili oil.
3. Grilled Stinky Tofu: Smelly but addictive—best tried at night markets.

Practical Tips for Your Day Trip

  • Transport: Use Didi (China’s Uber) or public buses to get around.
  • Weather: Guiyang is rainy—bring a light jacket and umbrella.
  • Cashless Payments: Most vendors accept WeChat Pay or Alipay.

Whether you’re a solo traveler, a couple, or a family, Guiyang’s nature parks offer something for everyone. From monkey encounters to island adventures, this day trip will leave you refreshed and inspired by Guizhou’s natural beauty.
