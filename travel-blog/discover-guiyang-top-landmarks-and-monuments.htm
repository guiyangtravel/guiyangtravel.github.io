Discover Guiyang: Top Landmarks and Monuments

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Nestled in the rolling hills of Southwest China, Guiyang, the capital of Guizhou province, is a city that often surprises the intrepid traveler. It’s a place where the humid, subtropical air carries the scent of blooming osmanthus, where modern skyscrapers peek out from behind lush, forested mountains, and where the vibrant cultures of numerous ethnic minorities infuse daily life with color and rhythm. This is not a city of overwhelming, world-famous monuments like the Great Wall or the Forbidden City; rather, it’s a destination whose treasures reveal themselves slowly, inviting you to explore its unique geological wonders, its profound historical pockets, and its dynamic, modern energy. To discover Guiyang is to understand a different, more nuanced side of China.

The Heart of the City: Qianling Mountain Park

No visit to Guiyang is complete without a pilgrimage to its green lung and spiritual center, Qianling Mountain Park. Located in the northwestern part of the city, this vast park is an oasis of tranquility, a world away from the urban buzz just beyond its gates.

Hongfu Temple: A Sanctuary in the Clouds

The centerpiece of the park is the magnificent Hongfu Temple, one of the most renowned Zen Buddhist temples in all of China. As you ascend the seemingly endless stone steps, flanked by ancient, towering trees, the city sounds fade, replaced by the chanting of monks and the gentle ringing of temple bells. The temple complex is a masterpiece of traditional architecture, with multiple halls dedicated to different Buddhas and bodhisattvas. The main hall, the Mahavira Hall, is a sight to behold, filled with the scent of sandalwood incense and the serene presence of golden statues. The true magic of Hongfu Temple, however, lies in its atmosphere. It’s a living, breathing place of worship, where devout pilgrims pray alongside curious tourists, all sharing a moment of peace high above the city.

Encounter with the Macaques

Qianling Mountain is also famously home to hundreds of wild Tibetan macaques. These playful, and sometimes mischievous, creatures are a major attraction. They swing from branches, groom each other on the pathways, and curiously observe the human visitors. While they are entertaining to watch, it's crucial to remember they are wild animals. Securing your belongings, especially food and shiny objects, is highly recommended. This interaction with wildlife in such a sacred and natural setting creates a unique, memorable experience that blends the spiritual with the primal.

A Geological Masterpiece: Jiaxiu Tower

Standing proudly on the Fuyu Bridge over the Nanming River, Jiaxiu Tower is the undisputed symbol of Guiyang. Its name, translating to "Champion's Tower," reflects its historical connection to scholars who topped the imperial examinations. This iconic three-story, three-eaved wooden pagoda has been destroyed and rebuilt several times since the Ming Dynasty, yet it remains a powerful emblem of the city's cultural and academic aspirations.

Visiting Jiaxiu Tower is best done in the evening. As dusk settles, the tower and its reflection in the river are bathed in golden light, creating a picture-perfect scene. Inside, you can explore calligraphy and painting exhibitions that delve into Guizhou's history. Climbing to the top offers a panoramic view of the modern city skyline juxtaposed with the traditional rooftops, a visual narrative of Guiyang’s journey through time. The surrounding Cuiwei Garden adds to the charm, with its classical Chinese landscaping providing a serene space for a stroll after admiring the tower.

A Glimpse into Aristocratic Life: Qingyan Ancient Town

Venture about 30 kilometers south of Guiyang’s city center, and you'll step back in time into Qingyan Ancient Town. Originally a military fortress built in the Ming Dynasty, it evolved into a prosperous trading post. Today, its remarkably well-preserved stone walls, gates, and labyrinthine streets offer a fascinating glimpse into China's past.

Stone Pathways and Ming-Qing Architecture

The first thing you'll notice is the stone—everywhere. The streets are paved with it, the buildings are constructed from it, and the formidable defensive walls are made of it. Wandering through the narrow alleyways, you'll pass by courtyards, temples, and ancestral halls showcasing the intricate woodcarvings and architectural styles of the Ming and Qing dynasties. It’s easy to lose yourself, both physically and mentally, in this historic maze, imagining the merchants and soldiers who once walked these same paths.

A Culinary Adventure

Qingyan is not just a feast for the eyes but also for the palate. The ancient town is famous for its local snacks, making it a food lover's paradise. You must try Qingyan's signature dish, zhang pig's foot (brined and stewed pork knuckle), which is tender, flavorful, and utterly delicious. Other local specialties include rose sugar ice powder, a refreshing sweet treat, various types of tofu, and cured meats. The vibrant food stalls and small restaurants are an integral part of the Qingyan experience, turning a history tour into a full-sensory adventure.

The Modern Pulse: Guiyang's Contemporary Landscape

Guiyang is not a city stuck in the past. It has embraced the future with vigor, becoming a major hub for big data in China, earning the nickname "The Big Data Capital of China." This modern identity is reflected in its urban development.

Jiaxiu Pavilion Financial District Skyline

The area around Jiaxiu Tower has transformed into a stunning financial district. By day, the glass-and-steel skyscrapers gleam in the sun; by night, they become a canvas for dazzling light shows that reflect off the Nanming River. This creates a spectacular contrast with the illuminated traditional tower, a powerful symbol of how Guiyang harmonizes its rich history with its ambitious future. A riverfront walk in this area at night is essential to feel the city's dynamic pulse.

Cultural Hubs: Provincial Museum and Opera House

To deepen your understanding of the region, a visit to the Guizhou Provincial Museum is a must. Its modern exhibitions are dedicated to the history, geology, and, most importantly, the diverse ethnic cultures of Guizhou. Here, you can learn about the traditions, costumes, and silver jewelry of the Miao, Dong, and Buyi peoples. Complementing this is the Guiyang Opera House, a modern architectural marvel that hosts performances ranging from Western opera to local ethnic song and dance shows, like the spectacular Miao grand song ceremonies. These institutions showcase the city's role as a curator and promoter of local culture on a world stage.

Venturing Further: The Natural Wonders on the Outskirts

Guiang’s appeal extends far beyond its city limits. Its karst landscape provides a dramatic backdrop for some of China's most stunning natural attractions.

Tianhe Lake: A Scenic Escape

Located in the western suburbs, Tianhe Lake Scenic Area is a beautiful retreat centered around a serene reservoir. Visitors can take boat rides on the emerald-green water, surrounded by forested karst hills. The area is perfect for hiking, picnicking, and simply enjoying the clean, fresh air. It’s a popular weekend getaway for locals and offers a different, more relaxed perspective on the region's natural beauty.

A Culinary Landmark in Its Own Right

No discussion of Guiyang's landmarks would be complete without mentioning its food scene. Guizhou cuisine, or Qiancai, is a major highlight of any trip, known for its sour and spicy flavors.

Si Wawa and Sour Soup Fish

You cannot leave Guiyang without trying two of its most iconic dishes. Si wawa is a beloved local snack—a delicate steamed dumpling wrapper made from rice flour, filled with a mixture of minced pork, mushrooms, and bamboo shoots, and served with a spicy dipping sauce. Another must-try is suan tang yu (sour soup fish). This hot pot dish features fresh river fish cooked in a fiery, tangy broth made from fermented tomatoes and wild chilies, creating a uniquely addictive flavor profile that is the very soul of Guiyang's culinary identity. Exploring the city's countless restaurants and bustling food streets is a landmark experience in itself.

From the spiritual heights of Qianling Mountain to the historic stones of Qingyan Town, from the symbolic Jiaxiu Tower to the vibrant, modern skyline, Guiyang is a city of beautiful contrasts. It invites you to look closer, to wander its paths, and to taste its unique flavors. It’s a destination that may not be on every traveler's initial itinerary, but one that leaves a profound and lasting impression on all who discover it.
