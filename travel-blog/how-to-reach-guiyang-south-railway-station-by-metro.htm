How to Reach Guiyang South Railway Station by Metro

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Guiyang, the capital of Guizhou Province, is a city where ancient traditions meet modern innovation. Nestled among lush green mountains and famous for its cool climate, it's a gateway to some of China's most stunning natural landscapes and cultural treasures. As a major transportation hub, Guiyang South Railway Station (Guiyangnan) is often the first stop for travelers eager to explore the region's wonders, from the Huangguoshu Waterfall to the minority villages of Southeast Guizhou. Navigating a new city can be daunting, but Guiyang’s modern and efficient metro system makes it incredibly easy. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know about using the metro to reach this crucial hub, seamlessly connecting your travel plans.

Why Guiyang South Railway Station is Your Gateway to Adventure

Before we dive into the metro specifics, it's important to understand why this station is so central to your travel itinerary. Guiyang South is not just a train station; it's a massive, integrated transport complex. It's one of the largest railway stations in Southwest China, serving high-speed trains (G-trains) that connect Guiyang to major cities like Chengdu, Chongqing, Guangzhou, and Shanghai.

Proximity to Tourist Hotspots

While the station itself is located in the Huaxi District, south of the city center, its high-speed rail connections are unbeatable. Within an hour or two, you can be: * Exploring the Huangguoshu Waterfall: A short train ride to Anshun West Station, followed by a bus, brings you to Asia's largest waterfall. The thunderous roar and misty spray are a sensory experience unlike any other. * Immersing in Minority Culture: Take a train to Kaili or Rongjiang to delve into the heart of Miao and Dong minority cultures. Witness their intricate silverwork, listen to their polyphonic choirs, and admire their magnificent wind-and-rain bridges. * Tasting the Local Cuisine: Guiyang is a foodie's paradise, famous for its sour and spicy flavors. Think Siwawa (a fresh spring roll), Changwang Noodles, and the ubiquitous hot pot. The ease of the rail network means you can take a day trip to Zunyi for a different culinary scene and be back by evening.

The Station Complex: More Than Just Trains

The station is a destination in itself. It houses numerous convenience stores, local food outlets where you can grab a quick bite of Yangroufen (rice noodles with mutton), and shops selling local specialties like Maotai liquor (if you're looking for a high-end gift) or chili sauces. There are also car rental offices and a large bus terminal right outside for connections to areas not served by rail.

Mastering the Guiyang Metro: Lines and Basics

Guiang's metro system, though newer than those in Beijing or Shanghai, is clean, user-friendly, and expanding rapidly. As of now, three lines are operational, with more under construction. For reaching Guiyang South, you only need to concern yourself with one.

Key Metro Lines for Travelers

  • Line 1 (The Blue Line): This is your workhorse line. It runs north-south, connecting the city center to the Guiyang North Railway Station and, crucially, to the Guiyang South Railway Station. It's the most important line for tourists.
  • Line 2 (The Orange Line): This line runs more east-west. While it doesn't go directly to the South Station, you might use it to reach important interchange stations like Zhongxing Road to connect to Line 1.
  • Ticket Purchase: Buying a ticket is straightforward. Use the automated ticket vending machines (TVMs) at any station. The interface can be switched to English. You can select your destination station—Guiyang South Railway Station—and pay with coins, banknotes, or by scanning a QR code with your WeChat or Alipay app. Single-journey tickets are RFID tokens. Alternatively, you can purchase a rechargeable metro card if you plan on multiple rides.

The Step-by-Step Metro Journey to Guiyang South Railway Station

Whether you're coming from the city center, the airport, or another part of town, the process is simple.

Step 1: Find Your Nearest Metro Station on Line 1

Your starting point will determine your route. If you are in the city center, key stations to look for include: * Jiefang Road: Close to the bustling main commercial areas. * Hebin Park (Riverside Park): Near the scenic Nanming River. * Zhongxing Road: A major interchange station where you can transfer from Line 2.

Locate the metro sign (a blue "M" often with the word "Metro") and head underground.

Step 2: Board the Train Bound for Xiaoshijiang

Once you're on the platform for Line 1, check the electronic display above the platform. You need to ensure you are getting on a train whose final destination is Xiaoshijiang. This is the direction that goes south towards Guiyang South Railway Station. Trains going the opposite way towards Guiyang North Station will be marked as bound for Xiamahe. Getting on the wrong train will take you away from your goal, so double-check!

Step 3: The Ride and Arrival at Guiyangnan Station

The ride is smooth and a great way to see the city transform outside the window. The journey from the city center (e.g., Jiefang Road) takes approximately 25-30 minutes. Listen for the station announcements in Mandarin and English. You will hear: * "The next station is Guiyang Railway Station South." * "Next stop, Guiyangnan Zhan."

As you approach, gather your belongings. The station is deep underground and has multiple exits.

Step 4: Exiting the Metro and Entering the Railway Station

This is the best part: the metro station is directly integrated into the basement level of the Guiyang South Railway Station complex. Simply follow the signs—they are abundant, clear, and in English. You will see directions for: * High-speed Rail (高铁出发): Points you towards the main concourse for departures. * Train Tickets (火车票): Directs you to the ticket hall if you need to purchase or collect a physical ticket. * Exit (出口): For if you need to go to the bus station or catch a taxi.

There is no need to go outside. You can go from the metro platform to the railway security check and waiting area in a matter of minutes, completely sheltered from the weather. This seamless integration is a testament to China's modern infrastructure.

Pro Travel Tips for a Smooth Journey

Best Apps to Use

While the signage is good, having a digital helper is invaluable. * Baidu Maps or Amap: These are the dominant map apps in China. They provide real-time metro navigation, including transfer guides, estimated travel time, and even the best train car to board for a smooth exit. They require a Chinese phone number to function fully. * Metro Man: A great English-friendly app that has maps of subway systems for all major Chinese cities, including Guiyang. It works offline, which is a huge plus.

Navigating During Peak Hours

Avoid traveling between 7:30-9:00 AM and 5:00-7:00 PM on weekdays if you have large luggage. The trains can get crowded with commuters. If you must travel then, be prepared to maneuver your suitcase carefully and allow for a little extra time.

Luggage and Accessibility

The Guiyang metro is modern and therefore very accessible. Stations have elevators and escalators. The gap between the train and the platform is small. There are dedicated spaces inside each train car for large luggage and wheelchairs. Just be mindful of other passengers.

Combining with Other Transport: From Airport to Metro

If you are arriving at Guiyang Longdongbao International Airport (KWE), the metro is your cheapest bet to get to the South Station. You need to take a short taxi or bus ride to the Longdongbao Airport Metro Station on Line 2. Then, take Line 2 to Zhongxing Road Station, transfer to Line 1 (towards Xiaoshijiang), and continue to Guiyang South. The entire trip takes about 50-60 minutes.

The development of the Guiyang Metro has fundamentally changed the travel experience in the city. It has made a once-daunting transfer between transport hubs into a simple, affordable, and efficient journey. This efficiency gives you, the traveler, more time and energy to focus on what truly matters: exploring the breathtaking landscapes, rich cultures, and delicious flavors that make Guizhou one of China's most captivating and underrated destinations. So, step onto the train with confidence, and let your Guizhou adventure begin the moment you leave your hotel.
