Guiyang’s Bus Routes to the Most Instagrammable Spots

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Guiyang, the capital of Guizhou Province, is a hidden gem in Southwest China. Known for its lush landscapes, vibrant culture, and unique urban charm, it’s a paradise for travelers and Instagram enthusiasts alike. While taxis and ride-sharing apps are convenient, exploring Guiyang by bus is an affordable and authentic way to discover the city’s most photogenic spots. Here’s your ultimate guide to navigating Guiyang’s bus routes for the perfect Instagram feed.

Why Take the Bus in Guiyang?

Public transportation in Guiyang is efficient, eco-friendly, and budget-friendly. The city’s bus system covers almost every major attraction, making it easy to hop from one scenic location to another. Plus, riding the bus gives you a glimpse into local life—something you won’t get from the backseat of a taxi.

Top Instagrammable Spots Accessible by Bus

1. Qianling Mountain Park (Bus Routes: 1, 2, 10, 12, 23)

Qianling Mountain Park is a must-visit for nature lovers and photographers. The park is home to wild macaques, serene lakes, and lush greenery. The best photo spots include:
- Hongfu Temple: A stunning Buddhist temple nestled in the mountains.
- Qianling Lake: Perfect for reflections and tranquil shots.
- Viewing Platform: Offers panoramic views of Guiyang’s skyline.

Pro Tip: Take Bus 12 from downtown Guiyang for a direct route. Arrive early to avoid crowds and capture the misty morning vibes.

2. Jiaxiu Pavilion (Bus Routes: 15, 34, 46, 48, 52)

This iconic Ming Dynasty-era pavilion sits elegantly on the Nanming River. It’s especially magical at night when illuminated. Key photo ops:
- The Pavilion at Sunset: Golden hour lights up the traditional architecture.
- Nanming River Bridges: Framed shots with the water and city lights.
- Nearby Ancient Streets: Full of lanterns and local snacks for candid shots.

Bus Hack: Bus 15 stops right by the entrance, making it super convenient.

3. Qingyan Ancient Town (Bus Routes: 203, 210, K203)

Step back in time at Qingyan Ancient Town, a well-preserved 600-year-old village. The stone pathways, Ming-Qing architecture, and local crafts make it an Instagram dream. Don’t miss:
- Ancient City Walls: Dramatic backdrops for portraits.
- Rose Sugar Workshops: Colorful and aromatic—great for foodie content.
- Temple of the God of War: A hidden gem with intricate carvings.

How to Get There: Take Bus 203 from Guiyang Railway Station for a scenic 1-hour ride.

4. Huaxi Wetland Park (Bus Routes: 201, 202, 248, K248)

For a mix of nature and modernity, Huaxi Wetland Park delivers. The park features:
- Lotus Ponds: Blooming in summer, ideal for ethereal shots.
- Suspension Bridges: Perfect for aerial-style photography.
- Local Minority Performances: Capture vibrant cultural moments.

Best Route: Bus 201 runs frequently from the city center.

5. Guiyang Olympic Sports Center (Bus Routes: 58, 70, 208, 209)

Futuristic and sleek, this stadium is a favorite for urban explorers. Highlights:
- Architectural Symmetry: The dome and walkways are geometric heaven.
- Night Lights: The LED-lit facade is a showstopper.

Photography Tip: Use a wide-angle lens to emphasize the scale.

Lesser-Known Gems Worth the Detour

1. Tianhetan Scenic Area (Bus Routes: 211, 212, K212)

A quieter alternative to Qianling Mountain, Tianhetan offers waterfalls, caves, and dense forests. The Tianhetan Waterfall is a hidden masterpiece—especially after rainfall.

2. Baihua Lake (Bus Routes: 225, 231, K231)

A local favorite for lakeside picnics and sunset shots. Rent a paddleboat for unique water-level perspectives.

3. Guiyang Art Museum (Bus Routes: 7, 19, 37)

A modern art hub with rotating exhibitions. The building itself is a work of art—minimalist and photogenic.

Bus Travel Tips for Instagrammers

  1. Use Real-Time Apps: Apps like Baibu Maps or AMAP provide live bus tracking.
  2. Avoid Rush Hour: 7:30–9:00 AM and 5:00–7:00 PM can be crowded.
  3. Carry Small Change: Some buses only accept cash (¥2–5 per ride).
  4. Learn Basic Phrases: While not mandatory, knowing "Xièxiè" (Thank you) helps.

Final Shot

Guiyang’s bus routes are your ticket to an unforgettable visual journey. From ancient towns to futuristic landmarks, every corner of this city is a potential Instagram hit. So grab your camera, hop on a bus, and let Guiyang’s beauty unfold—one snapshot at a time.
