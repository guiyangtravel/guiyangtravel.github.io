One Day in Guiyang: A Business Traveler’s Quick Guide

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Guiyang, the capital of Guizhou Province, is a vibrant city where modernity meets tradition. For business travelers with limited time, squeezing in the best of Guiyang requires a well-planned itinerary. From savoring local delicacies to exploring cultural landmarks, here’s how to make the most of your 24 hours in this dynamic city.

Morning: Fuel Up and Explore

Start with a Guiyang Breakfast

Kick off your day with a hearty Guiyang-style breakfast. Head to Nanjing Road (Nanming District), where street vendors serve up steaming bowls of Chang Wang Mian (肠旺面), a local noodle dish featuring pork intestines, blood curd, and spicy broth. Pair it with a cup of Guiyang soy milk or Zao Bing (糟饼), a fermented rice pancake.

Visit Jiaxiu Pavilion

After breakfast, take a short taxi ride to Jiaxiu Pavilion (甲秀楼), Guiyang’s most iconic landmark. This Ming Dynasty-era pavilion sits elegantly on the Nanming River and offers a serene escape from the urban bustle. Stroll along the riverbank or climb to the top for panoramic city views—perfect for a quick photo op before your meetings.

Midday: Business and Local Flavors

Coffee Break at a Hidden Café

If you need a caffeine boost before your business engagements, stop by Postman Coffee (邮差咖啡) in an old industrial building. This trendy spot serves artisanal brews in a rustic-chic setting, ideal for a quick call or last-minute prep.

Lunch at Siwa Snack Street

For a true taste of Guiyang’s street food scene, head to Siwa Snack Street (丝娃娃小吃街). Try Siwawa (丝娃娃), a DIY wrap filled with fresh veggies and dipped in sour-spicy sauce, or Sour Soup Fish (酸汤鱼), a regional specialty. The lively atmosphere makes it a great spot for informal networking.

Afternoon: Culture and Quick Sightseeing

Qianling Park & Hongfu Temple

If you have an hour to spare, visit Qianling Park (黔灵公园). A short hike leads to Hongfu Temple (弘福寺), a Buddhist sanctuary surrounded by lush greenery. Keep an eye out for the park’s famous macaques—they’re playful but notorious for snatching snacks!

Drop by Guizhou Provincial Museum

For a dose of local history, the Guizhou Provincial Museum offers compact exhibits on ethnic minority cultures and geological wonders like the Zhijin Cave. The architecture alone—a modern take on traditional Dong minority designs—is worth a quick visit.

Evening: Unwind and Network

Dinner at Lao Kao Yi

Cap off your day at Lao Kao Yi (老烤一), a popular spot for Guizhou-style barbecue. Their grilled fish with chili and beer-braised duck are must-orders. The communal dining style makes it easy to bond with colleagues or clients over fiery flavors.

Night Views at Huaxi Park

If time allows, take a short drive to Huaxi Park (花溪公园). The illuminated bridges and lakeside walkways create a picturesque setting for a post-dinner stroll. Alternatively, sip a cocktail at Sky Lounge in the Kempinski Hotel for skyline views.

Logistics Tips

  • Transport: Didi (China’s Uber) is reliable; metro Line 1 connects key areas.
  • Language: Basic Mandarin helps, but hotel staff often speak English.
  • Etiquette: Business cards are exchanged with both hands; meals are social affairs.

Guiyang packs a punch for business travelers—efficient, flavorful, and full of surprises. With this guide, you’ll experience the city’s essence without missing a beat.
