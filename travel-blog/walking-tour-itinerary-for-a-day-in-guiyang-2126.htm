Walking Tour Itinerary for a Day in Guiyang

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Guiyang, the capital of Guizhou Province, is a city where modernity meets tradition, surrounded by lush mountains and vibrant culture. Whether you're a foodie, history buff, or nature lover, this one-day walking tour will help you experience the best of Guiyang.

Morning: Exploring the Heart of the City

Start at Jiaxiu Pavilion (甲秀楼)

Begin your day at Jiaxiu Pavilion, Guiyang’s most iconic landmark. This Ming Dynasty-era structure sits elegantly on the Nanming River, offering picturesque views. Stroll across the Fuyu Bridge and admire the traditional Chinese architecture. Early mornings are ideal for avoiding crowds and capturing stunning photos.

Pro Tip: Visit the nearby Cuihu Park for a serene walk among willow trees and lotus ponds.

Breakfast at Local Eateries

Head to Qianling Mountain Road (黔灵山路) for an authentic Guiyang breakfast. Try:
- Changwang Noodles (肠旺面): A spicy noodle dish with pork blood and intestines.
- Siwawa (丝娃娃): A DIY-style wrap filled with fresh vegetables and tangy sauce.

Midday: Cultural and Culinary Delights

Qianling Mountain Park (黔灵山公园)

After breakfast, take a short taxi ride (or a 20-minute walk) to Qianling Mountain Park, a sprawling green oasis. Highlights include:
- Hongfu Temple (弘福寺): A serene Buddhist temple nestled in the hills.
- Wild Monkey Zone: Observe (but don’t feed!) the park’s playful macaques.
- Zhao Mountain (照山): Climb for panoramic city views.

Pro Tip: Wear comfortable shoes—the park involves some uphill walking.

Lunch at Lao Kaili Sour Fish Soup (老凯里酸汤鱼)

No visit to Guiyang is complete without trying suan tang yu (酸汤鱼), a tangy fish stew made with fermented tomatoes. The Lao Kaili chain is a local favorite. Pair it with zhua fan (抓饭), a sticky rice dish.

Afternoon: Markets and Hidden Gems

Guiyang’s Antique Market (阳明路古玩市场)

For a taste of local craftsmanship, explore the antique market on Yangming Road. Hunt for:
- Minority embroidery (Miao or Dong ethnic styles).
- Hand-carved wooden souvenirs.
- Vintage Mao-era memorabilia.

Qianxi Snack Street (青岩小吃街)

If you’re still peckish, head to Qianxi Snack Street near Qingyan Ancient Town (a short taxi ride away). Must-tries:
- Qingyan Tofu (青岩豆腐): Fried tofu with chili dip.
- Rose Sugar Ice Powder (玫瑰糖冰粉): A refreshing dessert.

Evening: Sunset and Nightlife

Nanming River Walk

As the sun sets, take a leisurely walk along the Nanming River. The city lights reflecting on the water create a magical atmosphere. Stop by Shanshui Coffee for a locally brewed cup.

Bar Street (文昌阁酒吧街)

End your day at Bar Street near Wenchang Pavilion. Popular spots include:
- Trio Bar: Live music and craft cocktails.
- Lingnan Space: A rooftop bar with city views.

Pro Tip: Try maotai cocktails—a twist on Guizhou’s famous liquor.

Bonus: If You Have Extra Time

  • Huaxi Park (花溪公园): A scenic retreat with bamboo forests and waterfalls (30-minute drive).
  • Guiyang Olympic Center: For architecture enthusiasts.

This itinerary balances history, food, and nature—perfect for a memorable day in Guiyang!
