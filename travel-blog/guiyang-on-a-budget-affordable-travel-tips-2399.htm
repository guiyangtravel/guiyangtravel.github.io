Guiyang on a Budget: Affordable Travel Tips

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Guiyang, the capital of Guizhou Province, is a hidden gem in Southwest China. Known for its lush landscapes, ethnic diversity, and mouthwatering cuisine, it’s a fantastic destination for budget-conscious travelers. Whether you're exploring vibrant night markets, hiking through karst mountains, or sipping on local tea, Guiyang offers plenty of affordable adventures. Here’s how to experience the best of Guiyang without breaking the bank.

Getting There and Around

Cheap Flights and Trains

Guiyang is well-connected by air and rail. Budget airlines like Spring Airlines and Lucky Air often offer discounted fares from major Chinese cities. If you prefer trains, high-speed rail (HSR) from Chengdu, Chongqing, or Kunming is both fast and economical.

Public Transportation

Guiyang’s metro system is expanding, with Line 1 and Line 2 covering key areas. A single ride costs as little as ¥2-¥5. Buses are even cheaper, though less English-friendly. For short distances, shared bikes (like Meituan or HelloBike) are a convenient and eco-friendly option.

Budget-Friendly Accommodation

Hostels and Guesthouses

Guiyang has a growing number of backpacker-friendly hostels. Areas near Jiaxiu Pavilion or Qianling Park are ideal for budget stays. Look for spots like Loft Youth Hostel or Guiyang Dream Inn, where beds start at ¥30-¥50 per night.

Homestays in Ethnic Villages

For a unique experience, consider a homestay in nearby Miao or Dong villages. Prices are low (around ¥80-¥150 per night), and you’ll get an authentic taste of local culture.

Eating Like a Local

Street Food Heaven

Guiyang’s street food is legendary—and dirt cheap. Don’t miss:
- Siwawa: A spicy rice-paper wrap stuffed with veggies (¥5-¥10).
- Changwang Noodles: A hearty bowl of noodles with braised pork (¥8-¥12).
- Grilled Fish with Sour Soup: A Guizhou specialty, best shared (¥30-¥50 for two).

Head to Erqi Road Night Market or Zhonghua North Road for the best bites.

Affordable Local Restaurants

Skip touristy spots and eat where locals do. Lao Kaili Sour Soup Fish and Yang’s Love Tofu offer delicious meals for under ¥20.

Free and Low-Cost Attractions

Qianling Park

This sprawling park is free to enter and home to wild macaques. Hike up Hongfu Temple for panoramic city views (¥5 entry to the temple).

Jiaxiu Pavilion

Guiyang’s iconic landmark costs just ¥10 to visit. The surrounding riverside area is perfect for a leisurely stroll.

Ethnic Minority Villages

Many villages near Guiyang, like Qingyan Ancient Town (¥10 entry) or Kaili’s Xijiang Miao Village (¥100, but free on certain days), offer a glimpse into traditional life without the high price tag.

Budget Shopping

Local Markets

For souvenirs, skip the malls and hit Flower and Bird Market or Shanlin Market. Handmade Miao silver jewelry, batik fabrics, and local teas make great gifts at bargain prices.

Supermarket Bargains

Chain stores like Wal-Mart or RT-Mart sell packaged local snacks (like Lao Gan Ma chili crisps) for a fraction of tourist-shop prices.

Money-Saving Tips

  1. Travel Off-Season: Avoid holidays like Golden Week. Spring and autumn offer pleasant weather and lower prices.
  2. Use Apps: Meituan and Dianping offer discounts on food and activities.
  3. Group Discounts: Many attractions offer cheaper rates for groups. Team up with fellow travelers!

Guiyang proves that adventure doesn’t have to be expensive. With these tips, you can soak up the city’s culture, flavors, and scenery—all while keeping your wallet happy.
