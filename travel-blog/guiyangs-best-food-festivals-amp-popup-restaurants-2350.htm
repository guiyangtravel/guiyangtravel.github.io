Guiyang’s Best Food Festivals & Pop-Up Restaurants

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Guiyang, the vibrant capital of Guizhou Province, is a hidden gem for food lovers. Known for its bold flavors, unique ingredients, and rich culinary traditions, the city offers an unforgettable gastronomic experience. From lively food festivals to trendy pop-up restaurants, Guiyang’s food scene is a must-explore for any traveler. Here’s your ultimate guide to the best food events and temporary dining spots in the city.

The Must-Attend Food Festivals in Guiyang

Guiyang International Beer & Food Festival

Every summer, the Guiyang International Beer & Food Festival takes over the city with a lively celebration of global and local cuisines. Held in the expansive Guiyang Olympic Sports Center, this festival features:
- Craft beer tastings from local and international breweries
- Street food stalls serving Guizhou specialties like Sour Fish Soup (酸汤鱼) and Siwawa (丝娃娃)
- Live music and performances to keep the energy high

This is the perfect place to mingle with locals while indulging in spicy, sour, and savory dishes that define Guizhou’s food culture.

Guiyang Spicy Hot Pot Festival

For those who love heat, the Guiyang Spicy Hot Pot Festival is a fiery delight. Held annually in autumn, this festival showcases:
- Over 50 hot pot varieties, from the classic Mala (麻辣) to herbal-infused broths
- Interactive cooking demos by top chefs
- Chili-eating contests for the brave

The festival is a testament to Guiyang’s love for spice, and it’s a fantastic way to experience the city’s communal dining culture.

Guizhou Minority Cuisine Fair

Guiyang is a melting pot of ethnic cultures, and the Guizhou Minority Cuisine Fair highlights the culinary traditions of the Miao, Dong, and Bouyei people. Visitors can:
- Taste rare dishes like Changwang Fish (长旺鱼) and Bamboo Tube Rice (竹筒饭)
- Watch traditional cooking methods, such as open-fire grilling and fermentation techniques
- Purchase handmade condiments like Fermented Chili Paste (糟辣椒)

This festival is a deep dive into the region’s heritage and a must for foodies seeking authentic flavors.

Trendy Pop-Up Restaurants You Can’t Miss

The Night Market Pop-Ups

Guiyang’s night markets are legendary, and seasonal pop-ups add an extra layer of excitement. Some standout options include:

Zhenghong Street Night Bazaar

  • Specialty: Grilled River Snails (烤田螺) and Stinky Tofu (臭豆腐)
  • Why visit? The smoky, aromatic stalls offer a true local experience.

Qianling Mountain Food Park

  • Specialty: Barbecue Skewers (烧烤串) with Guizhou’s signature zhe’ergen (折耳根) dipping sauce
  • Why visit? The mountain backdrop makes for a scenic dining spot.

Chef Collaborations at The Temporary Kitchen

A rotating pop-up concept, The Temporary Kitchen invites guest chefs to reinvent Guizhou classics. Recent highlights include:
- A fusion Sour Soup with Japanese ramen noodles
- Deconstructed Siwawa with molecular gastronomy techniques

These limited-time menus attract food enthusiasts looking for innovative twists on tradition.

Rooftop Dining at Cloud Nine

During summer, Cloud Nine transforms into an exclusive rooftop pop-up with:
- A curated tasting menu featuring Guiyang’s best seasonal ingredients
- Cocktails infused with local Maotai liquor
- Panoramic city views

Reservations are a must, as this pop-up only seats 20 guests per night.

Insider Tips for Food Travelers

  • Timing is key: Many festivals peak on weekends, so plan accordingly.
  • Bring cash: Smaller vendors may not accept digital payments.
  • Ask locals for recommendations: Hidden gems often aren’t listed online.

Guiyang’s food scene is dynamic, diverse, and deeply rooted in culture. Whether you’re slurping noodles at a festival or savoring a pop-up chef’s creation, every bite tells a story.
