Guiyang’s Best Spots for a Winter Wonderland Visit

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Winter in Guiyang is a magical time when the city transforms into a serene wonderland, offering a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural charm, and festive vibes. While many travelers flock to northern China for snow-covered landscapes, Guiyang’s milder climate and stunning scenery make it an underrated gem for winter exploration. From misty mountains to vibrant night markets, here are the must-visit spots for an unforgettable winter getaway.

Embrace the Scenic Beauty of Qianling Park

A Winter Stroll Through Lush Greenery

Unlike the harsh winters up north, Guiyang’s climate remains relatively mild, allowing Qianling Park to retain much of its lush greenery even in colder months. The park’s towering trees, tranquil lakes, and winding paths create a peaceful retreat. Don’t miss the chance to spot the park’s resident macaques, who are especially active during winter as they forage for food.

The Misty Magic of Hongfu Temple

Nestled within Qianling Park, Hongfu Temple is one of Guiyang’s most revered Buddhist sites. In winter, the temple’s golden rooftops contrast beautifully with the misty surroundings, creating an ethereal atmosphere. Visitors can light incense, meditate, or simply soak in the spiritual ambiance while enjoying panoramic views of the city.

Discover the Enchanting Qingyan Ancient Town

Step Back in Time

Just a short drive from Guiyang, Qingyan Ancient Town is a well-preserved Ming Dynasty relic that feels like a scene from a historical drama. Winter adds an extra layer of charm, with fewer crowds and a cozy, nostalgic vibe. Wander through cobblestone streets lined with traditional stone houses, and stop by local shops selling handmade crafts and winter snacks like la rou (cured meat) and sticky rice cakes.

Warm Up with Local Delicacies

Winter is the perfect time to indulge in Guizhou’s hearty cuisine. Qingyan’s food stalls offer steaming bowls of sour fish soup and spicy hot pot, guaranteed to warm you up. Don’t forget to try Changwang Noodles, a local favorite known for its rich broth and chewy texture.

Experience the Thrill of Skiing at Guiyang’s Snow Parks

Fun for All Ages at Nanjiang Ski Resort

While Guiyang doesn’t get heavy snowfall, nearby Nanjiang Ski Resort offers artificial slopes perfect for beginners and families. The resort features tubing lanes, sledding areas, and even snowboarding lessons. It’s a fantastic way to enjoy winter sports without braving sub-zero temperatures.

Night Skiing Under the Stars

For a unique experience, visit the resort after sunset. The illuminated slopes create a magical setting, and the cooler evening temperatures make the snow conditions ideal for skiing.

Unwind at the Hot Springs of Xifeng

Soak Away the Winter Chill

After days of exploring, nothing beats a relaxing dip in Xifeng’s geothermal hot springs. Surrounded by forested hills, these natural pools are rich in minerals and said to have healing properties. Choose from private tubs or communal pools, and let the warm waters melt away any lingering cold.

A Romantic Winter Escape

Many hot spring resorts in Xifeng offer private villas with outdoor baths, making it a perfect spot for couples. Imagine soaking under the open sky while sipping on a warm cup of local maotai tea—pure bliss!

Explore the Vibrant Nightlife of Guiyang’s City Center

Indulge in Street Food at Zhonghua Road

Guiyang’s food scene comes alive in winter, and Zhonghua Road is the place to be. From grilled skewers to siwawa (a spicy cold noodle dish), the night market offers endless options for foodies. The aroma of sizzling meats and spices fills the air, creating a lively and inviting atmosphere.

Sip on Craft Cocktails in Bar Street

For those who prefer a more upscale evening, head to Bar Street near Nanming River. Trendy lounges and speakeasies serve creative cocktails infused with local ingredients like goji berries and baijiu. It’s a great way to experience Guiyang’s modern side after a day of winter adventures.

Capture the Beauty of Huaxi Wetland Park

A Photographer’s Paradise

Winter mornings at Huaxi Wetland Park are nothing short of breathtaking. The mist rising over the lake, frost-kissed reeds, and migratory birds create a dreamy landscape. Bring your camera and capture the serene beauty of this hidden gem.

Birdwatching and Eco-Tours

The park is a haven for bird enthusiasts, with species like egrets and kingfishers frequenting the area. Guided eco-tours are available for those who want to learn more about the local ecosystem.

Join the Festive Celebrations at Jiaxiu Pavilion

Lantern Displays and Cultural Performances

During winter, Jiaxiu Pavilion—Guiyang’s iconic landmark—hosts dazzling lantern festivals and traditional performances. The pavilion, lit up against the night sky, reflects beautifully on the Nanming River, creating a postcard-perfect scene.

New Year’s Eve Countdown

If you’re visiting around December, don’t miss the lively New Year’s Eve celebrations. Fireworks, live music, and street performances turn the area into a festive hub, offering a memorable way to ring in the new year.

Stay Cozy in Guiyang’s Boutique Hotels

Luxury with a Local Touch

Guiyang boasts several boutique hotels that blend modern comfort with Guizhou’s ethnic aesthetics. Properties like The Lodge by Yangming offer heated floors, fireplaces, and locally inspired décor—perfect for a snug winter stay.

Tea Lounges and Winter Afternoon Teas

Many hotels feature cozy tea lounges where guests can unwind with a cup of Duyun Maojian (a premium green tea) and homemade pastries. It’s the ideal way to spend a chilly afternoon.

Venture Off the Beaten Path to Gaopo Miao Village

Immerse Yourself in Miao Culture

For a deeper cultural experience, visit Gaopo Miao Village, where the Miao ethnic group preserves centuries-old traditions. Winter is a great time to witness their vibrant festivals, featuring intricate silver jewelry, lusheng (bamboo pipe) music, and lively dances.

Handicraft Workshops

Join a workshop to learn traditional crafts like batik dyeing or embroidery. These make for unique souvenirs and a deeper connection to Guizhou’s rich heritage.

Final Tips for Your Winter Trip

  • Pack Smart: While Guiyang’s winters are mild, layers are key—mornings can be chilly, but afternoons warm up.
  • Try Seasonal Treats: Look for tangyuan (sweet glutinous rice balls) and hot ginger tea at street stalls.
  • Book Early: Winter is peak travel season for domestic tourists, so secure accommodations and tickets in advance.

Guiyang’s winter charm lies in its ability to offer both adventure and relaxation. Whether you’re seeking snowy fun, cultural immersion, or simply a peaceful escape, this city delivers it all with warmth and hospitality.
