Guiyang’s Best Tea and Local Product Souvenir Shops

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Nestled in the heart of China’s southwestern Guizhou Province, Guiyang is a city where tradition meets modernity. Known for its lush landscapes, vibrant ethnic culture, and—most importantly—its exquisite teas, Guiyang is a paradise for travelers seeking authentic local souvenirs. Whether you’re a tea connoisseur or simply looking for unique gifts, the city’s tea shops and local product stores offer an unforgettable shopping experience.

The Tea Culture of Guiyang

Guiyang’s tea culture is deeply rooted in the region’s history and geography. The surrounding mountains provide the perfect climate for growing some of China’s most prized teas, including Meitan Cuiya (湄潭翠芽) and Duyun Maojian (都匀毛尖). These teas are celebrated for their delicate flavors, floral aromas, and health benefits.

Top Tea Shops in Guiyang

1. Guiyang Tea Exchange (贵阳茶文化交流中心)

Located in the city center, this shop is a haven for tea lovers. Here, you’ll find a curated selection of premium loose-leaf teas, from fragrant green teas to aged pu’er. The staff are knowledgeable and often host tea-tasting sessions, making it a great place to learn about Guizhou’s tea traditions.

2. Qianchun Tea House (黔春茶庄)

A favorite among locals, Qianchun Tea House specializes in Duyun Maojian, a green tea known for its tender leaves and refreshing taste. The shop’s rustic charm and friendly atmosphere make it a must-visit.

3. Meitan Tea Emporium (湄潭茶叶专卖店)

If you’re searching for Meitan Cuiya, this is the place to go. The emporium sources directly from Meitan County, ensuring the freshest and highest-quality tea leaves. They also offer beautifully packaged gift sets, perfect for souvenirs.

Beyond Tea: Unique Local Products

While tea is a highlight, Guiyang’s souvenir shops also showcase other regional specialties. From handicrafts to spicy condiments, here’s what to look for:

Handicrafts and Ethnic Treasures

1. Miao and Dong Embroidery

The Miao and Dong ethnic groups are renowned for their intricate embroidery and silver jewelry. Shops like Guizhou Ethnic Art Gallery (贵州民族艺术馆) sell stunning hand-stitched textiles, perfect for wall hangings or fashion accessories.

2. Batik (La Ran 蜡染)

A traditional craft of the Miao people, batik involves dyeing cloth with indigo and creating patterns using wax. Guiyang Batik Workshop offers workshops where you can try your hand at this ancient art.

Food and Spices

1. Chili Products

Guiyang is famous for its spicy cuisine, and no visit is complete without picking up some Lao Gan Ma (老干妈) chili crisp or Guizhou fermented chili paste. These condiments pack a punch and make great gifts for foodies.

2. Sour Fish Soup Base (酸汤鱼调料)

A staple of Guizhou cuisine, this tangy, spicy soup base is a must-try. Many shops sell pre-packaged mixes, allowing you to recreate the flavors at home.

Where to Shop: Guiyang’s Best Souvenir Stores

1. Hongfu Tea Market (鸿福茶城)

This bustling market is the largest tea hub in Guiyang, with dozens of vendors selling everything from budget-friendly teas to rare aged pu’er. Bargaining is common, so don’t hesitate to negotiate.

2. Qianling Mountain Local Products Store (黔灵山特产店)

Located near Qianling Park, this store is a one-stop shop for Guizhou specialties, including teas, snacks, and handicrafts. The convenient location makes it ideal for last-minute shopping.

3. Wanda Plaza Souvenir Corner

For a more modern shopping experience, head to Wanda Plaza. Here, you’ll find upscale gift shops offering beautifully packaged local products, ideal for travelers with limited time.

Tips for Buying Souvenirs in Guiyang

  • Ask for samples: Many tea shops offer free tastings. Don’t be shy—try before you buy!
  • Check for authenticity: Look for shops with certifications or direct ties to tea-producing regions.
  • Pack carefully: If you’re flying, consider vacuum-sealed teas to preserve freshness.

Guiyang’s tea and souvenir shops are more than just retail spaces—they’re gateways to the region’s rich culture and traditions. Whether you’re sipping a cup of Duyun Maojian or admiring a piece of Miao embroidery, every purchase tells a story.
