Best Tea Houses in Guiyang for a Peaceful Afternoon

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Guiyang, the capital of Guizhou Province, is a city where modernity meets tradition. Nestled among its bustling streets and towering skyscrapers are serene tea houses that offer a perfect escape for those seeking tranquility. Whether you're a tea connoisseur or simply looking for a quiet spot to unwind, Guiyang’s tea houses provide an authentic experience steeped in local culture. Here’s a curated list of the best tea houses in Guiyang for a peaceful afternoon.

The Charm of Guiyang’s Tea Culture

Guiyang’s tea culture is deeply rooted in the region’s history. The city is surrounded by lush mountains and fertile land, making it an ideal place for tea cultivation. Local favorites include Maojian and Duyun Maojian, both known for their delicate flavors and aromatic profiles.

Why Visit a Tea House in Guiyang?

  • Escape the hustle and bustle: The city’s tea houses are oases of calm.
  • Authentic tea experiences: Many venues serve locally sourced teas with traditional brewing methods.
  • Cultural immersion: Tea houses often feature Chinese décor, calligraphy, and live guqin (a traditional Chinese instrument) performances.

Top Tea Houses in Guiyang

1. Chaxi Teahouse (茶溪茶舍)

Tucked away in a quiet alley near Qianling Park, Chaxi Teahouse is a hidden gem. The interior is adorned with wooden furniture, bamboo plants, and soft lighting, creating a Zen-like atmosphere.

What to Try:

  • Duyun Maojian: A premium green tea with a fresh, slightly sweet taste.
  • Hand-brewed Pu’er: Aged tea with deep, earthy notes.

Why Visit? The teahouse offers private rooms for small groups, making it perfect for intimate gatherings.

2. Yunwu Teahouse (云雾茶馆)

Located near Jiaxiu Tower, Yunwu Teahouse boasts panoramic views of the Nanming River. The open-air terrace is a highlight, especially during sunset.

What to Try:

  • Guiyang Yunwu Tea: A high-mountain tea with a floral aroma.
  • Osmanthus Oolong: A fragrant blend of oolong tea and osmanthus flowers.

Why Visit? The combination of river views and expertly brewed tea makes this spot unforgettable.

3. Mingyue Teahouse (明月茶馆)

A favorite among locals, Mingyue Teahouse is known for its minimalist design and focus on tea purity. The staff are knowledgeable and happy to guide beginners through a tea-tasting session.

What to Try:

  • Silver Needle White Tea: A delicate, lightly fermented tea.
  • Jasmine Pearl Tea: Hand-rolled green tea scented with jasmine blossoms.

Why Visit? The teahouse occasionally hosts tea ceremonies and cultural workshops.

4. Shanlin Xuan (山林轩)

For those who prefer a more rustic setting, Shanlin Xuan is nestled in the hills surrounding Guiyang. The journey to this teahouse is part of the experience, with winding paths leading through bamboo forests.

What to Try:

  • Wild Mountain Black Tea: A robust tea with hints of honey.
  • Ginger-infused Tea: A warming blend perfect for cooler days.

Why Visit? The secluded location makes it ideal for meditation and reflection.

5. Qinglian Teahouse (青莲茶馆)

Qinglian Teahouse is a modern twist on traditional tea culture. The sleek interior features contemporary art alongside antique tea sets.

What to Try:

  • Cold Brew Longjing: A refreshing take on the classic green tea.
  • Rose Black Tea: A floral, slightly sweet blend.

Why Visit? The fusion of old and new makes this teahouse stand out.

Tips for Enjoying Tea in Guiyang

Best Time to Visit

Late afternoon (3 PM - 6 PM) is ideal, as most tea houses are less crowded.

Tea Etiquette

  • Smell first: Appreciate the aroma before sipping.
  • Hold the cup properly: Use both hands as a sign of respect.
  • Sip slowly: Enjoy the tea in small, mindful sips.

Pairing Tea with Local Snacks

Many tea houses serve traditional Guizhou snacks like Laobing (crispy pancakes) or Zhusun Gao (bamboo shoot cakes).

Final Thoughts

Guiyang’s tea houses are more than just places to drink tea—they are sanctuaries where time slows down. Whether you prefer a riverside terrace or a secluded mountain retreat, each teahouse offers a unique way to connect with the city’s culture. So next time you’re in Guiyang, take a break from sightseeing and indulge in the simple pleasure of a perfectly brewed cup of tea.
