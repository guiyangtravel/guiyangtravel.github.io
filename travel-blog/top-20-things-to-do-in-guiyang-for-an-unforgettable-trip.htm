Top 20 Things to Do in Guiyang for an Unforgettable Trip

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Nestled among rolling green hills and surrounded by lush forests, Guiyang, the capital of Guizhou province, is a city that often surprises travelers with its vibrant culture, stunning natural scenery, and unique culinary delights. As a hub of minority cultures and a gateway to some of China's most breathtaking landscapes, Guiyang offers an array of experiences that cater to nature lovers, food enthusiasts, and culture seekers alike. This city, with its perpetually spring-like climate, invites you to explore its many wonders. Here are the top 20 things to do for a truly memorable journey.

Immerse Yourself in Natural Wonders

Guiyang is renowned for its pristine environment and proximity to some of the most spectacular natural attractions in China. The air is fresh, the hills are emerald, and the water is a stunning shade of blue.

Explore the Magical Qianling Mountain Park

A green lung in the heart of the city, Qianling Mountain Park is a must-visit for anyone seeking a peaceful retreat. The park is home to a troop of mischievous macaque monkeys, who are often the main attraction for visitors. Hike up the hill to the Hongfu Temple, a significant Buddhist site with a history spanning over 300 years. The climb is rewarded with serene temple halls, the chance to listen to monks chanting, and panoramic views of Guiyang from the top. It’s a perfect blend of nature, spirituality, and light exercise.

Get Lost in the Serenity of Huaxi Park

Huaxi Park is not just a park; it's a sprawling landscape of poetic beauty. Centered around the meandering Huaxi River, the park features traditional pavilions, arched stone bridges, and carefully manicured gardens. You can take a leisurely boat ride on the calm waters, stroll along the paths lined with willow trees, and feel as if you've stepped into a classical Chinese painting. It’s an ideal spot for a relaxing afternoon, away from the urban hustle.

Witness the Geological Marvel of Tianhe Pond

Located within the Nanjiang Grand Canyon, Tianhe Pond is a natural wonder that seems almost otherworldly. The pond is famous for its incredibly clear, turquoise-blue water, which is filtered through the karst limestone formations. The surrounding cliffs and lush vegetation create a dramatic backdrop. It’s a fantastic destination for hiking and photography, offering a sense of discovery and awe at the power of nature.

Cruise on the Beautiful Hongfeng Lake

Just a short drive from the city center, Hongfeng Lake is a vast, man-made reservoir surrounded by red-leaved trees (which give the lake its name). A cruise on the lake is a tranquil experience, offering stunning views of the water set against a backdrop of forested hills and strange karst formations. The area is also dotted with minority villages, adding a cultural dimension to the natural beauty.

Dive into the Rich Cultural Tapestry

Guiyang is the cultural heart of Guizhou, a province home to dozens of ethnic minority groups, including the Miao and Bouyei. Their vibrant traditions, colorful festivals, and unique architecture are integral to the city's identity.

Wander Through Qingyan Ancient Town

Step back in time with a visit to Qingyan Ancient Town, a well-preserved Ming and Qing dynasty town located in the southern suburbs of Guiyang. The town's stone-paved streets, ancient city walls, and historic courtyards tell stories of a bygone era. Don't miss trying the local snacks here, such as stinky tofu and various rice cakes. It’s a living museum where you can feel the history in the very stones beneath your feet.

Experience the Grandeur of the Jiaxiu Pavilion

Standing proudly on the Fuyu Bridge over the Nanming River, the Jiaxiu Pavilion is the iconic symbol of Guiyang. This three-story, 20-meter tall wooden pavilion dates back to the Ming Dynasty. Climb to the top for a fantastic view of the river and the modern city skyline. The pavilion is beautifully illuminated at night, creating a breathtaking reflection on the water—a perfect photo opportunity.

Discover Minority Culture at the Guizhou Provincial Museum

To truly understand the region, a visit to the Guizhou Provincial Museum is essential. Its exhibits provide a comprehensive overview of the history, culture, and traditions of the various ethnic groups in Guizhou. You'll see intricate silver jewelry, elaborate traditional costumes, and learn about unique customs like the Miao people's Lusheng wind instrument and their grand festival celebrations.

Be Mesmerized by the Miao and Dong Minority Performances

Seek out a cultural performance for an unforgettable evening. These shows are a feast for the senses, featuring spectacular displays of traditional music, dance, and costumes. The sound of the Lusheng, the synchronized movements of the dancers, and the dazzling silver headdresses of the Miao people are a powerful and moving representation of a living cultural heritage.

Embark on a Culinary Adventure

Guiyang's food scene is a highlight of any trip, known for its bold, sour, and spicy flavors. The local cuisine is a unique branch of Chinese food that will excite adventurous eaters.

Conquer the Sour and Spicy Flavors at a Local Eatery

Guiyang cuisine is famous for its "suan tang" (sour soup). Dive into a hot pot filled with this tangy, spicy broth, where you can cook a variety of fresh vegetables, meats, and fish. It's a communal and deeply satisfying dining experience. Another must-try is "Si Wa Wa," a local snack made from a thin rice flour wrapper filled with various ingredients and served with a spicy sauce.

Stroll and Snack at Erqi Road Snack Street

For the ultimate foodie experience, head to Erqi Road Snack Street as evening falls. This bustling pedestrian street comes alive with hundreds of food stalls. It's the perfect place to sample a wide variety of Guiyang and Guizhou specialties. Be sure to try "Chang Wang Mian," a comforting noodle dish, "Grilled Fish with Zingiber," and the unique "Zhu’er Gen," a root vegetable salad.

Sip on the Local Favorite: Maotai

While the town of Maotai is a few hours away, its world-famous spirit is a cornerstone of local culture. In Guiyang, you can visit specialty shops to see the various grades of this potent baijiu (white liquor) or, for the brave, sample a small glass at a local restaurant. It's a strong, aromatic experience that is deeply woven into the social and business fabric of the region.

Venture on Day Trips to Spectacular Sites

Some of China's most incredible landscapes are within a few hours' drive of Guiyang, making the city a perfect base for exploration.

Be Awed by the Huangguoshu Waterfall

No trip to Guiyang is complete without a day trip to Huangguoshu Waterfall, the largest waterfall in Asia. Feel the mist on your face as you witness the mighty cascade of water plunging 77.8 meters into the Rhinoceros Pool below. Walk behind the waterfall through the Water-Curtain Cave for a truly unique and powerful perspective. The sheer scale and thunderous sound are humbling and unforgettable.

Explore the Otherworldly Zhijin Cave

Venture to Zhijin Cave, one of the most spectacular karst caves in China. Often called a "underground treasure palace," the cave system is vast, featuring enormous chambers, intricate stalactites and stalagmites, and underground rivers. The colorful lighting illuminates the fantastic shapes, creating a surreal and magical atmosphere that feels like a journey to the center of the earth.

Visit a Traditional Miao Village in Southeast Guizhou

Take a longer day trip to the southeastern part of the province to visit authentic Miao villages like Xijiang Qianhu Miao Village, the largest Miao settlement in the world. Walking among the stilted wooden houses, watching locals go about their daily lives, and perhaps even witnessing a traditional song and dance performance offers a profound and authentic cultural immersion.

Enjoy Modern City Life and Relaxation

Beyond the ancient and the natural, Guiyang is a modern, developing city with its own unique urban charm.

Shop for Souvenirs at the Dazzling Grand Theater Area

The area around the Guizhou Grand Theater, with its unique architectural design inspired by the Zun, an ancient wine vessel, is a great place for an evening stroll. You can find shops selling local handicrafts, silver jewelry, and batik textiles—perfect for taking a piece of Guizhou's culture home with you.

Unwind in the Hot Springs of Xifeng

After days of hiking and exploring, treat yourself to a relaxing soak in the Xifeng Hot Springs, located just north of Guiyang. The mineral-rich waters are believed to have therapeutic properties and provide a perfect way to soothe tired muscles in a beautiful, natural setting.

Experience the Bustling Guiyang City Center

Spend some time simply wandering through the city's commercial centers, like the area around Dashizi. Here, you can experience the modern pulse of Guiyang, with its shopping malls, cafes, and the energetic flow of daily life. It's a great way to see how tradition and modernity coexist in this dynamic city.

Catch a Panoramic View from the Guiyang Olympic Sports Center

For a modern architectural sight, head to the Guiyang Olympic Sports Center. Its futuristic design is impressive, and the surrounding park is a popular spot for locals to relax and exercise. It offers a different perspective on the city's ambitions and its love for public green spaces.

Enjoy a Leisurely Afternoon at the Guiyang Botanical Garden

For a more curated natural experience, the Guiyang Botanical Garden is a wonderful place to explore. It houses a vast collection of plants, including a large greenhouse with tropical species. It's a peaceful, educational, and beautiful escape, perfect for a family outing or a quiet walk.

From the thunder of Asia's greatest waterfall to the quiet whisper of ancient temple bells, from the explosive flavors of its cuisine to the gentle hospitality of its people, Guiyang is a destination that captures the heart. This list of twenty experiences only scratches the surface of what this remarkable city and its surroundings have to offer. Each activity promises to add a unique and unforgettable layer to your travel story, making your trip to Guiyang one you will cherish for years to come.
