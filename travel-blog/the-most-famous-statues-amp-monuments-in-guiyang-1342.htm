The Most Famous Statues & Monuments in Guiyang

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Guiyang, the capital of Guizhou Province, is a city rich in culture, history, and natural beauty. While it may not be as globally renowned as Beijing or Shanghai, Guiyang boasts an impressive collection of statues and monuments that reflect its unique heritage and modern development. Whether you're a history buff, an art enthusiast, or simply a curious traveler, these landmarks offer fascinating insights into the city’s identity.

The Iconic Statue of Yang Ming

One of the most revered figures in Guiyang’s history is Yang Ming, a philosopher and statesman from the Ming Dynasty. His statue stands proudly in Yangming Square, a central gathering place for locals and tourists alike.

The Legacy of Yang Ming

Yang Ming was a key figure in Neo-Confucianism, and his teachings continue to influence Chinese philosophy. The statue captures his contemplative demeanor, symbolizing wisdom and introspection. Visitors often pause here to reflect, making it not just a monument but a spiritual landmark.

Surrounding Attractions

The square itself is a hub of activity, with street performers, food vendors, and seasonal festivals. Nearby, you’ll find the Guiyang Art Museum, which frequently hosts exhibitions on traditional and contemporary Chinese art.

The Majestic Jiaxiu Tower

While not a statue, Jiaxiu Tower (First Scholar’s Tower) is an unmissable monument in Guiyang. Perched on the Fuyu Bridge over the Nanming River, this Ming Dynasty-era pavilion is a symbol of academic excellence.

Historical Significance

Built in 1598, the tower was originally intended to inspire scholars preparing for imperial examinations. Its name translates to "First Scholar," reflecting its connection to education and success.

Best Time to Visit

At night, the tower is illuminated, casting a golden glow over the river. The surrounding Jiaxiu Square often features cultural performances, making it a perfect spot for an evening stroll.

The Modern Marvel: Guiyang Olympic Sports Center

For a taste of contemporary Guiyang, head to the Guiyang Olympic Sports Center, where a striking modern monument celebrates athleticism and progress.

Architectural Highlights

The center’s design is futuristic, with sweeping curves and LED lighting that changes colors at night. The Champion’s Statue at the entrance depicts a triumphant athlete, symbolizing perseverance and achievement.

Events & Activities

The venue hosts major sports events, concerts, and exhibitions. Even if there’s no event scheduled, the surrounding park is ideal for jogging or picnicking.

The Enigmatic Statue of the Miao Hero

Guiyang is the gateway to Guizhou’s ethnic minority regions, and the Statue of the Miao Hero pays tribute to the Miao people’s resilience.

Cultural Importance

The Miao are one of China’s largest ethnic groups, known for their vibrant festivals and intricate silver jewelry. The statue depicts a warrior in traditional attire, holding a symbolic weapon.

Nearby Experiences

Just a short trip from the city, you can visit Qingyan Ancient Town, where Miao culture thrives. Don’t miss the chance to try sour fish soup, a local delicacy.

The Whimsical "Giant Panda" Sculpture

In recent years, Guiyang has embraced playful public art, and the Giant Panda Sculpture in Qianling Mountain Park is a favorite among families.

Why It’s Popular

The panda, China’s national treasure, is depicted in a cheerful pose, making it a hit for photos. The park itself is a lush retreat, home to wild monkeys and scenic hiking trails.

Insider Tip

Visit early in the morning to avoid crowds and enjoy the park’s serene atmosphere.

The Revolutionary Monument: Liberation Tower

For a dose of 20th-century history, the Liberation Tower commemorates Guiyang’s role in China’s revolutionary struggles.

Historical Context

Built in the 1950s, the tower honors the soldiers who fought during the civil war. Its stark, socialist-realist style contrasts with the city’s older monuments.

Location & Accessibility

Situated in People’s Square, the tower is easily accessible and often included in historical walking tours.

The Hidden Gem: Bronze Statue of the Ancient Scholar

Tucked away in a quiet corner of Guiyang Botanical Garden, the Bronze Statue of the Ancient Scholar is a lesser-known but captivating piece.

Artistic Details

The statue portrays a scholar reading a scroll, surrounded by lush greenery. It’s a peaceful spot for contemplation.

Combine Your Visit

The botanical garden is home to rare plants and a butterfly exhibit, making it a great addition to your itinerary.

Final Thoughts

From ancient philosophers to modern athletes, Guiyang’s statues and monuments tell a story of tradition and transformation. Each landmark offers a unique perspective on the city’s past and present, ensuring that every visitor leaves with a deeper appreciation for this dynamic destination.
