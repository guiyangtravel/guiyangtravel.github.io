A Guide to Guiyang’s Public Holidays & Events

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Nestled in the lush, rolling hills of Southwest China, Guiyang has long been a haven from the summer heat, earning its moniker as the "Forest City" and "Cool Capital." But beyond its temperate climate and breathtaking karst landscapes, Guiyang pulses with a vibrant cultural heartbeat, one that crescendos during its public holidays and unique local festivals. For the discerning traveler, timing your visit to coincide with these events offers an unparalleled dive into the soul of the Guizhou province. This guide is your key to unlocking Guiyang’s most spectacular moments, where ancient traditions meet modern revelry.

The Rhythm of National Holidays in Guiyang

Guiyang observes China’s national public holidays, but each is infused with a distinct local flavor. The city doesn’t just celebrate; it transforms, offering experiences you won’t find anywhere else.

Chinese New Year (Spring Festival) – A Family Affair in Qian

While many mega-cities empty out during Spring Festival, Guiyang retains a warm, communal atmosphere. The city is adorned with red lanterns, and the air is filled with the scent of la rou (cured meat) and niangao (rice cakes). The real magic lies in the surrounding villages and ethnic towns, easily accessible as day trips. Witness Dixi, a mysterious "ground opera" where performers wear wooden masks and enact stories of gods and heroes, a tradition dating back centuries. For foodies, the New Year market near Qianling Park is a sensory overload, showcasing local specialties like si wa wa (a potato-based snack) and countless varieties of chili condiments. It’s a more intimate, traditional celebration of the lunar new year.

Qingming Festival – Serenity and Scenic Beauty

In early April, Qingming Festival (Tomb-Sweeping Day) brings a reflective calm to Guiyang. It’s a time for families to honor ancestors, but for visitors, it coincides with one of the most beautiful seasons. The hills are intensely green, and flowers are in full bloom. It’s the perfect time for hiking in Qianling Mountain Park or taking a boat ride on the serene Huaxi River. The holiday is less about boisterous celebration and more about appreciating natural renewal—a core philosophy in this green city. The weather is mild, making it ideal for exploring outdoor attractions like the Jiaxiu Tower without the summer crowds.

International Labor Day & National Day Golden Weeks – The Festival of Festivals

These two week-long holidays (May 1st and October 1st) are Guiyang’s grand showcases. The city fully embraces its role as the gateway to Guizhou’s ethnic tourism. While major sights are busy, the energy is electric. This is when Guiyang often hosts special events, food festivals, and cultural performances in central squares and parks. It’s the best time to experience a concentrated dose of Miao and Dong minority culture without venturing too far. You’ll hear the haunting melodies of lusheng (reed pipe) music and see the dazzling silver headdresses of dancers. Pro tip: Use Guiyang as your base but book day trips to nearby ethnic villages like Qingyan Ancient Town or Kaili in advance, as this is peak domestic travel season.

Guiyang’s Unique Local Events & Cultural Spectacles

The true gems of Guiyang’s calendar are its local and provincial events, deeply rooted in the culture of the highlands and its diverse ethnic groups.

Guiyang Summer Music Season – A Cool Soundtrack

Capitalizing on its perfect summer weather (average 75°F/24°C), Guiyang has launched a major Summer Music Season. Throughout July and August, the city comes alive with open-air concerts, indie band performances, and electronic music festivals, often held in scenic spots like the Guiyang Olympic Sports Center or park amphitheaters. It’s a fantastic fusion of Guiyang’s youthful, modern energy with its natural backdrop. Imagine listening to great music under a starry, cool sky—a far cry from the sweltering festivals elsewhere.

The Sisters’ Meal Festival – A Riot of Color and Romance

Though centered in neighboring Taijiang County (a few hours from Guiyang), this Miao ethnic festival is a major draw for travelers based in the city. Occurring typically in April or May (based on the lunar calendar), it’s often called the "Oriental Valentine’s Day." Miao women prepare elaborate, colorful "sisters’ meals" of sticky rice in symbolic hues. Dressed in their most magnificent silver-adorned attire, they sing and dance, and the festival is a traditional courtship ritual. Tour operators from Guiyang offer special packages during this time. The visual spectacle of thousands of participants in full regalia is utterly breathtaking and a photographer’s dream.

Lusheng Festivals – The Sound of the Mountains

The lusheng is more than an instrument; it’s the cultural heartbeat of the Miao people. Various villages around Guiyang hold their own Lusheng Festivals throughout the year, especially after the autumn harvest (September-October). Men play the long, bamboo pipes in synchronized groups, creating a powerful, resonant sound, while women dance in intricate circles. Visitors to Guiyang can often experience smaller-scale lusheng performances in cultural parks like the Guiyang Ethnic Culture and Tourism Corridor, but attending a genuine village festival is a transformative experience.

Travel Hotspots & Practical Tips for Holiday Visits

Navigating Guiyang during these peak times requires some savvy planning to maximize enjoyment.

Hotspot Focus: Qianling Park During Major Events

This massive urban park is a central hub for local celebrations. During Mid-Autumn Festival, it’s filled with families enjoying mooncakes under the full moon. During National Day, it might host flower exhibitions and folk performances. The park’s Hongfu Temple adds a spiritual dimension to the festivities. Expect lively crowds and a genuine slice of local life.

Foodie Alert: Holiday Market Delicacies

Public holidays trigger a explosion of specialty street food. Seek out the Nanzhimen (South Gate) area or the streets around Da十字 (Da Shi Zi). Look for zhang you ba (fried rice cakes with a savory sauce), chang wang mian (intestine-noodle soup), and during summer, liang fen (chilled jelly noodles). Holiday markets are the best place to try a dozen different local flavors in one go.

Accommodation & Transportation Strategy

Book hotels and trains well in advance for National Holiday and Labor Day—think months, not weeks. Consider staying near Guanshanhu Lake for a more modern, slightly less crowded base, or in the Yunyan District for easier access to old town vibes. The Guiyang Metro is efficient for beating traffic. For events in surrounding counties, renting a car with a driver or booking a guided tour is highly recommended, as public transport to remote villages will be overwhelmed.

Embracing the "Cool Capital" Vibe

No matter when you visit, Guiyang’s essence is its laid-back, cool demeanor. The local concept of "ba shi"—a contented, unhurried approach to life—permeates even the busiest festival. So, while you’re navigating the exciting chaos of a public holiday event, take a cue from the locals: grab a stool at a street-side tea shop, sip some local Maojian tea, and simply soak in the vibrant, unforgettable scene around you. The harmony of ancient mountain traditions and contemporary joy is what makes Guiyang’s event calendar truly special.
