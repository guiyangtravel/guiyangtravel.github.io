Cycling Routes in Guiyang: Active Travel Guide

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Guiyang, the capital of Guizhou Province, is a hidden gem for cycling enthusiasts. Nestled among lush mountains and crisscrossed by winding rivers, the city offers a mix of urban trails, scenic countryside routes, and challenging mountain paths. Whether you're a casual rider or an adrenaline junkie, Guiyang’s diverse cycling routes promise unforgettable adventures.

Why Cycle in Guiyang?

Guiyang’s unique geography makes it a paradise for cyclists. The city’s moderate climate, with cool summers and mild winters, ensures comfortable riding conditions year-round. Additionally, the local government has invested in cycling infrastructure, including dedicated bike lanes and rental systems, making it easier than ever to explore on two wheels.

Best Seasons for Cycling

Spring (March-May) and autumn (September-November) are ideal, with pleasant temperatures and minimal rainfall. Summer can be humid, while winter is chilly but still manageable for dedicated riders.

Top Cycling Routes in Guiyang

1. Qianling Mountain Loop

Difficulty: Moderate to Challenging
Distance: ~15 km (loop)
Highlights: Panoramic city views, dense forests, and temple visits

This route circles Qianling Mountain, one of Guiyang’s most iconic landmarks. The climb is steep in sections, but the reward is breathtaking views of the city skyline. Along the way, stop by Hongfu Temple, a serene Buddhist monastery nestled in the hills.

Pro Tip:

Start early to avoid crowds and catch the sunrise over the city.

2. Huaxi River Greenway

Difficulty: Easy
Distance: ~20 km (one-way)
Highlights: Riverside scenery, wetlands, and local cafes

Perfect for a leisurely ride, the Huaxi River Greenway follows the tranquil Huaxi River through parks and wetlands. The flat terrain makes it suitable for families and beginners. Stop by one of the riverside cafes for a coffee break while enjoying the view.

3. Baihua Lake Circuit

Difficulty: Moderate
Distance: ~30 km (loop)
Highlights: Lakeside trails, karst landscapes, and ethnic villages

Baihua Lake, located just outside Guiyang, offers a picturesque cycling route with crystal-clear waters and towering karst formations. The trail passes through small Miao and Buyi villages, where you can experience local culture and cuisine.

Must-See Spot:

The "Moon Lake" section, where the water reflects the surrounding cliffs like a mirror.

4. Nanming River Urban Trail

Difficulty: Easy
Distance: ~10 km (one-way)
Highlights: City landmarks, night rides, and street food

For those who prefer urban cycling, the Nanming River trail cuts through downtown Guiyang, passing landmarks like Jiaxiu Tower and the Guiyang Grand Theater. At night, the route lights up, offering a magical ride through the city’s vibrant nightlife.

5. Leigong Mountain Adventure Route

Difficulty: Challenging
Distance: ~50 km (loop)
Highlights: Off-road trails, waterfalls, and remote villages

For thrill-seekers, the Leigong Mountain route delivers rugged terrain and remote landscapes. The trail includes steep climbs, rocky descents, and even a few river crossings. Along the way, you’ll encounter hidden waterfalls and traditional Dong minority villages.

Gear Recommendation:

A mountain bike with sturdy tires and suspension is a must for this route.

Cycling Tips for Guiyang

Renting a Bike

Guiyang has several bike-sharing programs, including HelloBike and Meituan Bike, which are convenient for short rides. For longer trips, consider renting a high-quality bike from shops like Guiyang Cycling Club or Bike Kingdom.

Safety First

  • Always wear a helmet.
  • Carry a repair kit and spare tubes.
  • Stay hydrated—Guiyang’s humidity can be deceptive.

Local Etiquette

  • Yield to pedestrians on shared paths.
  • Avoid cycling during rush hour in crowded areas.

Combining Cycling with Other Activities

Food Stops Along the Way

Guiyang is famous for its street food. Don’t miss:
- Siwawa (a spicy local wrap)
- Sour Fish Soup (a regional specialty)
- Grilled Stinky Tofu (an acquired taste but worth trying)

Cultural Detours

Pair your ride with visits to:
- Qingyan Ancient Town (a well-preserved Ming-era village)
- Tianhetan Scenic Area (a stunning waterfall and cave system)

Final Thoughts

Guiyang’s cycling routes offer something for everyone—from serene riverside paths to heart-pumping mountain trails. With its stunning landscapes, rich culture, and growing cycling infrastructure, the city is a must-visit for active travelers. So grab a bike, hit the road, and discover Guiyang’s hidden treasures on two wheels!
