Best Parks and Gardens in Guiyang

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Guiyang, the capital of Guizhou Province, is often called China’s "Summer Capital" thanks to its mild climate and lush landscapes. Nestled among rolling hills and karst formations, the city boasts some of the most beautiful parks and gardens in the country. Whether you’re a nature lover, a photography enthusiast, or just looking for a peaceful retreat, Guiyang’s green spaces offer something for everyone. Here’s a guide to the best parks and gardens in Guiyang that you shouldn’t miss.

Qianling Park: The Heart of Guiyang’s Nature

A Sanctuary for Monkeys and Locals Alike

Qianling Park is arguably the most famous park in Guiyang, and for good reason. Located just a short distance from the city center, this sprawling green space is home to hundreds of wild macaques. These playful creatures are a major draw, but the park’s natural beauty—think dense forests, serene lakes, and winding trails—makes it a must-visit.

Hongfu Temple: A Spiritual Retreat

At the heart of Qianling Park lies Hongfu Temple, one of Guizhou’s most important Buddhist sites. The temple’s tranquil atmosphere and traditional architecture provide a perfect contrast to the bustling city outside. Don’t miss the chance to climb the temple’s steps for a panoramic view of Guiyang.

Huaxi Park: Where Water and Greenery Meet

A Scenic Lakeside Escape

Huaxi Park, located in the Huaxi District, is known for its picturesque lake surrounded by willow trees and flower beds. Visitors can rent paddle boats, stroll along the water’s edge, or simply relax on one of the many benches. The park is especially stunning in spring when cherry blossoms and azaleas are in full bloom.

The Ten-Mile Riverwalk

One of the park’s highlights is the Ten-Mile Riverwalk, a scenic path that follows the Huaxi River. This route is perfect for joggers, cyclists, or anyone who enjoys a leisurely walk amid nature. Along the way, you’ll pass charming bridges, small waterfalls, and even a few teahouses where you can stop for a break.

Baihua Lake: A Hidden Gem

A Paradise for Birdwatchers

Baihua Lake, located about 20 kilometers from downtown Guiyang, is a lesser-known but equally breathtaking destination. The lake’s clear waters and surrounding forests attract a variety of bird species, making it a haven for birdwatchers. If you’re lucky, you might spot rare migratory birds during certain seasons.

Kayaking and Hiking Adventures

For those who love outdoor activities, Baihua Lake offers kayaking and hiking opportunities. The trails around the lake range from easy walks to more challenging hikes, all offering stunning views of the water and nearby karst formations.

Nanming River Urban Wetland Park: Modern Meets Nature

A Green Oasis in the City

This recently developed park along the Nanming River is a perfect example of urban green space done right. With well-maintained walking paths, cycling lanes, and plenty of seating areas, it’s a favorite among locals for morning exercises and evening strolls.

Nighttime Illuminations

One of the park’s standout features is its nighttime lighting. As the sun sets, the park transforms into a magical landscape with illuminated bridges and trees reflecting on the river. It’s a fantastic spot for photography or a romantic evening walk.

Guiyang Forest Park: A Breath of Fresh Air

A Forest Within the City

Guiyang Forest Park is one of the largest green spaces in the city, offering dense woodlands, open meadows, and even a small zoo. It’s an ideal place for picnics, family outings, or simply escaping the urban hustle.

The Cable Car Experience

For a unique perspective of the park, take the cable car that runs above the treetops. The ride provides stunning aerial views of the forest and the city skyline in the distance.

Tips for Visiting Guiyang’s Parks

  • Best Time to Visit: Spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) offer the most pleasant weather.
  • What to Bring: Comfortable walking shoes, sunscreen, and a camera.
  • Local Etiquette: Respect the wildlife (especially the monkeys in Qianling Park) and avoid feeding them.
  • Transportation: Most parks are accessible by public transport, but taxis or ride-sharing apps are convenient for more remote locations like Baihua Lake.

Guiyang’s parks and gardens are more than just green spaces—they’re an integral part of the city’s charm. Whether you’re seeking adventure, relaxation, or a bit of both, these destinations promise an unforgettable experience.
