Top Places to Experience Guiyang’s Night Markets

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Guiyang, the vibrant capital of Guizhou Province, is a city that truly comes alive after dark. Known for its rich ethnic culture, stunning landscapes, and—most importantly—its incredible street food, Guiyang’s night markets are a must-visit for any traveler. Whether you’re a foodie, a culture enthusiast, or just someone who loves the buzz of a lively evening scene, these markets offer an unforgettable experience. Here’s a guide to the best night markets in Guiyang and what makes each one special.

1. Qingyan Ancient Town Night Market

A Blend of History and Flavor

Nestled in the outskirts of Guiyang, Qingyan Ancient Town is a beautifully preserved Ming Dynasty village that transforms into a food lover’s paradise at night. The cobblestone streets, lit by traditional lanterns, create a magical atmosphere perfect for an evening stroll.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Changwang Noodles (肠旺面): A local favorite featuring thick noodles in a spicy broth with pork blood cubes and intestines.
  • Stuffed Tofu (豆腐圆子): Crispy on the outside, soft on the inside, and stuffed with savory fillings.
  • Rose Sugar Ice Jelly (玫瑰糖冰粉): A refreshing dessert made with jelly, rose syrup, and crushed peanuts.

Pro Tip

Visit early to explore the historic sites before the food stalls open around sunset.

2. Erqi Road Night Market

The Heart of Guiyang’s Street Food Scene

Located in the city center, Erqi Road Night Market is the most famous and busiest night market in Guiyang. Stretching for nearly a kilometer, this market is packed with vendors serving everything from local delicacies to regional Chinese dishes.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Siwawa (丝娃娃): A DIY wrap filled with fresh vegetables, tofu, and a tangy dipping sauce.
  • Grilled Fish with Fermented Chili (酸汤鱼): A Guizhou specialty where fish is cooked in a sour-spicy broth.
  • Barbecue Skewers (烧烤): From lamb to mushrooms, these skewers are a late-night staple.

Pro Tip

Go with an empty stomach—there’s too much to try in one visit!

3. Huaxi Night Market

A Local’s Hidden Gem

Less touristy than Erqi Road, Huaxi Night Market offers a more authentic experience. Located near Huaxi Park, this market is where locals go for late-night snacks and socializing.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Zha’ergen (炸耳根): Deep-fried fish mint, a unique herb with a bold flavor.
  • Rice Tofu (米豆腐): A chewy, savory snack often served with chili oil.
  • Mala Tang (麻辣烫): A spicy, numbing hotpot where you pick your own ingredients.

Pro Tip

Pair your food with a glass of Moutai Beer, a local brew that’s lighter than the famous liquor.

4. Nanming Riverwalk Night Market

Scenic Dining by the Water

For those who prefer a more relaxed vibe, the Nanming Riverwalk Night Market combines great food with beautiful riverside views. It’s perfect for a post-dinner walk or a casual drink by the water.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Guizhou Sausage (贵州香肠): Smoky, slightly sweet, and packed with flavor.
  • Fried Potato Cakes (洋芋粑): Crispy on the outside, soft inside, and served with chili dip.
  • Pearl Barley Wine (薏米酒): A sweet, lightly alcoholic drink made from barley.

Pro Tip

Visit on weekends when live music performances add to the ambiance.

5. Wenchang Pavilion Night Market

Culture Meets Cuisine

Set near the historic Wenchang Pavilion, this night market is smaller but rich in cultural charm. It’s a great spot to try traditional snacks while admiring ancient architecture.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Liangfen (凉粉): A cold, jelly-like noodle dish with a spicy dressing.
  • Sticky Rice Balls (糍粑): Sweet or savory, these chewy treats are a crowd-pleaser.
  • Sour Plum Drink (酸梅汤): A tart, refreshing beverage to balance spicy foods.

Pro Tip

Climb the pavilion for a panoramic night view of Guiyang.

Bonus: Midnight Snack Streets Near Universities

Where the Students Go

Guiyang’s university districts, like near Guizhou University, have their own late-night snack streets. These spots are budget-friendly and full of youthful energy.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jianbing (煎饼): A savory crepe stuffed with eggs, crispy crackers, and sauces.
  • Spicy Crawfish (麻辣小龙虾): A messy but delicious late-night indulgence.
  • Bubble Tea (奶茶): Perfect for washing down all the spicy flavors.

Pro Tip

These markets stay open until the early hours, making them ideal for night owls.

Final Thoughts for the Adventurous Eater

Guiyang’s night markets are more than just places to eat—they’re cultural experiences. From the fiery flavors of Guizhou’s famous chili dishes to the sweet, cooling desserts, every bite tells a story. Whether you’re exploring ancient streets or mingling with locals at a bustling market, these spots promise unforgettable memories (and full stomachs). So grab your chopsticks, dive in, and let Guiyang’s night markets take you on a culinary adventure.
