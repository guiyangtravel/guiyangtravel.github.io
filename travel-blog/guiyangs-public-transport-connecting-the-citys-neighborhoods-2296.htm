Guiyang’s Public Transport: Connecting the City’s Neighborhoods

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Guiyang, the capital of Guizhou Province, is a city where modernity meets tradition, and its public transport system plays a pivotal role in weaving these elements together. For travelers, navigating Guiyang’s neighborhoods is a breeze thanks to an efficient network of buses, metro lines, and even cable cars. Whether you’re exploring the bustling city center or venturing into the lush outskirts, Guiyang’s transport options ensure you’re always connected.

The Backbone of Guiyang’s Transport: Metro and Buses

The Expanding Metro System

Guiyang’s metro system, though relatively new, has quickly become the lifeline of the city. With two operational lines (Line 1 and Line 2) and more under construction, the metro connects major hubs like Guiyang Railway Station, Guiyang North Station, and the city’s commercial districts.

  • Line 1 runs from Xiaomeng Industrial Park to Guiyang North Station, passing through key areas like Jinyang and the Guizhou Provincial Museum.
  • Line 2 links Baiyun District to Longdongbao International Airport, making it a favorite for travelers needing a quick transfer.

The metro is clean, affordable, and user-friendly, with announcements in both Mandarin and English. For tourists, it’s the fastest way to avoid Guiyang’s notorious traffic jams during peak hours.

The Ubiquitous Bus Network

If the metro doesn’t cover your destination, Guiyang’s extensive bus system will. With over 200 routes, buses reach every corner of the city, from the tourist-heavy Qianling Park to the lesser-known but charming Qingyan Ancient Town.

  • Double-decker sightseeing buses (Route 1 and 2) offer panoramic views of the city, perfect for first-time visitors.
  • Night buses ensure late-night explorers can still get back to their hotels safely.

Pro tip: Download the "Guiyang Bus" app for real-time updates and route planning.

Unique Transport Experiences in Guiyang

The Qianling Mountain Cable Car

For a scenic shortcut to one of Guiyang’s top attractions, hop on the Qianling Mountain Cable Car. This 10-minute ride glides over lush forests and offers breathtaking views of the city skyline. It’s not just a mode of transport—it’s an attraction in itself.

The "Little Taxis" (三轮车)

In older neighborhoods like Huaxi, you’ll spot three-wheeled electric "little taxis" zipping through narrow alleys. These are perfect for short trips where buses can’t reach, and they add a nostalgic charm to the journey. Just remember to negotiate the fare before hopping in!

Travel Tips for Navigating Guiyang Like a Local

  1. Use a Transit Card (一卡通): Purchase a rechargeable transit card for seamless metro and bus rides.
  2. Avoid Rush Hour (7:30-9:00 AM, 5:00-7:00 PM): Traffic congestion is real, especially on roads leading to the city center.
  3. Explore on Foot: Neighborhoods like Zhonghua North Road are best discovered by walking, with hidden tea houses and street food stalls at every turn.

How Public Transport Enhances Guiyang’s Tourism

Guiyang’s transport isn’t just functional—it’s a gateway to cultural experiences. Riding the metro reveals snippets of local life, while buses to ethnic Miao villages open doors to authentic Guizhou culture. The city’s commitment to sustainable transport (like electric buses) also aligns with the growing trend of eco-conscious travel.

So next time you’re in Guiyang, ditch the tour buses and embrace the local way of getting around. You’ll see more, save money, and maybe even make a friend or two along the way.
