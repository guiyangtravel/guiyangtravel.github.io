Discover Guiyang on Foot: The Ultimate Walking Tour Guide

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Guiyang, the capital of Guizhou Province, is a city where modernity meets tradition, and nature blends seamlessly with urban life. Known for its lush greenery, vibrant culture, and mouthwatering cuisine, Guiyang is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. The best way to experience its charm? On foot. This walking tour guide will take you through the city’s must-see spots, hidden alleys, and local favorites, ensuring an unforgettable adventure.

Why Walk Guiyang?

Walking is the perfect way to immerse yourself in Guiyang’s unique atmosphere. The city’s compact layout, pedestrian-friendly streets, and scenic routes make it ideal for explorers who want to soak in the sights, sounds, and flavors at their own pace. Plus, you’ll stumble upon hidden gems that most tourists miss.

The Best Walking Routes in Guiyang

1. Qianling Park: A Green Oasis in the City

Start your journey at Qianling Park, a sprawling urban park that offers a refreshing escape from the city buzz. The park is home to Qianling Mountain, where you can hike up to Hongfu Temple, a serene Buddhist monastery with panoramic views of Guiyang.

  • Highlights:
    • Hongfu Temple: A peaceful retreat with intricate architecture.
    • Monkey Encounters: Wild macaques roam freely—keep your snacks secure!
    • Lakeside Strolls: The park’s artificial lake is perfect for a leisurely walk.

2. Jiaxiu Pavilion: The Icon of Guiyang

No visit to Guiyang is complete without seeing Jiaxiu Pavilion, a Ming Dynasty-era landmark perched over Nanming River. This three-story wooden structure is especially magical at night when it’s illuminated.

  • Walking Route:
    • Start at Fuyuan Bridge for the best photo ops.
    • Cross the river and explore the surrounding ancient-style streets.
    • Stop by a teahouse for a cup of Guizhou’s famous Maojian tea.

3. Qingyan Ancient Town: Step Back in Time

A short bus ride from downtown, Qingyan Ancient Town is a well-preserved historical site with cobblestone streets, Ming-Qing architecture, and a rich military history.

  • Must-See Spots:
    • City Walls: Walk along the ancient fortifications.
    • Local Snacks: Try Qingyan Tofu and Rose Sugar Candy.
    • Hidden Temples: Discover tucked-away shrines and courtyards.

4. Huaxi Wetland Park: Nature’s Playground

For a change of scenery, head to Huaxi Wetland Park, a tranquil area with walking trails, lotus ponds, and karst landscapes. It’s a favorite among locals for morning jogs and picnics.

  • Pro Tip: Visit in early summer when the lotus flowers are in full bloom.

Off-the-Beaten-Path Walks

1. Guiyang’s Secret Alleys: Dongjia Alley & Baihua Lane

Escape the tourist crowds and wander through Dongjia Alley and Baihua Lane, where traditional houses, indie cafes, and street art create a bohemian vibe.

  • What to Do:
    • Sip coffee at a hidden courtyard café.
    • Browse vintage shops and local handicrafts.

2. Night Walk Along Nanming River

Guiyang’s nightlife is lively but not overwhelming. A riverside walk along Nanming River offers stunning reflections of city lights on the water, with food stalls serving late-night Siwawa (local spring rolls) and grilled fish.

Food Stops You Can’t Miss

Walking builds an appetite, and Guiyang’s street food scene is legendary. Here are must-try snacks along your route:

  • Changwang Noodles: Thick, chewy noodles in a spicy broth.
  • Sour Soup Fish: A Guizhou specialty—tangy and fiery.
  • Ciba (Sticky Rice Cake): Sweet or savory, perfect for energy.

Practical Tips for Walking in Guiyang

  • Best Time to Walk: Spring and autumn for mild weather.
  • Footwear: Comfortable shoes—some paths are uneven.
  • Local Etiquette: Greet vendors with a smile; bargaining is common in markets.

Guiyang is a city best discovered step by step. Whether you’re climbing ancient staircases, meandering through night markets, or pausing for a tea break, every corner tells a story. Lace up your shoes and let the adventure begin!
