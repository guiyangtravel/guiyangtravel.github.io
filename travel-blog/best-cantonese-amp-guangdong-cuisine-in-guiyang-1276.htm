Best Cantonese & Guangdong Cuisine in Guiyang

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Guiyang, the vibrant capital of Guizhou Province, is a city where culinary traditions collide. While the local Guizhou cuisine is famous for its bold, spicy flavors, there’s a growing appreciation for the delicate and refined tastes of Cantonese and Guangdong cuisine. Whether you're a traveler craving dim sum after exploring Qianling Park or a foodie seeking authentic roast goose, Guiyang has a surprising number of excellent Cantonese restaurants. Here’s a guide to the best spots to savor Guangdong’s culinary treasures in the heart of southwest China.

Why Cantonese Cuisine Stands Out in Guiyang

Cantonese food, known for its emphasis on fresh ingredients and subtle seasoning, offers a refreshing contrast to Guiyang’s fiery local dishes. Steamed seafood, slow-cooked soups, and crispy roasted meats are just a few highlights. Many of these restaurants cater to both locals and tourists, blending traditional techniques with modern twists.

The Rise of Guangdong Flavors in Guiyang

Over the past decade, Guiyang’s dining scene has expanded to include more regional Chinese cuisines. Cantonese restaurants have gained popularity due to:
- Health-conscious trends: Steaming and boiling methods appeal to those seeking lighter meals.
- Tourist demand: Visitors from Guangdong or Hong Kong often seek familiar flavors.
- Business dining: Upscale Cantonese spots are favored for business banquets.

Top Cantonese & Guangdong Restaurants in Guiyang

1. Imperial Treasure (御宝轩)

Location: Near Guiyang International Conference & Exhibition Center
Highlights: Luxurious setting, Michelin-inspired dim sum

This high-end restaurant brings Hong Kong’s fine dining standards to Guiyang. Their signature dishes include:
- Barbecued Iberian Pork: A melt-in-your-mouth delicacy with a caramelized glaze.
- Steamed Grouper with Ginger and Scallions: Fresh fish flown in daily.
- Truffle Mushroom Dumplings: A modern twist on classic dim sum.

Perfect for a special occasion or a lavish treat after visiting Jiaxiu Tower.

2. Canton Delight (粤味轩)

Location: Zhonghua North Road
Highlights: Affordable, family-friendly, extensive menu

A favorite among locals, Canton Delight offers hearty portions of classic Guangdong dishes:
- Roast Duck with Plum Sauce: Crispy skin, tender meat.
- Clay Pot Rice with Preserved Sausages: Comfort food at its best.
- Double-Boiled Chicken Soup: A nourishing must-try.

Great for a casual meal after shopping at the bustling Grandmall.

3. Pearl River Seafood Palace (珠江海鲜酒家)

Location: Nanming District
Highlights: Live seafood tanks, banquet-style dining

If you love seafood, this is the place to go. The restaurant sources live seafood from Guangdong daily:
- Garlic Steamed Lobster: Sweet, succulent, and aromatic.
- Braised Abalone with Oyster Sauce: A luxurious indulgence.
- Stir-Fried Crab with Ginger and Onions: Bursting with umami.

Ideal for group dinners or celebrating a successful day of exploring Huangguoshu Waterfall.

4. Dim Sum Garden (点心坊)

Location: Yunyan District
Highlights: All-day dim sum, cozy ambiance

For a relaxed brunch or late-night snack, Dim Sum Garden delivers:
- Har Gow (Shrimp Dumplings): Translucent wrappers, juicy filling.
- Char Siu Bao (BBQ Pork Buns): Fluffy and slightly sweet.
- Egg Tarts: Buttery crust, silky custard.

A perfect pit stop before heading to Qingyan Ancient Town.

Must-Try Cantonese Dishes in Guiyang

Roast Meats: A Crispy Delight

No Cantonese meal is complete without roasted meats. Look for:
- Siu Yuk (Crispy Pork Belly): Golden crackling with tender layers.
- Siu Aap (Roast Duck): Marinated with five-spice and honey.

Slow-Cooked Soups: Comfort in a Bowl

Guangdong’s lou fo tong (slow-boiled soups) are legendary:
- Watercress and Pork Bone Soup: Cleansing and light.
- Snow Fungus and Chicken Soup: A beauty tonic.

Dim Sum: Small Bites, Big Flavors

From cheung fun (rice noodle rolls) to phoenix claws (braised chicken feet), dim sum is a culinary adventure.

Pairing Cantonese Food with Guiyang’s Attractions

After indulging in Cantonese cuisine, explore these nearby attractions:
- Qianling Mountain Park: Walk off your meal while spotting wild macaques.
- Jiaxiu Pavilion: A historic landmark lit beautifully at night.
- Guiyang Olympic Sports Center: Catch a local soccer game.

Insider Tips for Enjoying Cantonese Dining in Guiyang

  • Tea etiquette: Most restaurants serve pu’er or jasmine tea—refill your cup to show appreciation.
  • Peak hours: Dim sum is busiest from 10 AM to 2 PM; book ahead.
  • Spice adjustments: Cantonese food is mild, but you can request chili sauce if needed.

Whether you’re a traveler missing home flavors or a curious foodie, Guiyang’s Cantonese dining scene won’t disappoint. From high-end banquets to humble dim sum joints, there’s something for every palate.
