Guiyang’s Best Spots for Wildlife Watching

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Guiyang, the capital of Guizhou Province, is a hidden gem for nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts. Nestled in the heart of China’s lush southwestern region, the city boasts a unique blend of urban life and pristine natural landscapes. From dense forests to serene wetlands, Guiyang offers incredible opportunities to observe rare and exotic wildlife. Whether you’re a birdwatcher, a photography enthusiast, or simply someone who enjoys being in nature, these spots will leave you in awe.

Qianling Park: A Green Oasis in the City

Why Visit Qianling Park?

Qianling Park is one of Guiyang’s most famous urban parks, covering over 400 hectares. It’s a sanctuary for both locals and wildlife, making it an ideal spot for casual wildlife watching. The park’s dense vegetation and tranquil lakes attract a variety of bird species, including the elusive Chinese Bamboo Partridge and the vibrant Red-billed Blue Magpie.

Best Time to Visit

Early mornings and late afternoons are the best times to spot birds and small mammals. Spring and autumn are particularly rewarding, as migratory birds pass through the area.

Wildlife to Look For

  • Birds: Red-billed Blue Magpie, Chinese Bamboo Partridge, White Wagtail
  • Mammals: Squirrels, occasional sightings of masked palm civets
  • Reptiles: Chinese water dragons near the ponds

Huaxi Wetland Park: A Birdwatcher’s Paradise

Exploring the Wetlands

Huaxi Wetland Park is a must-visit for serious birdwatchers. The park’s extensive marshlands and waterways create a perfect habitat for waterfowl and migratory birds. The boardwalks and observation decks allow visitors to get close to the wildlife without disturbing their natural behavior.

Notable Species

  • Black-crowned Night Heron – Often seen hunting at dusk
  • Little Egret – Graceful white birds wading in shallow waters
  • Common Kingfisher – A flash of blue darting across the water

Photography Tips

Bring a telephoto lens to capture birds in flight. The golden hours—just after sunrise and before sunset—provide the best lighting for stunning shots.

Nanjiang Grand Canyon: Wild and Untamed

Adventure Awaits

For those who prefer rugged landscapes, Nanjiang Grand Canyon offers a thrilling wildlife-watching experience. The steep cliffs, dense forests, and rushing rivers are home to some of Guizhou’s most elusive creatures.

Rare Wildlife Sightings

  • François’ Langur – An endangered monkey species with striking black and white fur
  • Chinese Serow – A goat-antelope that roams the rocky terrain
  • Golden Pheasant – A brilliantly colored bird often spotted in the underbrush

Safety Tips

The terrain can be challenging, so wear sturdy hiking boots and bring plenty of water. Hiring a local guide is recommended for spotting rare animals safely.

Baihua Lake: Serenity and Biodiversity

A Peaceful Escape

Baihua Lake, located just outside Guiyang, is a tranquil destination surrounded by rolling hills and forests. The lake’s calm waters attract a variety of water birds, while the surrounding woodlands are home to smaller mammals and reptiles.

What to Expect

  • Mandarin Ducks – These colorful ducks are a highlight for many visitors
  • Chinese Pond Heron – Often seen standing motionless near the shore
  • Butterflies and Dragonflies – Abundant in the warmer months

Best Activities

  • Kayaking along the lake’s edge for closer wildlife encounters
  • Picnicking in designated areas while enjoying the scenery

Tips for Responsible Wildlife Watching

Respect the Animals

  • Keep a safe distance to avoid stressing the wildlife
  • Avoid feeding wild animals, as it disrupts their natural diet

Pack the Right Gear

  • Binoculars for better viewing
  • A field guide to identify species
  • Eco-friendly sunscreen and insect repellent

Support Conservation Efforts

Many of these spots are protected areas. Consider donating to local conservation programs or participating in eco-tours that fund wildlife preservation.

Guiyang’s diverse ecosystems make it a fantastic destination for wildlife enthusiasts. Whether you’re exploring urban parks or venturing into remote canyons, the city’s natural beauty and rich biodiversity promise unforgettable encounters with nature.
