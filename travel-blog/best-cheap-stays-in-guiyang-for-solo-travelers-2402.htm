Best Cheap Stays in Guiyang for Solo Travelers

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Guiyang, the capital of Guizhou Province, is a hidden gem for solo travelers. With its lush mountains, vibrant minority cultures, and mouthwatering street food, it’s a destination that won’t break the bank. For those exploring on their own, finding affordable yet comfortable accommodations is key. Here’s a curated list of the best budget-friendly stays in Guiyang that offer great value, social vibes, and prime locations.

Budget Hostels with Social Vibes

1. Guiyang Loft International Youth Hostel

Located near the bustling Jiaxiu Pavilion, this hostel is a favorite among backpackers. Dorm beds start at just ¥50 per night, and the communal areas are perfect for meeting fellow travelers. The rooftop terrace offers stunning city views, and the staff frequently organize group outings to nearby attractions like Qianling Mountain.

2. Miao Village Hostel

For a cultural twist, this hostel incorporates Miao minority designs into its decor. Private pods in shared rooms provide privacy, while the common kitchen encourages guest interactions. It’s a short walk from the Guiyang Night Market, where you can try local delicacies like siwawa (a spicy rice wrap).

3. Wander Inn Hostel

A cozy spot near Huaxi Park, Wander Inn is known for its friendly atmosphere. The owners often host movie nights and DIY dumpling-making sessions. Budget tip: Book directly through their WeChat for occasional discounts.

Affordable Boutique Guesthouses

1. Tea Garden Inn

Tucked away in a quiet alley, this guesthouse blends modern minimalism with traditional tea culture. Private rooms start at ¥120, and guests can join free tea ceremonies. The location is ideal for exploring the city’s historic Qingyan Ancient Town.

2. Rainbow House

A LGBTQ+-friendly guesthouse with colorful murals and a laid-back vibe. Solo travelers love the daily breakfast buffet featuring Guizhou’s famous sour fish soup. It’s also close to public transport, making day trips to Huangguoshu Waterfall a breeze.

Unique Budget Stays

1. Capsule Hotel Guiyang

For a futuristic experience, try this capsule hotel near the financial district. Pods come with charging ports and privacy curtains for ¥80/night. The shared lounge has free coffee and travel guides.

2. Temple Stay at Hongfu Temple

A rare chance to sleep in a working Buddhist temple for under ¥100. Guests can join morning chanting or meditation sessions. Note: Curfew at 10 PM, but the serene ambiance is worth it.

Pro Tips for Solo Travelers

  • Public Transport: Guiyang’s metro and buses are cheap (¥2–5 per ride). Use DiDi (China’s Uber) for late-night returns.
  • Food Savings: Street stalls near Zhonghua Road serve hearty meals for under ¥15. Don’t miss changwang noodles!
  • Safety: Stick to well-lit areas at night and keep valuables secure. Most hostels offer lockers.

Whether you’re here for the karst landscapes or the spicy cuisine, these stays ensure you’ll explore Guiyang without emptying your wallet. Pack light, stay curious, and let the city’s charm surprise you!
