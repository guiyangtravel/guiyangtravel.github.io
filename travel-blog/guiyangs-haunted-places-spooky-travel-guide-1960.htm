Guiyang’s Haunted Places: Spooky Travel Guide

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Guiyang, the capital of Guizhou Province, is known for its lush landscapes, rich ethnic culture, and mouthwatering cuisine. But beneath its vibrant surface lies a darker side—stories of haunted locations, eerie legends, and spine-chilling encounters. For thrill-seekers and paranormal enthusiasts, this guide will take you through Guiyang’s most infamous haunted spots, blending history, folklore, and modern-day ghost hunts.

The Ghostly Secrets of Jiaxiu Pavilion

A Beautiful Landmark with a Dark Past

Jiaxiu Pavilion, an iconic Ming Dynasty structure, stands proudly over Nanming River. By day, it’s a picturesque symbol of Guiyang, but by night, whispers of ghostly apparitions unsettle visitors. Locals claim the pavilion is haunted by the spirits of scholars who failed imperial exams and took their own lives in despair.

Paranormal Encounters

Visitors report cold spots, shadowy figures, and the sound of faint weeping near the water. Some even claim to have seen ghostly scholars wandering the pavilion’s upper floors, still clutching their scrolls. Night tours occasionally pick up EVP (Electronic Voice Phenomena) recordings of whispered classical poetry.

The Abandoned Guiyang No. 6 Hospital

A Forgotten Asylum’s Tragic History

Built in the 1950s, Guiyang No. 6 Hospital was once a psychiatric facility before being abandoned in the late 20th century. Rumors persist of unethical experiments and patient mistreatment, fueling its reputation as one of China’s most haunted hospitals.

Exploring the Ruins

Urban explorers brave enough to enter describe an oppressive atmosphere, with flickering lights (despite no electricity) and distant screams echoing through empty halls. Some visitors report being touched by unseen hands or hearing disembodied footsteps following them. The hospital’s basement, allegedly used for controversial procedures, is said to be the most active paranormal hotspot.

The Cursed Qingyan Ancient Town

A Charming Village with a Sinister Side

Qingyan Ancient Town, a well-preserved Ming Dynasty village, attracts tourists with its stone streets and traditional architecture. However, some alleys are avoided after dark due to tales of vengeful spirits.

The Legend of the Weeping Bride

One famous ghost story involves a young bride who was murdered on her wedding night. Her spirit is said to wander the town’s narrow lanes, weeping for her lost love. Locals warn against answering whispers in the wind—it might be her trying to lure the living into the afterlife.

The Haunted Guizhou University Dormitory

Student Ghost Stories

Guiyang’s universities aren’t immune to supernatural tales. A particular dormitory at Guizhou University is infamous for Room 404, where a student allegedly died under mysterious circumstances. Residents claim the room is always icy cold, and some have reported seeing a shadowy figure sitting on the empty bed at night.

The Midnight Chanting

Another eerie phenomenon involves faint chanting in the hallway late at night, despite no one being there. Some believe it’s the spirit of a former student who practiced occult rituals. University staff avoid discussing the incidents, but students continue swapping ghost stories during late-night study sessions.

The Phantom Train of Guiyang Railway Station

A Ghostly Departure

Guiyang Railway Station, a bustling transit hub, has its own spectral legend: the "Phantom Train." According to witnesses, an old-fashioned steam train occasionally appears on abandoned tracks, filled with silent, pale passengers. Those who board supposedly vanish without a trace.

Modern-Day Sightings

Railway workers and late-night travelers claim to have seen the train’s ghostly lights or heard its whistle when no such train was scheduled. Paranormal investigators speculate it could be a residual haunting from a long-forgotten railway disaster.

Tips for Ghost Hunting in Guiyang

Best Times to Visit Haunted Spots

  • Midnight to 3 AM – Peak paranormal activity hours.
  • Qingming Festival (Ghost Month) – When the veil between worlds is thinnest.
  • Rainy Nights – Many report heightened supernatural encounters during storms.

Essential Ghost-Hunting Gear

  • EMF Meter – Detects electromagnetic fluctuations linked to spirits.
  • Infrared Camera – Captures anomalies invisible to the naked eye.
  • Voice Recorder – For capturing EVPs.
  • Protective Charms – Locals recommend carrying salt or a small bag of rice to ward off evil spirits.

Safety Warnings

  • Never provoke spirits—polite curiosity is safer than mockery.
  • Avoid solo explorations in high-risk areas like abandoned hospitals.
  • If you feel sudden dread or nausea, leave immediately—it might be a warning.

Whether you’re a skeptic or a believer, Guiyang’s haunted sites offer a thrilling twist to traditional tourism. Just remember: some stories are best experienced… from a safe distance.
