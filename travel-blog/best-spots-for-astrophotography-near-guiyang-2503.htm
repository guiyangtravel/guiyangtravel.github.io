Best Spots for Astrophotography Near Guiyang

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Guiyang, the capital of Guizhou Province, is a city surrounded by lush mountains and clear skies, making it an underrated gem for astrophotography enthusiasts. While light pollution in urban areas can be a challenge, venturing just a few hours outside the city reveals breathtaking dark-sky locations perfect for capturing the Milky Way, meteor showers, and celestial phenomena. Here’s a guide to the best spots near Guiyang for astrophotography, complete with tips for planning your shoot.

1. Qianling Mountain Park

Why It’s Great for Astrophotography

Located just a short drive from downtown Guiyang, Qianling Mountain Park offers a convenient escape from city lights. The higher elevation provides clearer skies, and the park’s open areas allow for unobstructed views of the night sky.

Best Time to Visit

  • Spring and Autumn: These seasons offer the clearest skies with minimal humidity.
  • New Moon Phase: For the darkest conditions, plan your visit during a new moon.

Photography Tips

  • Use a wide-angle lens (14-24mm) to capture the vastness of the night sky.
  • Arrive early to scout compositions before sunset.

2. Hongfeng Lake (Red Maple Lake)

A Scenic Dark-Sky Retreat

About an hour’s drive from Guiyang, Hongfeng Lake is a serene location with minimal light pollution. The reflective waters of the lake create stunning opportunities for capturing star trails and celestial reflections.

Ideal Conditions

  • Winter: Cold, dry air reduces atmospheric distortion.
  • Clear Nights: Check weather forecasts to avoid cloudy skies.

Gear Recommendations

  • A sturdy tripod is essential for long exposures.
  • A remote shutter release minimizes camera shake.

3. Tianhetan Scenic Area

Remote and Untouched Skies

Tianhetan, roughly two hours from Guiyang, is a hidden paradise for astrophotographers. The area’s low population density ensures exceptionally dark skies, perfect for deep-sky photography.

Unique Features

  • The surrounding karst landscapes add dramatic foreground elements.
  • Minimal artificial light makes it ideal for capturing faint nebulae.

Planning Your Trip

  • Pack warm clothing—nights can get chilly even in summer.
  • Bring extra batteries; cold temperatures drain them faster.

4. Longli Ancient Town

Combining History and the Cosmos

Longli, a well-preserved Ming Dynasty town about 90 minutes from Guiyang, offers a unique blend of cultural and astrophotography opportunities. The ancient architecture provides a striking contrast against the starry sky.

Best Compositions

  • Frame the Milky Way above traditional rooftops.
  • Use light painting techniques to illuminate historic structures.

Travel Tips

  • Stay overnight to capture both sunset and sunrise transitions.
  • Respect local customs—avoid disturbing residents during late-night shoots.

5. Leigong Mountain National Nature Reserve

A High-Altitude Haven

For those willing to venture farther (about 3-4 hours from Guiyang), Leigong Mountain offers some of the darkest skies in the region. The high elevation reduces atmospheric interference, making it a prime spot for astrophotography.

What to Expect

  • Cool temperatures year-round—dress in layers.
  • Rugged terrain; 4WD vehicles are recommended.

Astrophotography Highlights

  • Capture the Milky Way core during summer months.
  • Long-exposure shots reveal intricate star movements.

Essential Gear for Astrophotography Near Guiyang

Must-Have Equipment

  • Camera: A full-frame DSLR or mirrorless camera with high ISO capabilities.
  • Lenses: Fast wide-angle lenses (f/2.8 or wider).
  • Tripod: A heavy-duty model to withstand wind.

Optional but Helpful

  • Star tracker for pinpoint sharpness in long exposures.
  • Light pollution filter if shooting near faint city glows.

Final Thoughts on Timing and Etiquette

Respect the Environment

  • Always pack out trash and avoid disturbing wildlife.
  • Use red-light headlamps to preserve night vision for yourself and others.

Best Astronomical Events to Capture

  • Perseid Meteor Shower (August): High meteor rates with clear summer skies.
  • Gemini Meteor Shower (December): Winter clarity enhances visibility.

With these locations and tips, you’re ready to embark on an unforgettable astrophotography adventure near Guiyang. Whether you’re a seasoned photographer or a beginner, the region’s dark skies and stunning landscapes provide endless opportunities to capture the wonders of the universe.
