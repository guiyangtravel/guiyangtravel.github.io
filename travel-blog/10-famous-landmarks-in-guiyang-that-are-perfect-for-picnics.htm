10 Famous Landmarks in Guiyang That Are Perfect for Picnics

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Forget the standard tourist trail of hurried photos and crowded queues. To truly savor the essence of Guiyang—the vibrant, mist-kissed capital of China’s Guizhou province—one must embrace its leisurely, nature-infused soul. And there is no better way to do that than with a picnic. Guiyang isn’t just about spicy hotpot and majestic mountains; it’s a city of serene parks, historic nooks, and cultural hubs where the pace slows and the air sweetens. Here, a picnic is more than a meal; it’s a portal into the local lifestyle. We’ve curated a list of ten famous landmarks where you can unpack your basket, soak in the views, and experience Guiyang like a true local.

1. Qianling Park: The Mountain Sanctuary

As Guiyang’s green lung, Qianling Park is the quintessential picnic destination. It’s a sprawling oasis where locals practice tai chi, elders sing folk songs, and families gather for weekend outings.

Perfect Picnic Spot: Near Hongfu Temple or by the Macaque Zones

Find a quiet clearing on the slopes leading to the iconic Hongfu Temple. The sound of temple bells and the chatter of mischievous macaques create a unique soundtrack. Pack easy-to-eat finger foods like bingfen (a refreshing jelly), local fruits, and steamed buns. Be mindful of the monkeys—secure your snacks! The panoramic view of the city from higher paths offers a breathtaking backdrop for your alfresco dining.

2. Jiaxiu Pavilion: A Picnic with Poetic Flair

Standing proudly on Fuyu Bridge over the Nanming River, Jiaxiu Tower is Guiyang’s most recognizable symbol. This Ming-era pavilion, especially when lit at night, is a vision of classical elegance.

Perfect Picnic Spot: The Riverside Gardens

The key here is an evening picnic. As the sun sets and the pavilion’s lights reflect on the water, the atmosphere turns magical. Spread your blanket in the open gardens along the riverbank. Ideal picnic fare includes siwawa (a delicate veggie wrap) and some sweet treats. It’s a fusion of history and romance, perfect for a serene, culturally-rich meal.

3. Qingyan Ancient Town: A Historic Feast

Wandering the cobbled streets of this 600-year-old Ming Dynasty town feels like stepping back in time. Stone walls, ancient temples, and the aroma of local snacks fill the air.

Perfect Picnic Spot: A Quiet Corner Near the City Walls

Instead of picnicking in the bustling main lanes, purchase goodies from the vendors—like rose sugar candy, changwang noodles, or grilled tofu—and find a spot near a lesser-known section of the towering city walls. You’ll enjoy the ancient ambiance without the heaviest foot traffic, imagining the town’s storied past as you dine.

4. Huaxi Park: The Lakeside Retreat

This park, centered around the beautiful Huaxi River, is celebrated for its poetic landscape of connected islands, pavilions, and willow trees draping over calm waters.

Perfect Picnic Spot: Ten-Li Scenic Corridor

The so-called "Ten-Li Scenic Corridor" offers countless serene spots. Choose a grassy bank under a willow tree. The gentle water flow and traditional boats drifting by create a painting-like setting. A picnic here calls for light, elegant food—think tea eggs, delicate pastries, and a thermos of local green tea. It’s tranquility defined.

5. Guiyang Olympic Sports Center: Modern Greens & Urban Energy

A landmark of modern Guiyang, this vast, futuristic complex isn’t just for sports events. Its surrounding lawns and open spaces are meticulously maintained, offering a contemporary picnic vibe.

Perfect Picnic Spot: The Expansive Lawns Facing the Stadium

This spot is ideal for a lively, group picnic. Bring games, frisbees, and a basket full of shareable foods—sandwiches, chips, fruit salads, and cold drinks. The architectural spectacle of the stadium adds a dynamic, energetic feel, especially on a bright, sunny day when the city’s youth are out and about.

6. Baihua Lake: The Sprawling Reservoir Escape

A massive artificial lake on the city’s outskirts, Baihua Lake is a haven for water sports, cycling, and, of course, sublime picnics with waterfront views.

Perfect Picnic Spot: A Secluded Cove Accessible by Bike or Hike

Rent a bike and explore the perimeter road until you find your own private cove. The backdrop of forested hills and clear blue water is unparalleled. Pack a substantial picnic for an all-day adventure—grilled items (if portable grills are allowed), hearty bread, and plenty of water. It’s an active, rejuvenating escape from the urban core.

7. Dongshan Temple: A Picnic with a Sacred View

Perched on Dongshan (East Mountain), this ancient temple complex offers one of the most commanding views over Guiyang’s skyline, blending spiritual serenity with urban panorama.

Perfect Picnic Spot: The Terraced Gardens Below the Main Hall

After exploring the temple, descend slightly to find terraced areas with benches and open space. A picnic here is contemplative. Opt for vegetarian-friendly snacks out of respect for the site—fresh fruit, nuts, and simple sandwiches. As you eat, watch the city sprawl below, a perfect juxtaposition of peace and buzz.

8. Guizhou Provincial Museum Grounds: Culture on the Grass

The museum itself is a treasure trove of ethnic Miao and Dong culture. Its modern architecture is surrounded by inviting lawns and thoughtful landscaping, making it a cultural picnic hotspot.

Perfect Picnic Spot: The Lawn Adjacent to the Museum’s Sculptures

Combine a morning of cultural immersion with an afternoon picnic. Discuss the fascinating exhibits on minority textiles and silverwork while enjoying your meal on the grass. It’s an intellectually stimulating picnic experience. Pack sophisticated, neat foods to match the cultured ambiance.

9. Forest Park: The True Urban Jungle

True to its name, this park is a dense, lush forest right within the city. With miles of trails, a small zoo, and incredibly fresh air, it’s a favorite for nature lovers.

Perfect Picnic Spot: By the "Giant Tree" or a Forest Clearing

Hike a short way in to find a sun-dappled clearing. The chorus of birds and the scent of pine create a deeply relaxing environment. This is the spot for a classic, rustic picnic—a checkered blanket, a woven basket, cheese, cured meats, and a baguette. It feels miles away from any metropolis.

10. Wenchang Pavilion & Surrounding *Shanzhushui* (Mountain-Water) Area

Another classical gem, Wenchang Pavilion sits within a newer park complex that beautifully embodies the Chinese philosophy of integrating architecture with shanzhushui (mountain-water landscape).

Perfect Picnic Spot: The Pagoda's Hillside Gardens

The slopes around the pavilion are landscaped with meandering paths, ponds, and rockeries. Find a spot that offers a view of the elegant pagoda. A picnic here should feel refined—perhaps some la pi (a cold, spicy noodle dish) from a nearby vendor, paired with tea. It’s a scenic, peaceful end to a day of exploration, where history and natural harmony are the main courses.

So, pack your basket with local delicacies, grab a mat, and head to these iconic spots. In Guiyang, the most memorable travel experience might just be a simple meal shared with good company, framed by a view that tells a thousand stories.
