Best Places to Watch Sunsets & Sunrises in Guiyang

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Guiyang, the capital of Guizhou Province, is a city where urban life harmonizes with dramatic natural landscapes. Nestled among rolling hills, lush forests, and serene bodies of water, it offers some of the most breathtaking vantage points for sunrise and sunset viewing. The city’s unique karst topography, combined with its often misty atmosphere, creates a magical play of light, shadow, and color that is nothing short of poetic. Whether you are an early riser seeking the first light or a dreamer chasing the golden hour, Guiyang provides an array of unforgettable experiences. Here’s your guide to the best spots in and around the city to witness the day’s most spectacular moments.

Why Guiyang’s Skies Are Unforgettable

Guiyang isn’t just another city; it’s an experience. Its high altitude and location within the Yunnan-Guizhou Plateau mean you’re often literally above the clouds. The air, fresh and often crisp, carries a certain clarity that makes the colors of the sky pop with intensity. Furthermore, the region's frequent light mist and fog don’t hinder the view—they enhance it. At sunrise, these mists often create ethereal sunbeams, known as "Buddha's rays," piercing through the valleys. At sunset, the haze softens the sunlight, resulting in prolonged, deeply saturated displays of oranges, pinks, and purples that reflect beautifully off the city’s numerous lakes and rivers.

The Magic of Qianling Mountain Park

No list of scenic viewpoints in Guiyang is complete without mentioning Qianling Mountain Park. This urban oasis is a favorite among locals for morning exercises and evening strolls, and for good reason.

For sunrise, the journey begins in the quiet, dark hours. A hike up the mountain paths, accompanied by the sounds of awakening birds, leads you to the Hongfu Temple area and the viewing platforms near the iconic Qianling Mountain Zoo. As the first rays of light break over the eastern hills, they illuminate the city below, which is often blanketed in a soft, mystical fog. The sun appears as a fiery orb, casting long shadows and painting the temple roofs in a warm, golden glow. It’s a spiritually uplifting moment, often shared with monks beginning their morning chants.

Sunset from Qianling is an equally social and serene affair. The best view is from the peak near the cable car’s upper station. As the sun descends, it sets the sky behind the urban skyline ablaze. The contrast between the fiery heavens and the serene green forests of the park is stunning. The city lights begin to twinkle on just as the sky turns a deep indigo, offering a perfect transition from day to night.

The Expansive Views from Jiaxiu Pavilion

Standing proudly on the Fuyu Bridge over the Nanming River, Jiaxiu Pavilion is the iconic symbol of Guiyang. This historic tower, lit beautifully at night, provides a completely different perspective for sunset.

While it isn’t a typical sunrise spot due to its eastern orientation, it comes alive in the evening. The best way to experience it is from a teahouse on the opposite bank or from a boat on the river itself. As the sun sets in the west, its fading light bathes the pavilion’s ancient wooden structure in a rich, amber light, making it look like it’s made of gold. The colors of the sunset reflect perfectly on the calm waters of the Nanming River, creating a dazzling mirror image. It’s a scene deeply connected to Chinese classical painting—a perfect blend of cultural heritage and natural beauty. Photography enthusiasts flock here to capture this iconic composition.

Beyond the City Center: Lakes and Highlands

To truly appreciate the scale of Guizhou’s beauty, venturing slightly outside the urban core is essential. These locations offer vast, unobstructed horizons that make for truly epic sunrise and sunset vistas.

The Serenity of Hongfeng Lake

Located about 30 kilometers west of downtown Guiyang, Hongfeng Lake (Red Maple Lake) is one of the largest artificial lakes on the Guizhou plateau. Its vast expanse of water, dotted with islands and surrounded by forested hills, is a premier destination for day trips and, most importantly, for sunset views.

The best experience is had from a boat in the middle of the lake or from one of the lakeside resorts or viewing decks. As evening approaches, the sun sinks towards the watery horizon, creating a path of shimmering gold across the lake’s surface. The silhouettes of fishing boats and the distant hills add layers of depth to the view. The sheer scale of the sky here makes the sunset feel immense and powerful. The afterglow, lingering long after the sun has disappeared, is a spectacle in itself.

The Alpine Drama at Guiyang Forest Park

Guiyang Forest Park is a massive natural retreat offering a taste of wilderness right on the city’s doorstep. For a sunrise adventure, it is unparalleled. Hiking to one of its higher points, like the Lincheng Mountain area, before dawn is a rewarding challenge.

The sunrise here is an event. As the sun peaks over the endless rolling hills of the forest, it cuts through the morning mist that settles in the valleys, creating a breathtaking sea of clouds. The light filters through the pine trees, and the only sounds are the forest coming to life. It’s a raw, natural, and profoundly peaceful experience far removed from the city’s hustle.

Hidden Gems and Local Secrets

Beyond the well-trodden paths, Guiyang holds secrets known mostly to its residents. These spots offer a more intimate connection with the day’s rhythms.

Qianlingshan Park’s Lesser-Known Trails

While the main peak of Qianling Mountain is popular, its numerous side trails lead to secluded clearings and rocks that serve as private boxes for the sky’s daily show. Locals often claim these spots for tai chi at dawn or quiet contemplation at dusk. Exploring these paths might lead you to your own personal viewpoint, away from the crowds.

The Rooftop Bars of Nanming District

For a urban-chic sunset experience, the burgeoning scene of rooftop bars in Guiyang’s downtown offers a sophisticated alternative. Sipping a craft cocktail while watching the sun set over a canopy of modern skyscrapers and historic rooftops provides a unique blend of natural beauty and metropolitan energy. The changing colors of the sky reflected in the glass of high-rises create a dynamic, modern panorama.

Capturing the Moment: Tips for the Perfect Shot

Witnessing these moments is one thing; preserving them is another. Here are some quick tips for photographers and enthusiasts:

  • Check the Weather App: Guiyang’s weather can change quickly. Use apps to check cloud cover and sunrise/sunset times precisely.
  • Arrive Early, Stay Late: For sunrise, aim to arrive at least 30 minutes before the predicted time. For sunset, stay at least 20 minutes after the sun dips below the horizon for the best colors.
  • Pack a Tripod: Especially for the low light of sunrise and the long shadows of sunset, a tripod is essential for sharp, clear photos.
  • Embrace the Mist: Don’t be discouraged by light fog; it can add incredible mood and depth to your photographs, creating those classic "misty mountain" shots.
  • Respect the Environment: Always pack out what you pack in. These beautiful places are treasures that need to be protected.

The daily cycle of sunrises and sunsets in Guiyang is more than just a celestial event; it’s a cultural touchstone and a testament to the city’s stunning natural setting. From ancient pavilions to vast lakes and misty mountain peaks, each location offers a unique lens through which to view the world’s most dependable and dazzling performance.
