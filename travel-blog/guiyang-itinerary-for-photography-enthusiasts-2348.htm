Guiyang Itinerary for Photography Enthusiasts

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Guiyang, the capital of Guizhou Province, is a hidden gem for photographers. Nestled among lush mountains and dotted with ethnic minority villages, this city offers a unique blend of urban energy and natural beauty. Whether you’re into street photography, landscapes, or cultural portraits, Guiyang has something to inspire your lens. Here’s a detailed itinerary to help you capture the best of this captivating destination.

Day 1: Exploring the Urban Pulse

Morning: Qianling Mountain Park

Start your day at Qianling Mountain Park, a green oasis in the heart of the city. The park is perfect for capturing misty mornings, especially around Hongfu Temple, where golden rooftops peek through the trees. Don’t miss the wild macaques—they make for playful and dynamic subjects.

Pro Tip: Arrive at sunrise for soft, diffused light filtering through the forest.

Afternoon: Jiaxiu Pavilion & Nanming River

Head to Jiaxiu Pavilion, an iconic Ming Dynasty landmark. The pavilion’s reflection on the Nanming River creates a classic Chinese postcard shot. Wander along the riverbanks for candid street photography—locals fishing, playing chess, or practicing tai chi offer authentic moments.

Gear Suggestion: A polarizing filter will help reduce glare on the water.

Evening: Qingyan Ancient Town

Just 30 minutes from downtown, Qingyan Ancient Town is a photographer’s dream. Cobblestone streets, Qing-era architecture, and red lanterns provide endless framing opportunities. As dusk falls, the town glows with warm lights—ideal for long exposures.

Must-Shot: The Zhenshan Cultural Wall, covered in ivy and history.

Day 2: Ethnic Culture & Rural Landscapes

Morning: Huaxi Park

At Huaxi Park, the blend of karst formations and traditional Miao-style bridges is surreal. Early morning fog often lingers over the lake, creating an ethereal mood. If you’re lucky, you might encounter locals in ethnic dress—politely ask for a portrait.

Composition Tip: Use the curved bridges as leading lines.

Afternoon: Gaopo Miao Village

Venture into the countryside to Gaopo Miao Village, where terraced fields and wooden stilt houses dominate the landscape. The Miao people’s intricate silver jewelry and embroidered costumes are a visual feast. Time your visit with a local festival for vibrant, action-packed shots.

Cultural Note: Always ask permission before photographing villagers.

Sunset: Huajiang Grand Canyon

For dramatic landscapes, drive to Huajiang Grand Canyon. The golden hour lights up the cliffs in fiery hues. A telephoto lens will help compress the layers of terraced fields cascading down the slopes.

Safety First: Watch your step near the canyon edges.

Day 3: Hidden Gems & Night Scenes

Morning: Tianhetan Scenic Area

Tianhetan is a lesser-known spot with waterfalls and crystal-clear pools. The interplay of light and water here is magical—try slow shutter speeds to blur the cascades.

Packing List: A sturdy tripod for silky water effects.

Afternoon: Guiyang’s Street Food Markets

Switch gears to street photography at Erqi Road Snack Street. Steam rising from baskets of siwawa (local spring rolls) and the vibrant chaos of vendors are perfect for storytelling shots.

Camera Settings: Fast shutter speed to freeze motion.

Night: Guomao Shopping District

End your trip with Guiyang’s neon-lit skyline. The Guomao Tower and Jiaxiu Pavilion illuminated at night are stunning. For a unique angle, shoot from a rooftop bar (like Sky Lounge) to capture the city lights sprawling below.

Night Photography Hack: Use a low ISO to avoid noise.

Bonus: Seasonal Highlights

  • Spring (March–May): Cherry blossoms at Qianling Park.
  • Summer (June–August): Waterfalls at their fullest.
  • Autumn (September–November): Golden rice terraces in surrounding villages.
  • Winter (December–February): Frost-kissed landscapes in Longgang Karst Forest.

Guiyang is a canvas waiting for your creativity. Pack your gear, respect the culture, and let the city’s spirit guide your lens. Happy shooting!
