Guiyang Night Markets: Shopping and Snacking After Dark

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

When the sun sets over Guiyang, the capital of Guizhou province, the city transforms into a vibrant playground for food lovers and shoppers alike. The night markets here are more than just places to grab a bite—they’re cultural hubs where locals and tourists mingle, flavors collide, and the energy is electric. If you’re looking for an unforgettable after-dark adventure, Guiyang’s night markets should be at the top of your list.

The Allure of Guiyang’s Night Markets

Guiyang’s night markets are legendary, offering a sensory overload of sizzling street food, colorful handicrafts, and the lively chatter of vendors and patrons. Unlike the polished shopping malls or high-end restaurants, these markets thrive on authenticity. Here, you’ll find everything from spicy local delicacies to quirky souvenirs, all under the glow of string lights and neon signs.

Hongfu Temple Night Market

One of the most famous spots is the Hongfu Temple Night Market, located near the historic Hongfu Temple. This market is a paradise for foodies, with stalls serving up Guizhou’s signature dishes like Siwawa (a fresh spring roll stuffed with crunchy vegetables and chili sauce) and Changwang Noodles, a hearty bowl of noodles topped with braised pork and pickled vegetables.

But it’s not just about the food—this market also features vendors selling handmade jewelry, traditional Miao embroidery, and even vintage trinkets. The mix of flavors and crafts makes it a must-visit for anyone exploring Guiyang after dark.

Qingyan Ancient Town Night Market

For a more historical vibe, head to Qingyan Ancient Town, where the night market feels like stepping back in time. The cobblestone streets are lined with lanterns, and the aroma of stinky tofu (a love-it-or-hate-it delicacy) fills the air. This market is perfect for those who want to combine shopping with a bit of sightseeing, as the ancient architecture adds to the charm.

Must-Try Street Foods

No visit to Guiyang’s night markets is complete without indulging in the local street food. Here are some dishes you absolutely can’t miss:

Spicy and Sour Flavors

Guiyang’s cuisine is known for its bold, spicy, and sour flavors. One standout is Sour Fish Soup (酸汤鱼), a tangy and slightly spicy broth filled with tender fish slices. Another favorite is Zhusun Chao Rou (Bamboo Shoots with Pork), a stir-fry dish that showcases the region’s love for fresh, crunchy bamboo shoots.

Snacks on the Go

If you’re in the mood for something quick, try La Rou Bing (Spicy Meat Pancake), a crispy flatbread stuffed with minced pork and chili oil. For dessert, Ciba (Glutinous Rice Cake) is a sweet and chewy treat often served with honey or crushed peanuts.

Shopping for Unique Souvenirs

Beyond the food, Guiyang’s night markets are fantastic places to pick up unique souvenirs. Here’s what to look for:

Handicrafts and Textiles

The Miao and Dong ethnic groups are famous for their intricate embroidery and silver jewelry. At the markets, you’ll find beautifully crafted hand-stitched bags, indigo-dyed fabrics, and silver headpieces that make for stunning keepsakes.

Local Specialties

Don’t leave without grabbing some Guizhou chili paste or Maotai liquor (if you’re feeling adventurous). These make great gifts for friends back home—just be sure to check customs regulations if you’re traveling internationally.

Tips for Navigating the Night Markets

To make the most of your night market adventure, keep these tips in mind:

  • Go early or late: The markets get crowded around 8-9 PM, so arrive early to beat the rush or late if you prefer a more relaxed vibe.
  • Bring cash: While some vendors accept mobile payments, cash is still king in many stalls.
  • Dress comfortably: You’ll be doing a lot of walking (and eating), so wear comfortable shoes and clothes you don’t mind getting a little messy.
  • Be adventurous: Don’t shy away from unfamiliar dishes—some of the best flavors come from trying something new.

Guiyang’s night markets are a feast for the senses, offering a perfect blend of food, culture, and shopping. Whether you’re a seasoned traveler or a first-time visitor, these markets promise an unforgettable experience under the stars.
