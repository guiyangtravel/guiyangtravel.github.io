Guiyang’s Best Cafés for a Midday Pick-Me-Up

  1. Home
  2. Travel Blog
  3. Blog Details

Guiyang, the capital of Guizhou Province, is a city where modernity meets tradition. Known for its lush landscapes, vibrant minority cultures, and mouthwatering street food, it’s also home to a thriving café scene. Whether you’re a digital nomad, a traveler in need of a caffeine boost, or simply someone who appreciates a cozy ambiance, Guiyang’s cafés offer the perfect midday escape. Here’s a curated list of the best spots to recharge.

The Specialty Coffee Gems

1. N1 Coffee Lab

Tucked away in a quiet alley, N1 Coffee Lab is a haven for coffee purists. The baristas here are passionate about their craft, serving single-origin beans and expertly brewed pour-overs. The minimalist décor, with its clean lines and warm wood accents, creates a serene atmosphere perfect for focusing or unwinding. Don’t miss their signature Yirgacheffe—bright, floral, and utterly refreshing.

2. Seesaw Coffee (Qianxi Flagship Store)

A well-known chain with a Guiyang twist, Seesaw’s Qianxi location blends sleek design with local influences. The café sources beans from Yunnan, offering a unique Chinese coffee experience. Their cold brew, infused with subtle fruity notes, is a must-try on a warm afternoon. The spacious seating and natural light make it ideal for remote work or casual meetups.

Cafés with a View

3. Sky Lounge at Kempinski Hotel

For those who love a side of scenery with their espresso, the Sky Lounge offers panoramic views of Guiyang’s skyline. Perched on one of the higher floors of the Kempinski Hotel, this spot is all about luxury. Sip on a velvety flat white while watching the city buzz below. The elegant setting makes it a great place for a midday treat after exploring nearby attractions like Jiaxiu Tower.

4. Mountain View Café

Nestled near Qianling Mountain Park, this café lives up to its name. Floor-to-ceiling windows frame the lush greenery outside, creating a tranquil retreat. Their honey latte, made with local Guizhou honey, is a sweet and aromatic delight. Pair it with a slice of their matcha cake for the ultimate pick-me-up.

Hidden Gems & Local Favorites

5. Dui Yue Café

A charming spot in the historic Qingyan Ancient Town, Dui Yue Café blends traditional Chinese aesthetics with modern coffee culture. The courtyard setting, complete with wooden beams and hanging lanterns, transports you back in time. Their rose latte, infused with edible rose petals, is a fragrant and Instagram-worthy choice.

6. Miao’s Brew

This café celebrates Guizhou’s Miao culture with its décor and menu. Tribal textiles adorn the walls, and the baristas occasionally wear traditional Miao embroidery. Try their "Sour Soup Latte," a creative twist inspired by the region’s famous sour soup dishes. It’s tangy, spicy, and surprisingly addictive.

Cafés for the Digital Nomad

7. The Workbench

Designed for productivity, The Workbench offers fast Wi-Fi, ample outlets, and ergonomic seating. The industrial-chic space is quiet during weekdays, making it a favorite among freelancers. Their nitro cold brew is smooth and energizing—perfect for powering through emails.

8. Bean & Code

A hybrid café and co-working space, Bean & Code caters to tech-savvy visitors. The menu includes coffee named after programming languages (try the "Java Java"). The relaxed vibe and communal tables encourage networking, and the avocado toast is a healthy midday snack option.

Sweet Treats & Unique Brews

9. Dessert First Café

As the name suggests, this place is all about indulgence. Their affogato, made with local dairy ice cream and a shot of espresso, is pure bliss. The pastel-colored interior and Insta-friendly desserts make it a hit with younger crowds.

10. Bamboo Press

Inspired by Guizhou’s bamboo forests, this café serves coffee in bamboo cups. Their signature drink, the "Bamboo Mocha," incorporates bamboo charcoal for a smoky depth. The eco-friendly ethos extends to their straws and packaging, appealing to environmentally conscious travelers.

Final Sips

Guiyang’s café scene is as diverse as the city itself. Whether you’re after artisanal coffee, breathtaking views, or a taste of local culture, there’s a spot to suit every mood. So next time you’re exploring Guiyang’s bustling streets or scenic parks, take a break at one of these cafés—your taste buds (and Instagram feed) will thank you.
